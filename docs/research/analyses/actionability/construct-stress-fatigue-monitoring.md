## 5.4 What the subject may NOT do with this signal

Mandatory minimum NOT-DO refusal categories at tier-1 per `actionability_translation.md` §5.4:

1. **Predictive-use overclaim**: The subject may NOT use daily `all_day_stress_avg` to predict tomorrow's gevoelscore (or any future-day gevoelscore). The tier-1 descriptive correspondence is **same-day window observation**, not forecast. The map's predictive-claim-feasibility cell + §3.10 hard predictive gate explicitly block predictive use; no forward-validation HA exists for this construct. **"Today's stress predicts tomorrow's gevoelscore" is structurally a tier-3 claim that does not exist at this Stage A time.**

2. **Causal overclaim**: The subject may NOT infer that daily Garmin stress **causes** daily gevoelscore from this monitoring claim. Correspondence in the predicted direction is not mechanism per guide #2 §7.2; the construct is a descriptive shape-detection claim, not a causal-mechanism claim. The five-dimension candidate explanations (population, measurement, era, individual variation, methodological per topic §4.8) are explicitly the non-causal alternative readings the construct does not refute.

3. **Group-level overclaim**: The subject may NOT generalise the monitoring claim to "people like me" or "LC patients" or any group-level frame **without group-level confirmation** per L1 single-subject reach. The Stage S₂ topic-level DIVERGES + EXTENDS calls are within-subject data points against / extending the Wiggers consensus, not group-level findings. §5.8 below names the group-level study that would address this.

4. **Advice overclaim**: The subject may NOT use daily Garmin stress as a **prescriptive trigger** ("rest when stress crosses 40", "push more on low-stress days") — the signal licenses observation, not action. Stage A does NOT produce advice per `actionability_translation.md` §7.2 anti-pattern; advice would require intervention-study methodology with comparator arm and blinding, which the research line cannot run on itself per L4 (per §5.8 external-research entry: intervention RCT testing the inverted-U-direction operationalisation is L1+L4-scoped).

### Construct-specific overclaims (drawn from upstream chain)

5. **Inverted-U-as-mechanism overclaim**: The subject may NOT infer that the inverted-U / threshold-pattern shape implies a specific mechanism (sympathetic-arousal-saturation, conserving-everything-days confound, circadian-clustering, push-crash threshold). The shape is the operationalised pattern per [CONVENTIONS §4.1-§4.3](../../CONVENTIONS.md#4-stay-close-to-the-data--defer-interpretation); the mechanism that explains the shape is a separate question requiring mechanistic study. Per topic §4.8 candidate-explanations enumeration: five candidate mechanisms are listed at the topic level WITHOUT Stage S₂ picking which is correct; the construct does not pre-empt that pick.

6. **Direction-vs-Wiggers overclaim**: The subject may NOT infer that "the Wiggers convex-cost claim is wrong" from the topic-level DIVERGES finding. Per topic §4.7 L1 citation + §4.6 N=1-reach caveat: DIVERGES adds a within-subject divergence data point, not a refutation of the Wiggers consensus at group level. The cluster-level cross-operationalisation convergence on inverse direction strengthens the within-subject claim but does NOT extend it to a group-level claim about the Wiggers handleiding's pacing guidance for other LC patients.
