## 1. Purpose + LOAD-BEARING attribution

### 1.1 Two-step study framing

**Step 1** is a **descriptive companion** to HA-C4c's stringent 3-hour
`did_not_return_flag` operand: characterise the bout-return-time distribution
on the same LOCKED HA-C4c primary stratum, at four return-window thresholds
{30 min, 60 min, 120 min, 180 min}. Step 1 has two hard-separated passes:

- **Step 1 Pass 1 — distributional-only** (per §2). No effect view. No
  heavy-T-vs-non-heavy-T contrast. No crash / gevoelscore cross-look. Report
  per-day count distributions + base rates + zero-inflation per threshold.
  This pass is the sole input to the Step 2 decision under §4.
- **Step 1 Pass 2 — effect view across thresholds** (per §3). Only runs
  AFTER Pass 1 is written + locked. Reports Cliff's δ + bootstrap CI + block-
  permutation null per threshold, mirroring HA-C4c's primary contrast
  ([`hypotheses/HA-C4c/hypothesis.md`](../HA-C4c/hypothesis.md) §4.6). This
  pass is **descriptive substrate**; it does NOT license any claim upgrade
  and does NOT drive the Step 2 pre-registration decision.

**Step 2** is a **conditional confirmatory pre-registration** taken ONLY on the
distributional shape from Step 1 Pass 1, decided against a pre-committed
asymmetric-bar rule locked HERE (§4). Three candidate paths are pre-specified
without pre-registration:

- **Step 2a** — HA-C4c-v2 at a descriptively-motivated less-stringent absolute
  threshold (parent MD §3.2 revision cascade).
- **Step 2b** — HA-C4c-personal-baseline sister-pre-reg, parallel to the
  HA-C3 / HA-C3p precedent, on a personal-baseline SD-anchored operand per
  [CONVENTIONS §3.1](../../../CONVENTIONS.md#31-personal-baseline-not-absolute-thresholds).
  **This is the load-bearing path** for gap closure (§1.2 below).
- **Step 2c** — 2a and 2b in parallel.

Full Step 2 specifications appear in §5. The Step 2 pre-registration is a
NEW confirmatory pre-reg — not a re-analysis of HA-C4c r2 and not a re-write
of HA-C4c's licensed claim (§3 of HA-C4c Stage I stands as-locked).

## 2. Step 1 Pass 1 spec (distributional-only, S effort)

### 2.1 Scope + stratum

Same LOCKED HA-C4c primary stratum: cross-phase-pooled LC-era days with
`citalopram_phase ∈ {unmedicated, buildup, consolidation, afbouw,
post_afbouw}`; days `>= 2022-11-17` (recovery_phase 4b + 5); April 2024
cluster (2024-04-09 → 2024-04-16) excluded; 21-day device-baseline-lag
excluded per parent MD §3.4; `bout_n_did_not_return_day` computable + `exertion_class_lagged_lcera` computable per HA-C4c pre-reg §4.4 day-validity
gate. The stratum is verbatim from
[`hypotheses/HA-C4c/hypothesis.md`](../HA-C4c/hypothesis.md) §4.2 + §4.4.

### 2.2 Thresholds

Four return-window thresholds evaluated in parallel: **30 min, 60 min,
120 min, 180 min** (180-min is the HA-C4c-locked reference; the other three
are less-stringent variants per user Q B2 follow-up-1). For each threshold
`T ∈ {30, 60, 120, 180}` a per-day count operand
`bout_n_did_not_return_within_T_day` is defined as: per-day count of bouts
whose stress did not return to within +5 of `pre_bout_baseline` for ≥10
consecutive minutes within `T` minutes after peak (parent MD §3.2 form,
`T`-parameterised).

Note that `bout_n_did_not_return_within_180_day` is by construction identical
to HA-C4c's locked primary operand `bout_n_did_not_return_day`; it is
reported alongside the three less-stringent variants for continuity and
for the sanity check that Pass 1 reproduces HA-C4c's per-day base rate
(mean 0.6444, median 1.00, p25-p75 [0.00, 1.00]) at the 180-min threshold.

### 2.3 Analysis (per threshold, distributional only)

For each `T`:

1. **Per-day count distribution** on the stratum: mean, median, p25, p75,
   min, max, standard deviation.
2. **Base rate** at three cutoffs: fraction of days with ≥1 event,
   fraction of days with ≥2 events, fraction of days with ≥3 events.
3. **Zero-inflation** metric: fraction of days with 0 events; and a
   comparison of the observed zero-fraction against a Poisson expectation
   at the observed mean (`P(X=0 | Poisson(mean)) = exp(-mean)`), reported
   as a ratio (observed zero-fraction / Poisson-expected zero-fraction).
4. **Per-threshold missingness**: fraction of stratum days for which
   `bout_n_did_not_return_within_T_day` is NaN (upstream day-validity failure
   or bout-extraction gap). Should be identical across `T` — reported per
   threshold as a sanity check.
5. **Continuous underlying bout-return-time distribution**: per-bout
   `bout_return_time_min` (continuous minutes) reported as a
   histogram / quantile summary over the stratum's total bout population
   (all bouts, not just did-not-return bouts, so the tail structure is
   visible). Anchors the four threshold cuts against the underlying
   distribution.

### 2.4 EXPLICIT restrictions on Pass 1

Pass 1 hard walls, load-bearing for §6 p-hacking prevention:

- **NO effect view**: no heavy-T-vs-non-heavy-T contrast reported at this
  pass. No Cliff's δ, no Mann-Whitney U, no block-permutation null. Effect
  view is Pass 2 (§3) and cannot be looked at during the Step-2 decision.
- **NO crash / gevoelscore cross-look**: no correlation, no CCF, no
  conditional distribution against `is_crash` or `gevoelscore` at any
  threshold at Pass 1.
- **NO stratification by `exertion_class_lagged_lcera`**: even without
  computing the contrast, splitting the histograms by heavy-T status
  reveals the contrast pattern by inspection. Pass 1 distributions are
  pooled across all stratum days.
- **NO threshold recommendation from Pass 1**: Pass 1 output does NOT
  recommend a "best" or "descriptively-motivated" threshold. The Step 2
  decision under §4 reads Pass 1 output against pre-committed criteria;
  no ad-hoc recommendation surface exists.

## 4. Step 2 pre-committed decision rule

### 4.1 Rule scope

The Step 2 trigger decision is taken on the Pass 1 distributional output
alone, evaluated against the criteria below. The rule text is
**pre-committed at this protocol's lock event** and is not revised at Pass 1
read time. If the rule as stated here proves ambiguous at Pass 1 read, the
default is **do NOT trigger** (per §4.4 asymmetric bar); the reader may
propose a rule revision but any revision requires this protocol to re-lock
r2 BEFORE any Pass 1 re-read against the revised rule (the revision
discipline itself is p-hacking-preventive; changing the rule after seeing
the data invalidates the pre-registration).

### 4.2 Rule inputs (from Pass 1 per §2)

Per threshold `T ∈ {30, 60, 120, 180}` Pass 1 reports the fraction of days
with ≥2 events (per §2.3 item 2). Denote this fraction as
`f2(T) = fraction of stratum days with bout_n_did_not_return_within_T_day ≥ 2`.

`f2(T)` is the primary rule input. Additional context inputs from §2.3
(mean, median, zero-fraction, Poisson-zero ratio) inform the reader's
sanity check on Pass 1 but do NOT enter the trigger arithmetic.

### 4.3 Trigger criteria

**Trigger** — Step 2 pre-registration proceeds (path selection at §5.2)
IF AND ONLY IF for all four thresholds `T ∈ {30, 60, 120, 180}`:

- `f2(T) < 0.10` (fewer than 10% of stratum days carry ≥2 events at
  threshold `T`).

Rationale: tail-rarity preserved at all thresholds means the operand family
is uniformly capturing an already-rare stringent event, matching HA-C4c
Stage I §3's "stringent-threshold-captured small elevation on the extreme
tail of within-day recovery-failure" reading. Under this shape the two-step
study's stringent-operand-threshold interpretation carries through
regardless of which threshold within {30, 60, 120, 180} is selected, and
the personal-baseline sister candidate (§5.3) becomes a natural
operationalisation choice per user's Q B2 follow-up-2.

### 4.4 Non-trigger criteria

**Non-trigger** — Step 2 pre-registration does NOT proceed; Pass 1 output
is retained as a scoped descriptive artefact and the LOAD-BEARING §1.2
attribution is NOT operationalised via this OI. Fired IF for any threshold
`T ∈ {30, 60, 120, 180}`:

- `f2(T) > 0.30` (more than 30% of stratum days carry ≥2 events at
  threshold `T`).

Rationale: at f2(T) > 0.30 the ≥2-event pattern is no longer an already-rare
extreme-tail event at that threshold; the stringent-operand-threshold
reading collapses; the operand at that threshold captures everyday
elevation rather than tail events. Under this shape the substantive
Wiggers-C4 stringent-tail interpretation would not carry across the
threshold family, and the descriptive companion has closed the question
without triggering any new pre-registration. Downstream: OI-025 is closed
as descriptive-only-completed; §1.2 LOAD-BEARING gap-closure is NOT
achieved via this OI and the Stage A §5.6 row-4 pathway is exhausted; a
separate OI would need to be surfaced by user to keep the guide r3
§4.2.1 condition 2 future-closeability claim honest.

### 4.5 Ambiguous criteria + asymmetric-bar default

**Ambiguous** — DEFAULT is **do NOT trigger**. Fired IF for any threshold
`T ∈ {30, 60, 120, 180}`:

- `0.10 ≤ f2(T) ≤ 0.30` (fraction is in the borderline band).

AND no threshold has f2(T) > 0.30 (which would trigger the strict
Non-trigger rule of §4.4).

Under Ambiguous, the default action is **do NOT trigger** — the
asymmetric bar per §6 defaults to non-triggering on borderline patterns.
The Ambiguous outcome does NOT close OI-025 (unlike Non-trigger under
§4.4): the descriptive companion has produced an inconclusive read at the
protocol's committed thresholds, and the user may either (a) let OI-025
remain OPEN with the Ambiguous read attached (deferring the closure
pathway indefinitely), or (b) draft a separate independent OI proposing
a revised threshold family that this protocol's Step 2 rule does not
carry (which would require its own separate descriptive companion + rule).
Both options preserve p-hacking discipline; neither triggers Step 2 on
Ambiguous Pass 1.

### 4.6 Rule output recording

Pass 1's `descriptive_audit.md` §Step-2-decision block records the
outcome as one of three tokens:

- `TRIGGER — {2a | 2b | 2c}` (per user's path selection at §5.2 among the
  three candidates).
- `NON-TRIGGER (f2(T) > 0.30 at T ∈ {matched thresholds})`.
- `AMBIGUOUS (f2(T) ∈ [0.10, 0.30] at T ∈ {matched thresholds}) — DEFAULT NO TRIGGER`.

The recording block cites the specific numeric values of f2(T) for all four
thresholds so the read is reproducible and auditable per CONVENTIONS §4.1
descriptive-before-inference.

## 5. Step 2 sister-hypothesis outline (conditional)

### 5.1 Step 2 IS a NEW confirmatory pre-registration

Per [CONVENTIONS §4.3](../../../CONVENTIONS.md#43-prior-driven-hypotheses-are-confirmatory)
the Step 2 pre-reg (whichever path) is **confirmatory** — the prior comes
from HA-C4c's licensed §3 claim (direction-real + statistically-real +

day" — HA-C4c Stage I §4.6). The Step 2 pre-reg is NOT a re-analysis of
HA-C4c r2 and does NOT modify HA-C4c's licensed claim (§3 of Stage I stands
as-locked; §7 of HA-C4c pre-reg r2 remains as-locked). It is a new
substantive test on a new operand-configuration (2a) or a new operand-
class (2b).

### 5.2 Path selection at trigger time

At §4 Trigger fire, the user selects among:

- **2a** — HA-C4c-v2 at a descriptively-motivated less-stringent absolute
  threshold. Path selection reason: user reads Pass 1 as showing a natural
  descriptive inflection at a specific threshold, OR reads 180-min as
  arbitrary against a smooth continuous return-time distribution.
- **2b** — HA-C4c-personal-baseline sister-pre-reg. Path selection reason:
  user reads Pass 1 as showing tail-rarity uniformly preserved (per §4.3
  Trigger criteria); the personal-baseline framing directly addresses
  CONVENTIONS §3.1 discipline + user Q B2 lived-experience prior.
  **§1.2 LOAD-BEARING closure via 2b is the preferred path**.
- **2c** — both 2a AND 2b, parallel-dispatched. Path selection reason:
  Pass 1 shows a natural refined threshold AND personal-baseline framing
  is separately motivated. Heaviest lock cost (two parent MD revisions +
  two pre-regs + full 4-stage arcs on each).

The selection is documented in the same block that records the Pass 1
outcome under §4.6.

### 5.3 Step 2b operand — personal-baseline SD-anchored bout-return threshold

The preferred load-bearing path per §1.2. Operand candidates (all draw on
CONVENTIONS §3.1 personal-baseline + §3.2 lagged-baseline `_lagged_lcera`
discipline):

- **`bout_return_time_z = (bout_return_time_min - subject_lagged_mean) /
  subject_lagged_sd`** per-bout continuous. `subject_lagged_mean` and
  `subject_lagged_sd` are computed on the `[d-90, d-30]` lagged window
  restricted to LC-era days per CONVENTIONS §3.2 v3.2 lagged-baseline
  discipline; the baseline **excludes the candidate day itself** to
  prevent v3.1 inflation (per CONVENTIONS §3.2 lesson from HA01b
  recomputed).
- **`did_not_return_1sd_flag`** per-bout binary: `True` iff
  `bout_return_time_min > (subject_lagged_mean + 1 × subject_lagged_sd)`.
  Rolled up to `bout_n_did_not_return_1sd_day`.
- **`did_not_return_2sd_flag`** per-bout binary: `True` iff
  `bout_return_time_min > (subject_lagged_mean + 2 × subject_lagged_sd)`.
  Rolled up to `bout_n_did_not_return_2sd_day`.
- **`bout_return_time_z_max_day`** per-day continuous: highest z-scored
  return-time bout that day.

Exact Z-threshold selection (Z=1 or Z=2 or both, and which of the four
candidate per-day operands is primary vs sensitivity) is a Step 2b pre-reg
decision, made at pre-reg drafting time BEFORE peeking at any Pass 2 δ
curve (§6 wall) OR at any per-Z discrimination values on the personal-
baseline operand. The pre-reg drafting session locks:

- Primary per-day operand (one of the four candidates above).
- Primary Z-threshold (Z=1 or Z=2).
- Any sensitivity Z-threshold(s).
- Stratum (default: same cross-phase-pooled LC-era stratum as HA-C4c).
- Direction (pre-committed heavy-T > non-heavy-T; mirror HA-C4c).

### 5.4 Cross-operationalisation independence argument for Step 2b

Load-bearing for the §1.2 gap-closure obligation.

The HA-C4c primary operand `bout_n_did_not_return_day` is an
**absolute-threshold** operand: bouts flagged by a fixed 180-min return-
window cap + fixed +5 baseline tolerance + fixed ≥10-min return-hold, all
in raw stress units on the participant's per-minute Firstbeat trace,
without reference to the participant's within-corpus distribution.

The Step 2b operand family (§5.3) is a **personal-baseline SD-anchored**
operand: bouts flagged by the return-time being ≥ Z standard deviations
above the participant's own rolling `[d-90, d-30]` lagged-LC-era mean
return-time. The reference frame is the participant's own return-time
distribution, not an absolute stress-unit threshold.

The two operationalisations are **cross-operationalisation-independent**
in the sense §4.2 of the guide r3 requires ("different bin schemes,
different windows, different era strata, different cohort cells — not the
same signal tested three ways"). Specifically:

1. **Different reference frame** — fixed-absolute vs rolling-personal-
   baseline. HA-C4c bouts are flagged against a corpus-invariant
   threshold; Step 2b bouts are flagged against a subject-specific,
   time-varying reference distribution. The two flag-populations partly
   overlap but neither subsumes the other in general.
2. **Different construct alignment** — HA-C4c operationalises Wiggers'
   verbatim "stress doesn't decrease for a long time" (a fixed-duration
   claim about the stress trace); Step 2b operationalises "return time is
   atypical for me" (a subject-relative claim about the distribution of
   return times). Both are consistent with the same underlying autonomic-
   dysregulation prior but they measure two different things about the
   participant's within-day trace.
3. **Different sensitivity to distributional drift** — HA-C4c's flag rate
   would shift under a participant-wide baseline stress shift (e.g. a
   more-vs-less-stable era); Step 2b's flag rate is invariant to such
   shifts by construction (the lagged baseline moves with the era).
4. **Different failure modes at low n** — HA-C4c fails at high-motion
   confound rates (the 99.3% motion-confound corpus property; HA-C4c
   pre-reg §8 caveat 4); Step 2b fails at high per-day variance in the
   `[d-90, d-30]` reference window (small-window baseline noise).

Independence is NOT full independence at the raw-per-minute-stress-trace
substrate (both derive from the same Firstbeat-per-minute stress signal
via the same bout-detection pipeline). This shared-substrate dependence
is a caveat carried into Step 2b's pre-reg §8 caveats and into the
downstream cluster-C-bout-substance Stage S₁ re-consumption on drift
trigger (§5.6 below). The independence claim is at the operand-family
level, not the raw-substrate level; this is the same independence bar the
HA-C3 v2 / HA-C3p sister-pre-reg pattern used (both consume `all_day_stress_avg`
but operationalise it with different bin schemes / different reference
frames), which the guide r3 §4.2.1 condition 2 explicitly cites as the
canonical model.

## 6. P-hacking prevention discipline

The Step-1-Pass-1 → Pass 2 → Step 2 sequence has three built-in walls
that prevent p-hacking. Each is stated here so a future reader of the
protocol can audit them.

### 6.1 Pass 1 → Pass 2 wall

Pass 2 (effect view) does NOT run until Pass 1 (distributional view) is
written + user-accepted. This prevents the reader from seeing the δ curve
before the descriptive read is fixed. Pass 2 is sealed at Step 2 decision
time (§4.6 rule outcome recorded) and cannot enter the Step 2 trigger
criteria.

### 6.2 Pass 1 → Step 2 decision wall

The Step 2 trigger criteria (§4.3, §4.4, §4.5) are locked at this
protocol's lock event, with **no revision after Pass 1 lands**. The
protocol may be re-locked r2 to revise the criteria BEFORE any Pass 1
re-read against the revised criteria; but any revision after a first
Pass 1 read against the r1 rule invalidates the r1 pre-registration
and requires a new r2 pre-registration + fresh Pass 1 execution. This
prevents rule-tuning after peeking.

### 6.3 Pre-committed criteria + asymmetric bar

The §4 rule is pre-committed with an asymmetric-bar default:

- **Strict Trigger** at `f2(T) < 0.10` for all T (10% threshold, absolute).
- **Strict Non-trigger** at `f2(T) > 0.30` for any T (30% threshold,
  absolute).
- **Ambiguous** in the 10-30% band → **DEFAULT NO TRIGGER**.

The asymmetric-bar shape (strict-trigger tight; strict-non-trigger loose;
ambiguous defaults to non-trigger) is deliberately biased toward NOT
producing a new pre-registration. The bias reduces the probability that a
borderline distributional pattern licenses a new confirmatory pre-reg
that the researcher would then later find "just happens" to be well-
positioned to succeed. If the user's substantive reason to draft a Step 2
pre-reg is that the distribution actually looks like the Trigger criteria,
the pre-reg proceeds; if the distribution is borderline, the pre-reg does
not proceed and the OI's LOAD-BEARING closure obligation remains unresolved
via this pathway (per §4.5 handling).
