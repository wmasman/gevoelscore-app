The **within-single-year** Theil-Sen slopes collapse to 0.00 for

(-10.29 bpm/yr) is a short, dense fitness-deepening segment and is not part
of the LC-window trend.

### A3. Plateau check (the research question: is RHR flattening now, ~4 years in?)

Because RHR is integer-tied, the plateau discriminator is reported two ways:
raw-daily Theil-Sen (tie-degenerate within short windows) and the 28-day
rolling-median trajectory slope (tie-robust). Both, plus the window sweep,
are shown so window-dependence is visible (reviewer finding 5).

| window | n | raw Theil-Sen slope (bpm/yr) | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|---|
| early-LC 2022-2023 | 639 | **1.31** | [0.81, 1.77] |
| recent 2025-2026 | 510 | **1.61** | [0.98, 2.12] |
| mid 2023-2024 | 730 | 0.00 | [-0.85, 0.00] |
| 2024-2026 | 875 | 2.30 | [2.11, 2.52] |

| window | n | 28d rolling-median slope (bpm/yr) | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|---|
| early-LC 2022-2023 | 651 | 1.39 | [1.06, 1.87] |
| recent 2025-2026 | 519 | 1.67 | [1.14, 2.20] |
| mid 2023-2024 | 731 | -0.59 | [-0.88, 0.00] |

**Plain answer: the recent rise is NOT below the early-LC rise, so the
trajectory is NOT front-loaded, but the estimator is window-sensitive and
this conclusion rests on a two-window comparison.** The recent (2025-2026)
raw slope (1.61 bpm/yr) is not below the early-LC
slope (1.31 bpm/yr); the rolling-median
trajectory agrees (recent 1.67 not below early
1.39). The direction (not front-loaded) is what
pure year-1-plateau deconditioning would NOT produce.

**Window-sensitivity, disclosed:** the raw estimator swings hard with window
choice. The **mid 2023-2024 window is essentially flat** (raw
0.00 [-0.85, 0.00], rolling-median -0.59):
a flat window sits inside the record. The 2024-2026 window is the steepest
(2.30). So the non-plateau conclusion is a statement
about the two multi-year end-windows, and single-year / some multi-month
windows are degenerate or flat. The direction survives the honestly-chosen
windows, but this rests on multi-year windows and inherits the year-wobble
seen in Part B. (Descriptive statement of shape only; attribution is Part B.)

## PART B -- Confounder decomposition (exploratory, ledger section 4)

Model fitted (Huber soft-L1 robust loss; bounds per ledger priors):

```
RHR(t) = intercept
       + decond_amp * decond_shape(t)        # unit curve, amp bounded 0..12 bpm
       + b_weight * (weight_interp(t) - 74)   # centred at athlete baseline
       + b_dose * dose_plasma_mg(t)           # citalopram, dose-proportional
       + b_sin*sin + b_cos*cos                # annual seasonal phase
       + b_age * age_years                    # aging, bounded 0..0.5 bpm/yr
       + LC_residual(t) + noise
```

Sensitivity pair on the deconditioning curve: **(i)** literature exponential
`1 - exp(-t/tau)` anchored at cessation 2022-03, tau in {3, 4, 6} months
(plateau by ~year 1); **(ii)** Garmin VO2Max-decay shape (secondary; per-kg,
partially re-encodes weight). B7 additionally tests slow / never-plateauing
deconditioning shapes (plateau at year 2/3/4 and a linear-forever ramp).

### B1. Fitted coefficients (point + block-bootstrap 95% CI)

Coefficients are stable across all four curves (they differ only in the
decond column, which is non-identifiable -- see B2). Representative fit
(literature, tau = 4 months):

| term | point | 95% CI | lit prior |
|---|---|---|---|
| intercept | 52.68 | [51.7, 53.7] | -- |
| **decond_amp** | **0.00** | [0.00, 0.00] | 5-12 bpm |
| **b_weight** | **0.322 bpm/kg** | [0.19, 0.56] | ~0.3 bpm/BMI-unit |
| **b_dose (citalopram)** | **-0.038 bpm/mg** | [-0.065, -0.002] | down, -8 bpm |
| b_sin | 0.81 | [0.37, 1.23] | -- |
| b_cos | 0.70 | [0.25, 1.23] | ~2 bpm swing |
| b_age | 0.500 (at cap) | [0.00, 0.50] | small, +0.15 bpm/yr |

- **Weight** enters at **0.322 bpm/kg**, essentially identical to the
  literature ~0.3 bpm/BMI-unit prior. Over the 74 -> 89 kg span this is a
  ~4.8 bpm slow contribution -- a measured slow driver, but see the
  weight-interpolation limit: over the post-2023 window it is interpolated,
  not measured.
- **Seasonal amplitude** = sqrt(b_sin^2 + b_cos^2) ~= 1.07 bpm
  (peak-to-trough ~2.1 bpm), matching the ~2 bpm literature prior.
- **Aging** pins at its prior cap (0.5 bpm/yr). Per the aging review this
  cap is ABOVE the plausible resting-HR aging rate (~0 to ~0.3 bpm/yr), so
  the model OVER-removes aging and the residual below is conservative (B8).

### B2. Citalopram in-sample RHR beta (vs the -8 bpm prior)

**b_dose = -0.038 bpm/mg -> -1.14 bpm at 30 mg steady state**, CI roughly
[-1.9, -0.1] bpm across all four curves. **Sign matches the literature prior
(DOWN), magnitude is much smaller in-sample (~1 bpm vs the ~8 bpm prior).**

This attenuation is expected and is itself informative: citalopram initiation
(2024-04) coincides with the **steepest** RHR-rise window (raw RHR rises
+3.16 bpm/yr over the citalopram era). The drug's downward push is
being fought by a strong concurrent up-trend, so the in-sample dose
coefficient is a **lower bound on the true pharmacological effect**: the drug
is masking part of a rising LC baseline, exactly the ledger's "citalopram
MASKS an LC elevation" mechanism. The fact that RHR **rose while the drug
pushed it down** is corroborating evidence for a real, non-nuisance up-driver
post-2024.

### B3. Deconditioning term -- non-identifiable (per ledger section 5)

In every fit the **decond_amp collapses to 0** (contribution span 0.00 bpm).
This is not a null result about deconditioning physiology; it is the ledger's
predicted collinearity: the literature decond curve is **flat from ~year 1
onward**, so post-2022 it is a near-constant that is **collinear with the
intercept and the plateau level**, and its amplitude cannot be separated from
the static level (ledger section 5). The Garmin VO2Max-decay curve has a
wider CI on amplitude (up to ~6.1 bpm) but its point also sits at 0 once
weight and season are in.

The earlier draft argued the conclusion survives "because the curve is flat
after year 1 by construction" -- a by-construction argument that reads as
circular (it assumes deconditioning cannot continue, which is the very thing
the researcher hypothesised might). That argument is **replaced** by the
empirical test in B7: even a deconditioning curve that is still rising at
year 4 cannot absorb the post-2023 residual.

### B4. LC residual trajectory (the signal)

LC residual = observed RHR minus the modelled drivers
(decond + weight + citalopram + season + age), holding the intercept as the
level. Identical across all four curves (decond contributes 0):

| segment | residual median | Theil-Sen slope (bpm/yr) | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|---|
| pre-LC | 52.7 | -14.57 | [-16.49, -12.69] |
| 2022 | 53.7 | -2.20 | [-3.44, -1.13] |
| 2023 | 52.4 | 0.74 | [-0.16, 1.54] |
| 2024 | 51.4 | 1.06 | [0.62, 1.55] |
| 2025 | 53.0 | -1.05 | [-1.60, -0.28] |
| 2026 | 53.9 | 1.14 | [-1.03, 3.99] |
| **POST-2023 (2024+)** | -- | **+1.21** | analytic [1.05, 1.37]; **block-boot [0.79, 1.59]** |

**After removing deconditioning + weight + citalopram + season + aging, the
residual point estimate rises +1.21 bpm/yr post-2023.** The headline
interval is the block-bootstrap-of-slope CI in B5 (the analytic Theil-Sen
interval understates uncertainty; see B5). The rise sits in year 2-4 of LC,
well past the deconditioning plateau, on top of an over-capped aging term and
against a citalopram term that pushes down. But its magnitude and its
significance are fragile (B5, B6, B9); the residual is SUGGESTIVE, not
established (see verdict).

### B5. Confidence interval -- CORRECTED (reviewer finding 3a)

The post-2023 residual has **lag-1 autocorrelation 0.94** (it does
not decorrelate for weeks). The analytic Theil-Sen interval
[1.05, 1.37] assumes near-independence and is far too tight; the
earlier draft mislabelled it as a block-bootstrap interval. It is NOT
autocorrelation-corrected. The **proper interval is a block bootstrap OF THE
RESIDUAL SLOPE ITSELF** (resample the residual daily series in contiguous
(time, value) blocks, recompute the Theil-Sen slope per resample):

| block length | residual-slope 95% CI |
|---|---|
| 10 d | [0.79, 1.59] |
| 20 d | [0.72, 1.68] |
| 30 d | [0.66, 1.72] |
| 45 d | [0.58, 1.85] |

**Headline corrected CI: roughly [0.6, 1.9] bpm/yr** (block=10-20d gives
[0.79, 1.59] and [0.72, 1.68]; it
widens toward [0.58, 1.85] at block=45d). This is about 3x wider than the
mislabelled [1.05, 1.37]. It still excludes zero at short block lengths, but
the precision claim in the earlier draft was overstated.

### B6. Leave-one-year-out -- significance is FRAGILE (reviewer finding 3b)

The aggregate +1.21 is not evenly supported across years. Per-year residual
slopes: **2024 +1.06, 2025 -1.05, 2026 +1.14** --
2025 is NEGATIVE. Dropping each post-2023 year in turn:

| dropped year | post-2023 residual slope | 95% CI | verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| drop 2024 | 0.13 | [-0.14, 0.44] | **CROSSES ZERO** |
| drop 2025 | 1.22 | [1.03, 1.40] | excludes zero |
| drop 2026 | 1.31 | [1.09, 1.52] | excludes zero |

**Dropping 2024 collapses the slope to 0.13 [-0.14, 0.44], CI crossing zero.** The aggregate
is driven by 2024 and 2026, while 2025 is negative. So the +1.21 "excludes
zero" verdict is **NOT robust to removing the single most influential year**.
The residual rises across MOST of the post-2023 window, but the aggregate
slope is 2024/2026-driven and its significance is year-selection-fragile.

### B7. Slow / never-plateauing deconditioning -- the circularity defeater (reviewer finding 1c)

The researcher's original hypothesis was that deconditioning might CONTINUE
for years rather than plateau by year 1. Rather than assume it away, we hand
deconditioning shapes designed to absorb the late rise and re-fit:

| decond shape | fitted decond_amp | post-2023 residual slope | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|---|
| plateau at year 2 (tau=1y) | 0.00 | 1.21 | [1.05, 1.37] |
| plateau at year 3 (tau=2y) | 0.00 | 1.21 | [1.05, 1.37] |
| plateau at year 4 (tau=4y) | 0.00 | 1.21 | [1.05, 1.37] |
| linear-forever ramp (no plateau) | 1.40 | 1.08 | [0.93, 1.23] |

**This is the empirical result, not an assumption.** Even a deconditioning
curve that never plateaus -- a linear-forever ramp fit to 1.40 bpm/yr, a
physiologically unjustified 5.86 bpm rise across 2024-2026 -- only drops the
residual from +1.21 to +1.08. The exponential curves
that plateau at year 2/3/4 leave the residual at +1.21 unchanged, because
weight and age (both monotone-rising, mutually correlated ~0.96) are already
competing for that late trend and the optimizer prefers them. So the
"deconditioning does NOT dominate" DIRECTION does not rely on assuming a
year-1 plateau: it survives even when deconditioning is allowed to keep
rising through year 4. This directly answers the researcher's question "can
deconditioning continue longer?" -- yes it can be modelled that way, and it
still cannot absorb the post-2023 residual.

### B8. Aging bound -- a quantified minor confounder (reviewer finding 4)

The aging term pins at the model's 0.5 bpm/yr cap. The focused aging review
([`resting_hr_aging_magnitude_review.md`](../../literature/reviews/resting_hr_aging_magnitude_review.md))
bounds resting-HR aging in midlife at **~0 to at most ~0.3 bpm/yr (central
near zero or negative)** -- a MINOR, bounded confounder that cannot
manufacture a ~1 bpm/yr trend. Because the model cap (0.5) is ABOVE that
bound, the model OVER-removes aging, which makes the residual CONSERVATIVE:

| aging removed at | post-2023 residual slope | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|
| 0.5 (model cap; over-removes) | 1.21 | [1.05, 1.37] |
| 0.3 (aging-review upper bound) | 1.41 | [1.25, 1.57] |
| 0.0 (aging = zero) | 1.71 | [1.55, 1.87] |

A higher aging cap soaks MORE trend and SHRINKS the residual, so capping at
0.5 (above the literature ceiling) is conservative for the "residual still
moves" direction: at the literature upper bound (0.3) the residual is
1.41, and with aging=0 it is 1.71. Arithmetic check:
a residual of ~1.2 bpm/yr ON TOP of an already over-capped 0.5 bpm/yr aging
term implies ~1.7 bpm/yr unattributed to nuisance, far above any plausible
adult aging rate (~0.1-0.3 bpm/yr). Aging is folded in as a quantified minor
term; it cannot carry the residual, and LC and aging still cannot be
separated WITHIN the residual (the residual is labelled "LC/aging").

### B9. Weight interpolation -- the residual is weight-conditional (reviewer finding: additional)

Across the **entire post-2023 leverage window the weight regressor is
interpolated, not measured**: the largest weigh-in gap is
523 days (2023-09-12 to 2025-02-16), and the
2026 value (89 kg) is participant-reported, not in the dump. So the weight
curve under the residual is a straight line between one 2023 weigh-in and one
self-reported 2026 anchor. That interpolated rise (82.2 ->
89.0 kg) removes 2.20 bpm over 2024-2026 at
b_weight=0.322, and weight and the residual trade off almost one-for-one:

| weight coefficient | post-2023 residual slope | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|
| 0.322 (fitted) | 1.21 | [1.05, 1.37] |
| 0.644 (2x fitted) | 0.28 | [0.12, 0.44] |

**Doubling b_weight collapses the residual from +1.21 to
+0.28 [0.12, 0.44].** So the residual
magnitude is conditional on the weight coefficient being near 0.322, over a
window where weight is essentially unmeasured. The residual inherits the
uncertainty of an interpolated weight curve.

### B10. Sensitivity across both deconditioning curves -- robust (direction)

| curve | decond_amp | post-2023 residual point | headline block-boot CI |
|---|---|---|---|
| literature, tau = 3 mo | 0.00 | +1.21 | [0.79, 1.60] |
| literature, tau = 4 mo | 0.00 | +1.21 | [0.79, 1.58] |
| literature, tau = 6 mo | 0.00 | +1.21 | [0.79, 1.60] |
| Garmin VO2Max-decay | 0.00 | +1.21 | [0.80, 1.62] |

The residual POINT estimate is identical across all four curves (decond
collapses to 0 and, being flat post-year-1, cannot touch the post-2023
residual). Combined with B7 (slow-continuing curves also cannot absorb it),
the DIRECTION of the residual rise is robust to the deconditioning model. The
MAGNITUDE and SIGNIFICANCE are not (B5, B6, B9).

### B11. Falsification framing (ledger section 4) -- verdict

The ledger's falsification test: *if RHR keeps rising materially past year
1-2 after the confounder terms, deconditioning does NOT dominate and the
residual carries real LC/aging signal; if fully absorbed (flat residual), the
drift is largely nuisance.*

**Verdict: deconditioning does NOT dominate the long-run drift (direction),
but the residual is SUGGESTIVE, not established (magnitude/significance).**
The residual is not flat: it rises across most of 2024-2026 at a point
estimate near +1.21 bpm/yr, and this DIRECTION survives adversarial
testing -- a still-rising (linear-forever) deconditioning curve cannot absorb
it (B7), and the over-capped aging term makes it conservative (B8). BUT the
corrected CI is wide (~[0.6, 1.9], B5), the significance depends on including
2024 (leave-2024-out crosses zero, B6), and the magnitude depends on the
interpolated post-2023 weight term (2x b_weight collapses it, B9). So this is
**consistent with an LC-consistent residual and inconsistent with
deconditioning-dominant drift, but it is NOT a firm quantitative finding.**

## Honesty / limits (verbatim substance from ledger section 5, plus revision fragilities)

- **The residual is SUGGESTIVE, not established.** The DIRECTION (positive
  residual; deconditioning does not dominate) is robust; the MAGNITUDE
  (~[0.6, 1.9] bpm/yr) and SIGNIFICANCE are fragile. Three specific
  fragilities, added at review (2026-07-02):
  - **CI correction.** The residual has lag-1 autocorrelation ~0.94; the
    honest block-bootstrap-of-slope CI is ~[0.6, 1.9] bpm/yr, about 3x wider
    than the mislabelled analytic [1.05, 1.37] (B5).
  - **Year-selection fragility.** The aggregate is driven by 2024 and 2026;
    2025 is negative; leave-2024-out collapses the slope to 0.13 with a CI
    crossing zero (B6).
  - **Weight-interpolation fragility.** Post-2023 weight is entirely
    interpolated across the 523-day gap plus one self-reported
    2026 anchor; doubling b_weight collapses the residual to ~0.29 (B9).
- **The fitness DECLINE is modelled, not measured.** Real running-fitness
  data ends 2022-03 (last training run 2022-03-17); the VO2Max fall 53 -> 37
  after that is Garmin's inactivity-decay model, so we have a measured PEAK
  only, not a measured decline. The deconditioning term is a model curve
  either way (the literature/Garmin sensitivity pair, plus the B7 slow curves).
- **The slow drivers are collinear over 2022-2023.** The weight gain
  (74 -> ~82 kg), the VO2Max fast-drop, and LC onset all fall in the same
  2022-04 to 2023-06 window, and the weight series has no weigh-ins in exactly
  that window (interpolated over the 14-month gap). The early rise is where
  fitness, weight, and LC are MOST collinear and least separable; concretely
  the decond amplitude collapses to 0 (non-identifiable against the
  intercept/plateau level).
- **Two weigh-in gaps, both interpolated, both under leverage windows.** The
  2022-04 to 2023-06 gap sits over LC onset (early rise); the
  523-day 2023-09-12 to 2025-02-16 gap sits
  directly under the post-2023 residual this analysis interprets. Both are
  disclosed with equal prominence (B9).
- **Only trajectory shape/timing and the post-year-1 residual are
  identifiable. The static year-4 plateau LEVEL is NOT attributable** among
  the slow drivers. This analysis reports the post-2023 residual SLOPE, which
  is identifiable; it does NOT claim to partition the ~7 bpm static rise
  (54 -> 61) among the slow drivers.
- **The aging term is folded in as a quantified minor confounder** (~0 to
  ~0.3 bpm/yr upper, aging review) and sits at the model's higher 0.5 cap,
  which OVER-removes aging and makes the residual conservative (B8). Aging
  cannot carry a ~1 bpm/yr residual, but LC and aging cannot be separated
  within the residual, which is labelled "LC/aging" throughout; downstream
  synthesis must not quietly relabel it "LC".
- **The citalopram in-sample beta (~1 bpm) is a lower bound**, attenuated
  because initiation coincides with the steepest RHR-rise window; the true
  pharmacological effect is plausibly nearer the -8 bpm prior but is masked by
  the concurrent LC up-trend (citalopram start 2024-04 sits inside the
  accelerating phase).
- **The plateau (Part A) rests on multi-year windows and the year-wobble.**
  The non-plateau conclusion is a two-window comparison; a flat window
  (mid 2023-2024) sits inside the record, and window choice swings the raw
  slope widely (A3). The direction survives the honestly-chosen windows and
  the tie-robust rolling-median discriminator, but is window-sensitive.
- **n=1. Every attribution is "consistent with", never proof.**
- **"stress" = Garmin HRV-derived stress score (GSS), NOT mental stress.**

## Bottom line

1. **Plateau: no (window-sensitive).** The RHR rise is not bottoming out at
   ~4 years; the recent (2025-2026) slope 1.61 is
   not below the early-LC slope 1.31, and the
   rolling-median trajectory agrees. This rests on a two-window comparison
   with a flat window (mid 2023-2024) inside the record, so it is directional,
   not a precise slope.
2. **LC residual: SUGGESTIVE, not established.** After decond + weight +
   citalopram + season + age, the residual point estimate rises +1.21
   bpm/yr post-2023, but the corrected CI is ~[0.6, 1.9] and the significance
   is fragile to dropping 2024 and to the interpolated weight coefficient.
3. **Direction robust: deconditioning does NOT dominate.** The positive
   residual direction survives adversarial testing INCLUDING a slow /
   never-plateauing deconditioning curve (B7) and the aging bound (B8). This
   part is robust; it does not depend on assuming a year-1 plateau.
4. **Magnitude/significance NOT robust.** Corrected CI ~[0.6, 1.9] (B5),
   leave-2024-out crosses zero (B6), 2x b_weight collapses the residual (B9).
   The honest reading: consistent with an LC/aging residual and inconsistent
   with deconditioning-dominant drift, but not a firm quantitative finding.
