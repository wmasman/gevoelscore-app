## 1. What the data shows

The pre-reg does not yet carry empirical results — it is a r1 draft awaiting lock and execution. The **pre-committed empirical claim** is narrow: on calm days (HA11 v1's null-pool reference dates restricted to the unmedicated phase × train era × this pre-reg's §4.4 day-validity), the bout-level operand `bout_n_fast_recovery_day` (count of bouts with `recovery_half_life ≤ 15 min` AND `tail_length ≤ 45 min`, per parent MD §6.1 thresholds) is predicted to reproduce HA11 v1's SUPPORTED-on-train signal (+22.8 pp discrimination, median signed z = 2.168) at three §5 comparability bars — directional sign, ≥ +12.8 pp magnitude (±10 pp of HA11 v1's +22.8 pp), p < 0.05 under a block-permutation null at E[L] = 7. Verdict bands: PASSED (all 3 bars) / PARTIAL (2 of 3) / FAILED (≤ 1 of 3) / INCONCLUSIVE (walk-forward n < 30 calm or n < 10 crash). The framing is **methodology-validation, not substantive Wiggers-C4 retest**; a PASSED unblocks downstream HA-C4c without caveat, FAILED halts the bout-level cascade.

**[L3.2 — lag-carryover / reference-date inheritance precision — substantive]** — fires.

§4.5 specifies the calm-day pool as "200 random non-overlapping reference dates per HA11 v1 §4.9, generated with HA11 v1's seed (`20260605`), restricted to days satisfying §4.4 … further restricted to the unmedicated × train era subset (any of the 200 reference dates outside 2022-09-03 → 2023-12-31 are excluded)." But HA11 v1 `test.py` lines 202–206 (`build_null_sample`) draws the 200 reference dates from `ANALYSIS_START + timedelta(days=10) <= d <= VALIDATE_END` — the *full* train+validate corpus. The pre-reg's filter-after-sample inheritance is mathematically clean, but two consequences are not pre-stated and become load-bearing pre-lock:

1. The effective post-filter n is roughly the train share of the eligible window (train ≈ 485 calendar days; eligible window ≈ 1362 days; expected ≈ 71 of 200 reference dates land in train). Adding HA11-bout-redo's stricter §4.4 gate (the 21-day device-baseline lag + April-2024 cluster, neither in HA11 v1's §4.4) and §4.5's coverage gate (≥ 3 of 4 days with valid `bout_n_fast_recovery_day` AND lagged baseline) will reduce further — plausibly leaving ~50–65 calm-day reference windows. Comfortably above the §4.9 walk-forward gate (n ≥ 30), but worth pinning pre-lock.
2. The drafter's open question #2 anticipates this; a 30-second counting check on `per_day_master.csv` would settle it BEFORE lock, not at dry-run. The §10.4 dry-run halt path only fires if the gate fails at execution; the structural-completeness sanity-check at lock-time (per [`hypothesis_lock_process.md` §5 lock-blocking row "populatable across train AND validate eras"](../methodology/hypothesis_lock_process.md#5-sanity-check-questions-before-lock)) explicitly covers this class of pre-lock arithmetic check, and HA-C4b v1's `consolidation × train` zero-overlap precedent is the worked example of "what we catch at lock now to avoid v2 redrafts." Magnitude: *substantive* — the test will likely PASS the walk-forward gate, but the pre-reg should record the expected n explicitly (per CONVENTIONS §3.6 named-counts discipline carried at the gate-test level) before lock, not at execution. **Why the convention exists**: Natesan Batley 2023 finds 83.8% of n-of-1 studies ignore autocorrelation; the corollary at the reference-date-inheritance level is that ignoring the seed/sample-vs-filter distinction silently changes the comparability baseline. HA11 v1's contrast strength of +22.8 pp was estimated against 200 reference dates spanning both eras; HA11-bout-redo's ≈ 70 train-restricted dates have higher variance and may shift the comparability bar's effective threshold.

**[L3.2-companion — σ ≤ 0.5 low-variability skip inheritance — minor]** — fires.

§4.6 inherits HA11 v1's σ ≤ 0.5-events low-variability skip threshold verbatim ("If σ ≤ 0.5 events the day is flagged low-variability and skipped per HA11 v1 §4.5"). HA11 v1's `u_dip_count` had observed σ in [0.78, 0.98] and zero low-variability skips (per HA11 result.md trailing paragraph). But `bout_n_fast_recovery_day` is structurally a different count distribution — the [`bout_level_dose_response_calibration/result.md`](../analyses/descriptive/operationalisation_support/bout_level_dose_response_calibration/result.md) buildup-post-CPAP cell reports `bout_n_per_day` β = −0.020 over 50 days (operand is populated) but does not directly report the σ distribution for `bout_n_fast_recovery_day` on the rolling 60-day baseline. A higher zero-rate per day (plausible for a stricter threshold operand) would push baseline σ below 0.5 for a larger fraction of days, silently inflating the low-variability skip rate. Magnitude: *minor* — pre-reg §10.2 companion descriptives correctly note "low-variability skip rate" is reported; pre-reg §10.4 dry-run halt criteria do NOT include a low-variability skip-rate halt trigger. **Why the convention exists**: HA11 v1's threshold was tuned to its operand's variability shape; inheriting verbatim without verifying the operand's variability shape risks a silent degradation of n at the calm-day pool. Drafter's open question #1 names this risk in the spirit. A pre-lock fix: either add a §10.4 halt condition ("if low-variability skip rate > X%, HALT") OR pre-commit a one-line dispatch in §8 acknowledging the inheritance risk + a §10.3 obligation to report skip rate prominently for cross-comparison.

Expected effect: closes the L3.2-companion fire; protects the pre-reg against a silent n-degradation failure mode that the walk-forward gate may pass through.
