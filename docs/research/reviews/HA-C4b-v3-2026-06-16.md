## 2. What fired and why

### Layer 1 — Universal reporting (inherits from SCRIBE 2016, STROBE 2007)

**[L1.4 — confounders enumerated, not silently controlled, CONVENTIONS §2.1] — substantive.** The v3 §4.3 rationale (3) asserts: *"1b.ii was originally introduced by v1 r1 to address a visualisation-driven concern in the consolidation phase, NOT the unmedicated phase. ... The unmedicated phase has substantially denser FIT coverage than consolidation ... The 1b.ii gate's binding case does not exist in v3's headline phase."* This claim is rhetorically load-bearing for the 1b.ii drop, but it is empirically partially-refuted by v2's own data: one unmedicated-phase episode (`2023-02-04`) did in fact fail the 1b.ii quartile-coverage check (per the §1 / §4.3 narrative). 10% of the unmedicated pooled pool failed 1b.ii on this corpus — not zero. The spec elides this by reframing the binding case as "the *pathological pattern* (partial coverage producing misleading per-day predictor values)" rather than "the gate firing", and asserts the pathological pattern is absent in unmedicated, but does not verify that assertion against the dropped episode's actual within-day coverage profile. A defensible 1b.ii drop on this footing requires either (a) a check that 2023-02-04's within-day coverage was *uniformly under-sampled* (a pattern 1b.i would also catch had the threshold been right) rather than *quartile-concentrated* (the pathological pattern 1b.ii is designed to catch), or (b) honest acknowledgment that the 1b.ii catch is being overridden as a deliberate tradeoff. Neither is in the v3 spec; the spec presents the binding-case-doesn't-bind-here framing as if settled.

### Layer 2 — Observational n=1 (inherits from Daza 2018, Personal Science)

**[L2.2 — stationarity check / acknowledgment, CONVENTIONS §3.1] — minor.** The v3 §4.3 defence assumes uniform FIT coverage density across the unmedicated phase (the asserted "denser FIT coverage than consolidation"). This is a stationarity-in-coverage claim across a ~24-month window (2022-04-04 → 2024-04-08) that contains lived-experience era shifts (pre-ergotherapy / post-ergotherapy per the §8 v3 caveat itself; CPAP-start 2024-01-10 per inherited v2 §8). The spec doesn't quantify or check the coverage stationarity assumption; the one empirical data point we have on the corpus (2023-02-04 failing 1b.ii) is dispatched by reframing rather than examined. Magnitude: minor because (a) the §4.5 lagged-baseline machinery already absorbs within-phase variability and (b) the v2 inherited caveat about CPAP-start surrounds the same window. The fix is a one-line acknowledgment in §8 or §4.3 that the v3 1b.ii drop assumes uniform-enough FIT coverage across the unmedicated window for the 600 + 900-sample gates to be sufficient, with the corpus check deferred.

**[L2.5 — held-out structure framing, memory `project_garmin_research_bias_boundary`] — substantive.** The v3 drafting context has seen v2's full result.md: per-episode z-scores on the unmedicated train arm (including 2023-02-04 at `max_signed_z = +3.73` on d-1), the E[L]* = 3.30 observation, the train arm (a)=42.9% / validate arm (a)=0% directional inconsistency, and the dropped-episode identity. The 1b.ii drop has the specific mechanical effect of restoring 2023-02-04 to the pool; the v3 drafter knows this is the train arm's highest-z episode. The Authorship block owns this exposure explicitly and the §8 v3 caveat acknowledges *"a strict reviewer might object that the drop was post-hoc 'rescue' of a specific high-signal episode"* — but the spec then dispatches the objection on the asymmetry-and-primitive-validity argument, which is independent of the specific dropped episode only in *principle*. In *practice* the spec's choice between three options (drop 1b.ii at full-run; apply 1b.ii at dry-run; tighten 1b.i to subsume 1b.ii's intent) is made under exposure to which option restores the high-z train episode. The spec picks option 1. Magnitude: substantive — the discipline is partially preserved (no headline-cell relock; the §5.1 bar is unchanged; the spec is honest about the exposure level), but the *operational choice within the asymmetry-fix space* is data-informed. The right discipline-rebuilding move would be a fresh-session redraft of the §10.2 fix space alone (which option to pick) with the dropped-episode identity held out — but that would be a §3.2 redraft, not the §3.3 refinement v3 claims to be.

### Layer 4 — Project-specific audit hooks (inherits from CONVENTIONS §3, §4)

**[L4.7 — caveat-class vs a-priori-class, CONVENTIONS §4.2] — minor.** The pacing-behaviour confounder caveat (§8 + §9) is qualitative-only and stays in the reporting layer, BUT the §9 SUPPORTED branch's pacing-era companion-read paragraph subtly *narrows the SUPPORTED reading's scope* in a way that could leak into interpretive use of the verdict: *"if v3 surviving episodes cluster in the pre-ergotherapy era ... SUPPORTED reading aligns with 'poor physical pacing → physical exertion → motion-filtered stress lead-up → crash'; if they cluster in the post-ergotherapy era OR in emotional-exertion-rich periods, the SUPPORTED reading requires more careful interpretation."* The §5.1 (a)+(b)+(c) bar is unchanged, so the verdict mechanics are fine, but the result.md is now empowered to attach pacing-era *interpretive caveats* to the SUPPORTED verdict whose strength depends on user-supplied date approximations ("approximate dates TBD" per §8). Magnitude: minor because the verdict bar itself is locked and the annotations are explicitly marked descriptive. ## 4. What would strengthen this finding

1. **Verify 2023-02-04's within-day coverage profile against the 1b.ii pathological pattern** before locking v3. Run a small ad-hoc check (FIT-file-level) on whether 2023-02-04 (and any of its lead-up days `[d-4, d-3, d-2, d-1]` that the episode profile reads) shows the *quartile-concentrated under-sampling* pattern 1b.ii was introduced to catch, or merely *uniform under-sampling* near the 1b.i threshold (which 1b.i alone is sufficient for). If 2023-02-04 shows the pathological pattern, the v3 1b.ii drop is overriding a real catch and the §8 caveat should say so honestly. If 2023-02-04 shows uniform under-sampling, the L1.4 / L2.2 fires above largely close. Inherits from CONVENTIONS §3.1 (personal-baseline-with-coverage-verified) and the [`stress_low_motion_primitive.md` §5 NaN policy](../methodology/stress_low_motion_primitive.md#5-nan-policy--day-validity-gate). Expected effect: either tightens the rationale (if pattern is uniform under-sampling, the binding-case-doesn't-bind claim is empirically supported) or surfaces an honest tradeoff (if pattern is quartile-concentrated, the §8 caveat needs to acknowledge the override and the result.md needs to report 2023-02-04's z-trace with that context).
2. **Tighten the §4.3 rationale (3) phrasing** to match what the data actually shows. Replace *"The 1b.ii gate's binding case does not exist in v3's headline phase"* with *"The pathological pattern 1b.ii was introduced to catch (partial-day coverage producing misleading per-day predictor values) is rarer in the unmedicated phase than the consolidation phase; the 2023-02-04 catch is an isolated case to be verified against its within-day coverage profile per [item 1 above]."* Inherits from CONVENTIONS §2.1 (limitations stated separately from results — and accurately). Expected effect: closes the L1.4 substantive fire as a wording-tightening rather than a structural change.
3. **Add a one-line acknowledgment of the discipline-stretch in the Authorship block.** The §3.3 framing is rhetorically convenient but the v3 situation (between a *test execution* and a *successor draft*) extends §3.3's written envelope (between *initial draft* and *first audit*). A one-line note — *"This invokes §3.3's intent in an extended scope per [pending §3.3b proposal]; the discipline-preserving requirements (no headline-cell change, fresh-session v3 test execution, honest §8 disclosure) are honoured even though §3.3's literal scope is r1-between-draft-and-audit"* — closes the side observation and creates an audit-able marker for a future `hypothesis_lock_process.md` revision. 4. **Reframe the §9 pacing-era companion-read to be verdict-invariant.** The current language conditions the pacing-era reading on the verdict label ("if SUPPORTED... if NOT-SUPPORTED..."). Replace with a single descriptive paragraph: *"Surviving episodes are annotated with pacing-era (pre-/post-ergotherapy) and emotional-event-proximity flags per §8. These flags are properties of the episode dates, not of the verdict label; the result.md surfaces them alongside the verdict regardless of which branch fires."* Inherits from CONVENTIONS §4.2 (caveat-class framing kept, a-priori-class framing cut — symmetrically across verdicts). Expected effect: closes the L4.7 minor fire as a wording-tightening, removes the interpretive flexibility risk surfaced in the L4.7 paragraph.
5. **Move the user-supplied date elicitation to lock-time, not result.md-time.** Approximate pacing-era + emotional-event dates are descriptive context that should ideally be in the v3 *spec record* (and held out from the v3 test-execution session), not introduced *during result.md production*. A short §8 sub-block with the user-supplied dates at lock-time, written before v3 lock and held out from the test session, removes the "post-result interpretive flexibility" risk that the side observation surfaced. Inherits from CONVENTIONS §1.2 (the contamination boundary discipline applied to descriptive context, not just the falsification bar). Expected effect: gives the v3 test-execution session a clean handoff (descriptive context locked at v3 lock; verdict at full-run; annotation read against the pre-locked context).
