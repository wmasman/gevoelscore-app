## 1. What the data shows

The target is a **pre-registration** (not a result). It states, before any logistic fit on the predictor, the locked operationalisation for a Personal-register recovery-debt test:

- **Headline claim**: in both train (2022-09-03 → 2023-12-31) and validate (2024-01-01 → 2026-06-05) windows independently, on an LC-era day `d` with yesterday's `is_crash == False` (gap day), a logistic regression of `is_crash at d` on `crash_count_14d = sum(is_crash[d-14 : d-1])` yields **OR > 1 with 95% Wald CI excluding 1**, AND the binned per-day rate is monotonically non-decreasing across `crash_count_14d ∈ {0, 1, 2, 3+}` within a 5-percentage-point tolerance. NOT-SUPPORTED requires all three of: (a) Wald CI brackets 1, (b) monotonicity violated, (c) ≥ 2 of 3 window arms (7d / 14d / 30d) fail to discriminate.
- **Secondary descriptive outcomes**: window-sensitivity ladder (W = 7, 14, 30); per-phase tests across the four Citalopram phases; same-day Spearman ρ between `crash_count_14d` and `gevoelscore`.

The empirical claim is a logistic-OR-plus-binned-monotonicity test on a labels-only predictor. The interpretive framing — that this metric operationalises "recovery debt" rather than "shared underlying cause" — is held separate from the OR / CI mechanics, and §8 caveat 1 is explicit that the design cannot adjudicate the two readings.

### Layer 3 — Time-series specific (inherits from Natesan Batley 2023, WWC 2022, CENT 2015)

**[L3.1 / L3.4 — Natesan Batley 2023, CONVENTIONS §3, [methodology/permutation_null_block_length.md](../methodology/permutation_null_block_length.md)] — blocking.** The §4.5.1 primary inference is a **plain logistic regression with Wald 95% CI on OR**, and §4.5.3 explicitly retires any external null sample: *"Logistic regression already provides an internal null comparison (the H0: β_1 = 0). No external null sample is needed for the primary headline."* The Wald CI on logistic-regression coefficients assumes i.i.d. Bernoulli residuals — and on this test that assumption is violated in **two compounding ways**:

1. **Predictor autocorrelation by construction.** `crash_count_14d(d)` and `crash_count_14d(d+1)` share 13 of 14 input days. Adjacent eligible-day predictor values differ by at most ±1 (one entering, one leaving). On a sequence of 700-1000 eligible days, the effective independent sample size is a small multiple of the number of distinct 14-day-window-windows, not the day count.
2. **Outcome autocorrelation conditional on the predictor.** Crash *episodes* span multiple consecutive days; even after the §4.2 gap-day-d-1 eligibility rule restricts the sample to crash-start days plus pure-baseline gap days, the predictor itself groups days by being in dense vs sparse periods, and dense periods cluster temporally. The "stressful period that causes both" failure mode (§8 caveat 1) is precisely the temporal-clustering mechanism whose Wald-CI signature is downward-biased standard errors and inflated OR significance.

The project canonical methodology decision in [`methodology/permutation_null_block_length.md`](../methodology/permutation_null_block_length.md) is **stationary bootstrap with E[L] = 7 days as the project-wide default for both bootstrap CIs and permutation null tests**. That MD is neither cited nor applied in HA-P7. The §4.5.3 explicit retirement of an external null is the load-bearing fire — it states as a positive design choice that no autocorrelation-aware inference is needed, which contradicts the policy MD.

Natesan Batley 2023's headline finding is that 83.8% of n-of-1 medical articles ignore autocorrelation; HA-P7's §4.5 as drafted is exactly that failure mode. Combined with the [`citalopram_phase_stratification.md` §6 "Independent obligations"](../methodology/citalopram_phase_stratification.md#6-pre-registration-template-for-new-hypothesis-mds) clause ("autocorrelation handling per CONVENTIONS §3 + Natesan Batley 2023: HAC SE / block bootstrap / effective-N"), this is a binding obligation the pre-reg does not engage. Magnitude: **blocking**, because the headline verdict in §5 is OR-CI-driven (criterion a) and the OR-CI is the precise quantity the i.i.d. assumption inflates. Under a block-bootstrap CI at E[L] = 7 days the §5 (a) bar may convert SUPPORTED → INCONCLUSIVE on the same point estimate.

**[L3.3 — multiple testing across lags/channels, CONVENTIONS §3] — substantive.** The reported family is the 3-window logistic ladder × {train, validate} × 5 phase cells (4 phases + pooled) × {primary outcome, secondary 3-day forward outcome} — implicitly 60+ inferential tests reported in the result. The §5 headline does concentrate on a single combination (pooled LC × W=14 × primary outcome × {train, validate}), and the §5 (c) criterion folds **window-sensitivity** into the headline by requiring ≥ 2 of 3 windows to fail — so window multiplicity is absorbed by the structure. But the headline-vs-sensitivity discipline for the **per-phase** sub-tests is not pre-registered as a hard rule. §4.4 says "per-phase verdicts are descriptive" and §4.6 surfaces them as INCONCLUSIVE-where-low-power, which is the right direction; but the doc never pre-registers that per-phase verdicts cannot promote to SUPPORTED individually, leaving room for narrative-level upgrading post-result. Magnitude: substantive (tractable as a one-paragraph §5 lock equivalent to HA-C4b's equivalent).

**L3.2 (lag-carryover assumptions named) — no fire.** The predictor *is* a lagged count; the lag structure is explicit (W ∈ {7, 14, 30}) and §4.1 names the inclusive/exclusive window endpoints precisely.

**L3.5 (trend vs level claim) — no fire.** The claim is an OR-on-density at a moving observation window — neither a trend claim nor mixed with one. The framing is consistent throughout.

### Layer 4 — Project-specific audit hooks (inherits from CONVENTIONS §3, §4)

**[L4.4 — crash-drop sensitivity row, CONVENTIONS §3.4] — minor.** The §3 + §4 descriptive same-day Spearman correlation between `crash_count_14d` and `gevoelscore at d` is a Layer-4-class correlation on a PEM-pacing variable (gevoelscore). CONVENTIONS §3.4 binds such correlations to report ρ both with and without `is_crash == True` rows. The §4.2 eligibility rule already excludes `is_crash at d-1 == True`, but does NOT exclude `is_crash at d == True` (which is the outcome side). The §3.4 hook is not retired by descriptive-only labelling — its purpose is audit completeness. Magnitude: minor (one row in the §4.5.2 / descriptive output). A second-order point: for the **primary inferential** test, the §3.4 hook is **inapplicable by construction** — dropping `is_crash at d == True` rows from a test whose outcome IS `is_crash at d` would eliminate every positive case. A one-sentence explicit dispatch in §8 ("§3.4 inapplicable to the primary because outcome IS `is_crash`; applied to the descriptive same-day gevoelscore correlation as ...") would close the hook with intent rather than by omission.

**[L4.6 — named counts, CONVENTIONS §3.6] — minor.** §3 + §6 name the labels source as `crash_v2-definition/labels_crash_v2.csv` (path issue: see Side). The §4.4 phase-n estimates name the predicate ("eligible heavy-exertion subset" — well, "eligible gap-day-d-1 subset" here) but use ranges like "~700-1000 LC-era pooled" without the source-file predicate. CONVENTIONS §3.6 binds three things: labeling scheme + unit + source file in the same sentence. The pre-reg's count discipline is reasonable but a couple of phrasings ("Total eligible (gap-day-d-1) days estimated at ~700-1000 LC-era pooled" in §3) would tighten by naming `is_crash` predicate explicitly + the labels CSV path. Magnitude: minor (in line with how HA-C4b handled this).

**L4.1 (personal baseline) — no fire.** Crash-day density is a count, not a continuous channel needing z-against-rolling-baseline. The §3.1 hook is about HR/steps/stress absolutes; it does not bind here. The §4.5.2 binned tabulation is itself a kind of personal baseline (rates within the participant's own distribution), which is the right shape.

**L4.2 (`_lagged_lcera` for PEM hypothesis) — no fire.** The predictor uses no Garmin channel; no `exertion_class` / `step_z_30d` / `push_burden_7d` appears. The §3.2 audit hook is not triggered.

**L4.3 (one column per definitional pair) — no fire.** Only `is_crash` enters the predictor (with 3 window aggregations, which are distinct sensitivity arms by design, not near-identical pairs).

**L4.5 (spike-detecting metric) — no fire.** §3.5 is about acute arousal proxies (HR / stress / respiration). A 14-day count of binary crash-day labels is a density across multiple days, not a daily average being preferred over a within-day spike. The hook is non-applicable.

**L4.7 (caveat-class vs a-priori-class) — no fire.** §8 entries are all caveat-class. The per-phase stratification choice is grounded in the independently-confirmed v3 dose-response finding ([`citalopram_dose_response §5.6`](../methodology/citalopram_dose_response_stress_mean_sleep.md#56-v3-amendment--multi-channel-confirmation-added-2026-06-14)) plus the §4.4 explicit rationale that the predictor is a count, not a continuous channel, so §5.B-style dose-adjustment is not applied. This is CONVENTIONS §4.2-permitted: the stratification axis is pre-specified by an independent prior result.

**L4.8 (prior-driven confirmatory framing) — no fire.** Three named priors (lived experience explicit + named user quote; PEM recovery-debt mechanism; literature partial). Confirmatory framing per §4.3 is justified. The held-out validate window is preserved (both-windows-independent requirement) so walk-forward discipline is maintained.

The same broken path appears in HA-C4b §3 (see [`reviews/HA-C4b-2026-06-15.md`](HA-C4b-2026-06-15.md) — that reviewer accepted the citation verbatim without verifying), so the bad path is propagating by copy-paste. Cleanest fix: point at the labels MD `crash_v2-definition/definition.md` for the *scheme* and at `processed/crash_labels/labels_crash_v2.csv` (via `$GEVOELSCORE_DATA_PATH`) for the *file*.
- **Side** — sample-definition revision vs register entry. [`personal_hypotheses.md` §P7 Operationalisation](../personal_hypotheses.md) Sample row says *"LC era days where `d` itself is NOT in a crash episode (exclude within-episode autocorrelation)"*. HA-P7 §4.2 explicitly inverts this — *"the rule does NOT exclude days where `d` itself is a crash-start"* — and names this as the Option A resolution to the register-table inconsistency. The pre-reg is self-consistent about the revision; but `personal_hypotheses.md` P7's Operationalisation row is now stale and would benefit from a one-line forward pointer (e.g. "see HA-P7 §4.2 for the revised eligibility rule").
- **Side** — caveat-4 reference compression. §4.4 says "the phase-stratified read addresses P7 caveat 4 — `gevoelscore` (and hence `crash_v2` labels derived from it) is mildly dose-modulated per [intervention_effects §8]'s small detrend-surviving step at 2026-03-20." The personal_hypotheses.md P7 caveat 4 is materially more specific — it documents that the v3 multi-channel dose-response test did NOT extend to `gevoelscore` (which is out of scope per parent §3b), and that Session C's small detrend-surviving step at 2026-03-20 stands unchanged. HA-P7's compressed phrasing implies the small step is the dose-response evidence on `gevoelscore`, which is the right read but stronger than the register caveat phrasing. Minor; verify the §4.4 sentence quotes the register's "session C small detrend-surviving step" framing rather than a "mildly dose-modulated" paraphrase.
- **Side** — early-LC-era coverage shadow. §4.2 condition 4 requires the 14-day primary window be fully within crash_v2 coverage. Per DATA_DICTIONARY.md is_crash coverage starts 2022-09-03 — so eligible-by-LC days from 2022-04-04 through 2022-09-16 are excluded by the W=14 coverage constraint (longer for W=30: through 2022-10-02). The §3 note ("Total eligible ... ~700-1000 LC-era pooled") absorbs this implicitly. Worth a one-line surface in §4.2 ("approximately the first 5 months of LC era are excluded by W=14 coverage") so the §10.1 dry-run is not surprised.
- **Side** — §5 (b) monotonicity tolerance is loose at small bin rates. With §7 expected bin-0 rates of 2-5% and bin-3+ rates of 5-15%, the absolute 5-percentage-point tolerance permits e.g. bin-1 at 3% and bin-2 at 0% to pass monotonicity (rate(2) = 0 ≥ rate(1) − 0.05). At small expected rates the 5pp absolute tolerance effectively waives criterion (b) for the lower bins. A relative tolerance (e.g. "bin-i+1 may be at most 50% lower than bin-i") or a Wilson-CI-overlap test between consecutive bins would tighten the criterion without losing the monotonicity intent.
- **Side** — secondary outcome `any_crash_in_next_3d` defined inconsistently. §4.3 names it `any_crash_in_next_3d(d) = any(is_crash[d : d + 3])` and then describes "covers d, d+1, d+2, d+3 (4-day forward window)". The Python slice `is_crash[d : d + 3]` is a half-open interval covering exactly 3 days (d, d+1, d+2), not 4. Either the slice or the prose is wrong. (Tested mentally: if `d` is an index, `[d:d+3]` is 3 elements; "covers d, d+1, d+2, d+3" is 4.) Off-by-one to fix before lock — propagates into the §10.2 test script if left.

This is exactly the Daza 2018 within-subject covariate-history framing the layer inherits from, and it is named at the right granularity (subject-baseline-at-similar-exposure, not population).

The doc names confirmatory framing without overclaiming. Walk-forward discipline preserved via both-windows-independent requirement.
- **L4.7 (caveat-class vs a-priori-class) passes structurally** — §8 caveats are diagnostic (causal-attribution ambiguity, selection bias, self-report propagation, dose-modulation), not analytical lifts. The per-phase stratification is grounded in an independently-confirmed prior result, not on a within-corpus inspection.
- **§8 caveat 6 (protocol disturbs the test) passes well** — explicitly names the protocol-protective vs predictor-discriminative ambiguity, and §9's NOT-SUPPORTED-in-both-windows branch surfaces both readings ("no recovery-debt mechanism" AND "protocol is protective"), recommending result.md surface BOTH without favoring one. This is the correct shape for an n=1 protocol-overlapping test.

## 4. What would strengthen this finding

1. **Adopt block-bootstrap CIs on OR per [`methodology/permutation_null_block_length.md`](../methodology/permutation_null_block_length.md) in §4.5.1; retire the §4.5.3 "no external null needed" claim.** Replace the Wald CI on OR with a stationary-bootstrap CI at E[L] = 7 days (project canonical), reporting the data-driven E[L]* estimator as a confirmatory companion. For the binned tabulation in §4.5.2, replace Wilson CIs with block-bootstrap percentile CIs over the same E[L] = 7 days policy (Wilson CIs are i.i.d. by construction). Equivalently acceptable: a GEE-style cluster-robust SE clustered on crash-episode id, with the §10.1 dry-run reporting effective N relative to nominal N. Expected effect: the headline Wald CI on OR will widen; the §5 (a) bar may convert SUPPORTED → INCONCLUSIVE on the same point estimate, which is the honest read. This is the **load-bearing pre-lock revision**; closes L3.1 + L3.4 together. (Layer 3 blocking.)

2. **Pre-register a `gevoelscore`-recent-mean covariate sensitivity for the §8 caveat 1 alternative readings.** Caveat 1 names "recovery-debt" vs "shared underlying cause" as undecidable, but the §4 measurement protocol pre-registers no operational mitigation. A simple secondary sensitivity adding `gevoelscore_lagged_mean_14d(d) = mean(gevoelscore[d-14 : d-1])` as a covariate in the §4.5.1 logistic regression would discriminate between "the recent-crash signal is just recent low-gevoelscore signal" (β_count → 0 once gevoelscore mean is in the model) vs "the recent-crash count carries information beyond recent gevoelscore" (β_count survives). This is a covariate sensitivity, not a primary spec change — pre-registered as such it constrains post-result interpretation without re-defining the headline. (Layer 4 substantive.)

3. **Pre-register the within-test multi-comparison discipline as a hard §5 rule** — pick one of: (a) "the headline locks **only** on pooled LC × W=14 × primary outcome; all per-phase, per-window-arm, and secondary-outcome verdicts are diagnostic and cannot promote to SUPPORTED individually"; or (b) explicit Bonferroni-style adjustment across the reported tiers. (a) is what §4.4 + §5 effectively imply; making it a hard rule retires L3.3 fully. (Layer 3 substantive.)

4. **Add a crash-drop sensitivity row (or one-line explicit dispatch) for the descriptive same-day gevoelscore Spearman** — for the §3 / §4.5.2-adjacent same-day correlation between `crash_count_14d` and `gevoelscore at d`, report ρ both on the full eligible-day sample and with `is_crash at d == True` dropped, with `|Δ ρ| > 0.10` flagged per CONVENTIONS §3.4. Closes L4.4 minor. Equivalently acceptable: a one-sentence §8 dispatch explaining why §3.4 is inapplicable to the inferential test (outcome IS `is_crash`) and naming the descriptive correlation as the §3.4-binding venue. (Layer 4 minor.)

5. **Fix the broken `labels_crash_v2.csv` path** in §3 and §4.5 — replace `crash_v2-definition/labels_crash_v2.csv` with either the in-repo definition MD reference (`crash_v2-definition/definition.md` for the scheme) plus the actual file path (`processed/crash_labels/labels_crash_v2.csv` via `$GEVOELSCORE_DATA_PATH`), or document the indirection one-line. The bad path is also in HA-C4b §3 — the fix should propagate. (Side / L4.6.)

6. **Reconcile §4.3 secondary-outcome slice with prose** — `is_crash[d : d + 3]` is a 3-element slice; the prose says "covers d, d+1, d+2, d+3 (4-day forward window)". Pick one. If 4-day is intended, write `is_crash[d : d + 4]`. (Side.)

## 5. Verdict

**REVISION RECOMMENDED** — Layer 3 blocking fire: §4.5.1 uses Wald CIs on logistic OR and §4.5.3 explicitly retires any external null, on a predictor whose construction (overlapping 14-day rolling counts of a serially-clustered binary outcome) is exactly the autocorrelation pattern Natesan Batley 2023 flags and the project canonical [`permutation_null_block_length.md`](../methodology/permutation_null_block_length.md) policy binds against; the headline OR-CI is likely the affected quantity.
