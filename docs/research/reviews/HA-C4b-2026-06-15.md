## 1. What the data shows

The target is a **pre-registration** (not a result). It states, before any test on the new primitive's discrimination against `is_crash`, the locked operationalisation for a Wiggers-C4-style precursor test:

- **Headline claim**: among `crash_v2` episodes whose lead-up window includes a heavy / very_heavy `exertion_class_lagged_lcera` day, at least one day's `stress_low_motion_min_count_S60_Mlow` deviates from the lagged same-phase baseline by `z ≥ 1.5` (one-sided elevated), and the crash-episode frequency of this trigger exceeds the matched null-sample frequency in **both train and validate windows**, with the **consolidation-phase verdict** as the headline.
- **Secondary descriptive outcomes**: same-day Spearman of the primary against `gevoelscore`; per-episode construct-disambiguation against `stress_high_duration_min` (primary sibling, ρ = 0.79 per the post-viz revision) and `u_dip_count` (secondary sibling, ρ = 0.556); respiration-companion sensitivity via `n_minutes_resp_above_18`.

The empirical claim is a deviation-from-lagged-baseline trigger-frequency test. The interpretive framing — that this metric operationalises "stuck sympathetic / walls of orange while body is at rest" — is held separate from the trigger-frequency mechanics, and the §8 caveats list explicit motion-filter caveats and protocol-as-disturbance warnings.

## 2. What fired and why

### Layer 1 — Universal reporting (inherits from SCRIBE 2016, STROBE 2007)

No fires. Pre-specification is the whole point of this document; the analysis frame (LC-era; train 2022-09-03 → 2023-12-31; validate 2024-01-01 → 2026-06-05; per-phase headline at consolidation), the predictor primitive and its source script, the lagged baseline window `[d−90, d−30]`, the trimmed-mean construction, the 600-then-900-sample validity gates, the three N_std tiers, and the null-sample seed are all named explicitly. The §8 caveats section is structurally separate from the §5 falsification criterion.

### Layer 2 — Observational n=1 (inherits from Daza 2018, Personal Science)

No substantive fires. The counterfactual framing is per-phase comparison-to-subject-baseline-at-similar-exposure (heavy-exertion days within the same Citalopram phase), which is the right shape for n=1. Stationarity in the citalopram dimension is addressed by per-phase stratification; stationarity in the LC-vs-pre-LC dimension is addressed by the `_lagged_lcera` baseline restriction; the §8 "buildup-vs-afbouw asymmetry" caveat acknowledges a residual non-stationarity. Calendar-time vs subject-time is consistently calendar-time. Data provenance traceable to specific scripts and Stage 1's commit (`14a32a3`). Prior motivation (lived-experience pacing protocol; Wiggers C4; mechanism) is explicit in §2.

### Layer 3 — Time-series specific (inherits from Natesan Batley 2023, WWC 2022, CENT 2015)

**[L3.1 / L3.4 — Natesan Batley 2023, CONVENTIONS §3] — substantive.** The null-sample machinery (§4.9: 200 random non-overlapping reference dates per phase, seed `20260605`) is inherited verbatim from HA11/HA06b/HA10 and is **silent on within-window serial dependence**. The project's canonical methodology decision in [`methodology/permutation_null_block_length.md`](../methodology/permutation_null_block_length.md) is "stationary bootstrap with E[L] = 7 days as the project-wide default" applied to both bootstrap CIs *and* permutation null tests. That MD is neither cited nor applied in HA-C4b. The MD explicitly grandfathers HA01b/HA02c/HA08/HA11/H05 ("keep their original block treatment for their original verdict") but HA-C4b is a *new* pre-reg, post-MD, and should adopt the default. Natesan Batley 2023's headline finding is that 83.8% of n-of-1 medical articles ignore autocorrelation; the C4b null-as-random-restart construction is exactly the failure mode. This concern is reinforced by [`citalopram_phase_stratification.md` §6 "Independent obligations"](../methodology/citalopram_phase_stratification.md#6-pre-registration-template-for-new-hypothesis-mds), which binds framework adopters to "autocorrelation handling per CONVENTIONS §3 + Natesan Batley 2023: HAC SE / block bootstrap / effective-N" — HA-C4b is a framework adopter (§4.4) but does not engage this obligation. Magnitude: substantive. Under correlated lead-up windows the null frequency is biased downward, inflating apparent discrimination; under block-permutation the §5 (b) 15-pp margin may compress.

**[L3.3 — multiple testing across lags/channels, CONVENTIONS §3] — substantive.** The reported family is the 9-column sensitivity ladder × 3 N_std tiers × 4 phases × {primary 4d, secondary 5d} × {one-sided, bidirectional} — implicitly hundreds of inspected combinations. The §5 falsification criterion concentrates the headline on a single combination (consolidation × S60_Mlow × N=1.5 × primary 4d × one-sided), and the §8 "Multi-comparison" caveat addresses the *across-pre-reg* multiplicity. But the *within-test* family discipline is implicit, not pre-registered. The doc would be tighter under either (i) "headline locks **only** on the single pre-specified combination; all other arms are diagnostic and cannot promote to SUPPORTED on their own" stated as a hard rule in §5, or (ii) a Bonferroni-style correction across the reported tiers. The current phrasing reports the secondary and sensitivity-check tiers "alongside" the primary, which leaves room for narrative-level upgrading. Magnitude: substantive (probably tractable as a one-paragraph §5 lock).

**L3.2 (lag-carryover assumptions named) — no fire.** §4.2's exertion-conditioning rule uses `d` OR `d−1` heavy / very_heavy as the eligibility condition, which is the carryover-aware framing. The `_lagged_lcera` variant is used throughout.

**L3.5 (trend vs level claim) — no fire.** The claim is a deviation-from-lagged-baseline trigger (a level claim against a sliding reference), consistently framed. Trajectory and level are not mixed.

### Layer 4 — Project-specific audit hooks (inherits from CONVENTIONS §3, §4)

**[L4.4 — crash-drop sensitivity row, CONVENTIONS §3.4] — minor.** The §4.11.1 secondary descriptive outcome is a same-day Spearman correlation between `stress_low_motion_min_count_S60_Mlow` and `gevoelscore`. CONVENTIONS §3.4 binds "every Layer 4+ correlation / CCF / regression that touches PEM-pacing variables" to report the result both with and without `is_crash == True` rows, with `|Δ ρ| > 0.10` surfaced as a finding. The primary column is a PEM-pacing variable; the §4.11.1 outcome is a Layer-4 same-day correlation; the doc does not pre-register the crash-drop sensitivity row. The doc's framing of §4.11.1 as "descriptive only; no SUPPORTED bar" softens but does not retire the §3.4 obligation, which is about audit completeness, not headline status. Magnitude: minor (a row in the §4.11.1 output table).

**[L4.3 — one column per definitional pair, CONVENTIONS §3.3] — minor.** §8 explicitly acknowledges "the `below_moderate` motion class is currently identical to `low_or_below`. The S{50,60,75}_Mbelow_mod columns will produce identical results to the corresponding S_Mlow columns by construction. The 9-column ladder effectively reduces to 6 unique columns in v1." §4.10 then reports the §5 falsification criterion across "all 9 stress × motion columns". The §3.3 audit hook says "if a draft Layer 4 result includes both members of a near-identical pair, stop and pick one." The honest read is that the redundancy is acknowledged in the caveats but the sensitivity-ladder block still reports the duplicates as if they were independent arms. Cleaner: §4.10 reports 6 unique columns and the 3 duplicates are referenced once as "identical-by-construction" in the table caption. Magnitude: minor (alignment with §3.3 spirit; the §8 acknowledgment defuses most of the concern).

**L4.1 (personal baseline vs absolute thresholds) — no fire.** The absolute `stress >= 60` is part of the primitive's spike-detection mechanism; the test discriminates on z-deviation from a lagged personal baseline. CONVENTIONS §3.1 explicitly permits absolutes-as-sanity-floor inside the primitive when personalisation happens in the test. The §8 dose-modulation caveat acknowledges the residual concern and the per-phase stratification is the mitigation.

**L4.2 (`_lagged_lcera` for PEM hypothesis) — no fire.** Explicit and well-cited.

**L4.5 (spike-detecting metric) — no fire.** The primitive *is* a spike-counting metric by construction.

**L4.6 (named counts) — no fire.** Counts are scheme + unit + source: "29 crash_v2 episodes (via `crash_v2-definition/labels_crash_v2.csv`)", "1739 days covered (1722 valid via the ≥ 600-stress-sample gate)", phase-by-phase n estimates in §4.4. A couple of in-line phrasings ("~100-200 days in the conditioned subset") could name the predicate but §3 covers source-file specificity globally.

**L4.7 (caveat-class vs a-priori-class) — no fire.** §8 entries are properly caveat-class. The per-phase stratification choice is a-priori grounded in the *independently confirmed* v3 dose-response finding ([`citalopram_dose_response §5.6`](../methodology/citalopram_dose_response_stress_mean_sleep.md#56-v3-amendment--multi-channel-confirmation-added-2026-06-14)), which CONVENTIONS §4.2 permits for stratification choices that are pre-specified by an independent prior result.

**L4.8 (prior-driven framing) — no fire.** Three named priors (lived experience, Wiggers source, mechanism); confirmatory framing is justified per §4.3.

### Side observations

- **Side** — date arithmetic on the buildup CPAP buffer. §4.4 + §6 say "exclude buildup days before 2024-04-30 (the first 22 days of buildup)". With buildup starting 2024-04-09, "before 2024-04-30" excludes 2024-04-09 through 2024-04-29 = **21 days**, not 22. Either drop the inequality to "before or equal to 2024-04-30" (excludes 22 days) or change the count to "the first 21 days". Off-by-one in the date math; trivial to fix.
- **Side** — `afbouw + post-afbouw` window in the §4.4 table is shown as "2026-03-20 → 2026-06-05" with no separator. Per [`citalopram_phase_stratification §3`](../methodology/citalopram_phase_stratification.md#3-the-four-phase-citalopram-traject-stratification), afbouw ends 2026-06-05 and post-afbouw runs 2026-06-06 → present. The merge choice (post-afbouw subsumed into afbouw because of the 9-day data-cut window) is documented in the prose, but the table row label is loose; cleaner to write `afbouw (post-afbouw merged, see prose)`.
- **Side** — the post-viz revision cites a file at `C:/Users/Gebruiker/Documents/gevoelscore-data/analyses/stress_low_motion_viz/viz-notes.md`. That path is outside the audit-able tree; doc-only review cannot verify the cited 0.79 / 0.556 ρ values or the Family A 2024-11-26 case. The post-viz revision is materially shaping §4.3 + §4.11, so the reproducibility chain is now anchored on an external artefact. Either commit a sanitised summary of the viz notes into the repo or document that the cited values are reproducible from a script + commit hash.
- **Side** — §4.10 phrases the monotonicity gate as "S=50 ≥ S=60 ≥ S=75 frequency" where "frequency" reads ambiguously: across the sensitivity ladder, frequency-of-episodes-passing-the-falsification-bar and per-day-qualifying-minutes-count are different monotonicities (the former follows from the latter under the same baseline but is not the same check). Worth a one-line clarification of which monotonicity is the gate.

## 3. What does not fire (selective)

- **L4.2 (`_lagged_lcera` discipline) passes with non-trivial evidence** — the baseline construction (§4.5) layers three constraints: LC-era only, same-phase only, and §4.3-valid only. Explicitly cited to CONVENTIONS §3.2.
- **L4.5 (spike-detecting metric) passes structurally** — the primitive is per-minute threshold-crossing aggregated to a per-day integer count, the §3.5 canonical pattern; the methodology MD §503 engages this audit hook by name.
- **L2.6 + L4.8 (prior-driven confirmatory framing) passes** — three independent priors (lived experience, Wiggers source text, mechanism), and the doc names confirmatory framing without overclaiming. Walk-forward discipline is preserved via the held-out validate window + both-windows-independent requirement.
- **L2.4 (data provenance) passes well** — every cited column, script, and commit is traceable.

## 4. What would strengthen this finding

1. **Cite and apply [`methodology/permutation_null_block_length.md`](../methodology/permutation_null_block_length.md) in §4.9** — replace the random-non-overlapping-reference-date null with a stationary-bootstrap null at E[L] = 7 days (block-permutation of crash / null episode labels across the per-phase eligible pool), and report the data-driven block-length estimator E[L]\* as the methodology MD specifies. Expected effect: the §5 (b) discrimination margin will likely compress under the autocorrelation-aware null; the §5 (a) frequency criterion is unaffected. This is the single most important pre-lock revision because it closes the L3.1 + L3.4 fires together. (Layer 3 substantive.)

2. **Pre-register the within-test multi-comparison discipline in §5** — pick one of: (a) "the headline locks **only** on consolidation × S60_Mlow × N_std = 1.5 × primary 4d × one-sided; all sensitivity arms are diagnostic and cannot promote to SUPPORTED individually"; or (b) explicit Bonferroni-style adjustment across the reported tier × phase combinations. (a) is simpler and is what the §5 verdict structure already implies; making it a hard rule retires L3.3. (Layer 3 substantive.)

3. **Add a crash-drop sensitivity row to §4.11.1** — for the same-day Spearman of `stress_low_motion_min_count_S60_Mlow` vs `gevoelscore`, report ρ both on the full LC frame and with `is_crash == True` dropped, with `|Δ ρ| > 0.10` flagged per CONVENTIONS §3.4. This is a single row in the §4.11.1 output table; closes L4.4. (Layer 4 minor.)

4. **Tighten the §4.10 sensitivity ladder to 6 unique columns** — note the 3 `_Mbelow_mod` columns as identical-by-construction in the table caption rather than listing them as independent arms, per CONVENTIONS §3.3. The §8 caveat already engages this, so the §4.10 table just needs to match. Closes L4.3 cleanly. (Layer 4 minor.)

5. **Anchor the post-viz revision's cited values in the repo** — the Family A / Family D2a / Family B4a findings now materially shape §4.3 + §4.11, but the source notes live outside the audit-able tree. Either commit a sanitised summary (or pointer to a script + commit hash that reproduces the cited ρ = 0.79 and ρ = 0.556) into `docs/research/`, so a future fresh-session reviewer can verify those numbers without leaving the repo.

## 5. Verdict

**REVISION RECOMMENDED** — Layer 3 substantive: the null-sample machinery inherited from HA11/HA06b/HA10 predates the project-default block-permutation policy in [`permutation_null_block_length.md`](../methodology/permutation_null_block_length.md), which is neither cited nor applied; combined with implicit (rather than pre-registered) within-test multi-comparison discipline across the 9-column × 3-tier × 4-phase family, the §5 (b) discrimination margin is exposed to autocorrelation-driven inflation that the canonical project methodology already specifies the fix for.
