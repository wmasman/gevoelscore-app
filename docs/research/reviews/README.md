# Research reviews

**Status**: scaffolded 2026-06-13; merged with the older `review/`
folder on 2026-06-14.

This folder holds three related kinds of work:

1. **Peer-review reports** of reviewer-mode artefacts (HA results,
   synthesis docs, addenda) per [../CONVENTIONS.md §1.2](../CONVENTIONS.md).
   Filename convention: `<target-id>-YYYY-MM-DD.md`. The 4-layer
   checklist + verdict spec below applies to these. Generated by
   [`/research-review`](../../.claude/commands/research-review.md).
2. **Methodology-MD reviews** of producer-mode methodology MDs
   (`methodology/*.md`) against CONVENTIONS §2.2's four-input bar
   plus the applicable layer items. Filename convention:
   `methodology-<md-slug>-YYYY-MM-DD.md`. Generated by
   [`/research-methodology-review`](../../.claude/commands/research-methodology-review.md).
   The verdict tiers differ (DEFENSIBLE / DEFENSIBLE with revision /
   REVISIT) — see the command file for the §2.2-anchored spine.
3. **Research-to-app translation** documents — `app-plan.md`,
   `2026-06-07-research-to-app-methodology.md`, the independent-advisor
   strategy doc, and historical material in [`_archive/`](_archive/).
   These translate completed research into product / app planning.
   They are NOT peer reviews; the 4-layer checklist does not apply.

All three kinds live here because all three *review* the body of
research, in different senses: (1) audits a single artefact for
quality; (2) audits a single methodology choice against the
four-input bar; (3) reads the body of research and decides what to
build from it.

This folder is itself part of the project's research-discipline
methodology: the act of building a review checklist anchored in
established reporting standards — and explicitly adapted for the
gaps where those standards do not cover observational n=1
time-series — is the guardrail.

---

## Why this exists

Established single-subject reporting checklists exist (SCRIBE 2016,
CENT 2015, STROBE 2007), but none cleanly cover the shape of this
corpus. The 2023 Translational Psychiatry systematic review by
[Natesan Batley et al.](../literature/methodology/natesan_2023_nof1_evidence_reporting_systematic_review.pdf)
found that of 115 published n-of-1 medical articles, only 4 (3.48%)
met all WWC SCED evidence standards, and 83.8% ignored
autocorrelation. The combination of observational design + single
subject + temporally dependent time-series falls outside what any
single existing checklist was built for.

The review checklist used here adapts what transfers from
each established standard and adds project-specific audit hooks
from [../CONVENTIONS.md §3](../CONVENTIONS.md). Where a standard
does not transfer, this is stated rather than papered over.

## The 4-layer review checklist

Each review report walks four layers in order. Layer headers cite the
external standard the layer inherits from; per-item check lines cite
internal `CONVENTIONS.md` sections.

### Layer 1 — Universal reporting

Inherits from SCRIBE 2016 items 3-5, 14, 18, 22-24 ([Tate et al.,
PMC4873717](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4873717/)) and
STROBE 2007 items 6, 12, 13
([../literature/methodology/vonelm_2007_strobe_observational_checklist.pdf](../literature/methodology/vonelm_2007_strobe_observational_checklist.pdf)).
Pre-specification of hypothesis and analysis, defined measures with
source files, named confounders, stated limitations.

### Layer 2 — Observational n=1

Inherits from Daza 2018
([../literature/methodology/daza_2018_self_tracked_n_of_1_counterfactual.pdf](../literature/methodology/daza_2018_self_tracked_n_of_1_counterfactual.pdf))
and Personal Science norms (Wolf et al., Meth Inf Med 2017 focus
theme). Counterfactual framing, stationarity assumption, calendar-time
vs subject-time separation, data provenance traceability. Includes
the tactical-vs-analytical Garmin-use distinction per memory
`project_garmin_research_bias_boundary` for held-out-structure
framing.

### Layer 3 — Time-series specific

Inherits from the autocorrelation literature surveyed by
[Natesan Batley et al. 2023](../literature/methodology/natesan_2023_nof1_evidence_reporting_systematic_review.pdf)
(headline finding: 83.8% of n-of-1 medical studies ignore
autocorrelation), the WWC 2022 SCED standards
([../literature/methodology/wwc_2022_standards_handbook_v5_0.pdf](../literature/methodology/wwc_2022_standards_handbook_v5_0.pdf)),
and CENT 2015 carryover/ordering guidance
([../literature/methodology/shamseer_2015_cent_consort_nof1.pdf](../literature/methodology/shamseer_2015_cent_consort_nof1.pdf)).
Autocorrelation reporting, lag-carryover assumptions, multiple
testing across lags/channels with permutation null, named analysis
frame.

### Layer 4 — Project-specific audit hooks

Inherits from [../CONVENTIONS.md §3](../CONVENTIONS.md). Personal
baseline §3.1; `_lagged_lcera` variant §3.2; one column per
definitional pair §3.3; crash-drop sensitivity row §3.4;
spike-detecting metric §3.5; named counts §3.6; caveat-class vs
a-priori-class §4.2; prior-driven vs post-hoc §4.3.

Each layer item gets a check line that names the convention it
inherits from, states whether the convention fires for this
document, and (if it fires) cites the offending paragraph or column
reference.

## Verdict format

Three-tier:

- **PASS** — checklist clean, no Layer 4 audit hook fired, no
  framing concerns at Layer 2 or Layer 3.
- **PASS with caveats** — substantive findings hold, but at least one
  audit hook or framing item warrants disclosure in the result or
  synthesis doc itself.
- **REVISION RECOMMENDED** — at least one Layer 1-4 item indicates
  the result as stated may not survive the convention it inherits
  from.

The verdict label is never the final word. The mandatory body of
every report:

1. **What the data shows** — restate the result in plain terms,
   separating the empirical claim from the interpretive framing
   used by the original author.
2. **What fired and why** — for each Layer 1-4 item that fired, the
   inheritance citation, the specific reading of the document that
   triggered it, the magnitude of concern, and (where applicable)
   the convention's own statement of why it matters.
3. **What does not fire (selective)** — Layer items that explicitly
   pass with non-trivial evidence (e.g. autocorrelation is
   addressed via permutation null with documented block length).
   Skip trivially-passing items.
4. **What would strengthen this finding** — constructive close.
   Concrete, named, ideally pointing at an existing methodology MD
   or audit-script path. Not "consider X" but "the
   `[name]` MD already specifies the block length; cite it inline".
5. **Verdict + one-sentence reasoning** — the three-tier label, with
   the single-sentence reason. If REVISION RECOMMENDED, the reason
   names the layer the highest-priority concern came from.

A verdict without a body of this shape is not a verdict; it is a
label. The user feeds the body — not the label — back to the
producer agent.

## Filename convention

```
<target-id>-YYYY-MM-DD.md
```

Examples:

- `HA01b-2026-06-13.md` — review of `analyses/hypotheses/HA01b-*/result.md`
- `RESEARCH-REPORT-ADDENDUM-II-2026-06-15.md` — review of that addendum
- `wiggers-progress-2026-06-08-review-2026-06-13.md` — review of a
  dated progress doc (preserve original date + add review date)

The target-id matches the reviewed doc's stem; the date is the date
of review, not the date of the reviewed doc (unless the reviewed
doc is itself dated — then include both).

When the same target is reviewed more than once (e.g. after a
revision), each review gets its own dated file; do not overwrite.

## What this folder is NOT

- Not for **producer-mode** descriptive cards. Those live in
  `../analyses/` per the existing structure.
- Not for **methodology MDs**. Those live in `../methodology/` per
  [../CONVENTIONS.md §2.2](../CONVENTIONS.md).
- Not for **triage-instruction docs**. Those live in
  `../analyses/reviews/` (separate concept: instructions to the
  human reviewer of source data, not reports on hypothesis results).

If a review report needs a chart or computed sensitivity row that
isn't in the reviewed doc, the computation goes in
`../analyses/` (or `$GEVOELSCORE_DATA_PATH/analyses/`) and the
review report cites it. Reviews don't carry private artefacts.
