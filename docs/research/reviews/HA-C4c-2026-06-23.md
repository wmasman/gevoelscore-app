## 1. What the data shows

This is a pre-registration, not a result — there is no empirical claim *yet*. The doc commits to a single-cell substantive Wiggers C4 retest at bout-level resolution. The empirical question to be tested is whether the per-day count `bout_n_did_not_return_day` is systematically higher on heavy-T days than on non-heavy-T days within a stratum the doc names "cross-phase-pooled phase 5 + phase 6 (`pacing_habit_established` UNION `citalopram_modulated`)". Headline decision: SUPPORTED / PARTIAL / REJECTED / INCONCLUSIVE per the §5 bars (block-perm p < 0.05 AND Cliff's δ ≥ +0.20, both in the predicted positive direction). The interpretive framing carefully separates this from HA-C4 v2's daily-aggregate REJECTED verdict (a resolution-mismatch finding, not a supersession) and inherits four cascade-context caveats from HA11-bout-redo PARTIAL and the β-recalibration 0/7 CONFIRMED result.

## 2. What fired and why

### Layer 1 — Universal reporting (inherits from SCRIBE 2016, STROBE 2007)

**[L1.2 — Source-file traceability of operationalised measures; CONVENTIONS §2.1]** — **substantive**. §3 declares the citalopram-phase enum as `{unmedicated, buildup, consolidation, afbouw, post_afbouw}` (correct per [`citalopram_phase_stratification.md §3`](../methodology/citalopram_phase_stratification.md), function body lines 162-171). But §4.2 then re-declares the primary stratum as *"`citalopram_phase(d) ∈ {pacing_habit_established (phase 5), citalopram_modulated (phase 6)}`"*. Those two values are **not in the `citalopram_phase` enum**; they are values of `recovery_phase` per [`lc_recovery_phase_axis.md §3.4b + §3.5`](../methodology/lc_recovery_phase_axis.md). And the phase numbering is wrong on the recovery axis too: `pacing_habit_established` is sub-phase **4b**, not 5; `citalopram_modulated` is phase **5**, not 6. (`lc_recovery_phase_axis.md §3.5` explicitly says *"this MD does NOT add a phase 6"* — the post_afbouw sub-phase is currently out-of-corpus.) The same wrong column + wrong numbering propagates to §1, §6, §10.2, §10.4, and the §3.8 gate table. The verdict-running stratum is therefore not unambiguously specified by the pre-reg — a `test.py` writer would either (a) implement `citalopram_phase` membership against values that don't exist (returning empty), or (b) silently substitute `recovery_phase` and run on a *different* axis than `citalopram_phase` represents. This is a Layer-1 traceability fire at the load-bearing stratum-definition surface and must be resolved before lock.

### Layer 4 — Project-specific audit hooks (inherits from CONVENTIONS §3, §4)

**[L4.7 — Caveat-class vs a-priori-class framing; CONVENTIONS §4.2]** — **substantive**. The dispatcher brief flagged this as the load-bearing audit check (the recalibration audit verified the underpowered-NULL framing discipline at five surfaces; HA-C4c §8 must honor the same framing on Approach A). §8 caveat 3 reads: *"0/7 features CONFIRMED at this corpus's bout-level n; Approach A is NOT load-bearing → HA-C4c primary is dose-naive"* and frames Approach A as *"a pre-spec sensitivity rather than a load-bearing dose-correction"*. This is on the right side of the line at the headline. **But** the §4.9 inheritance-by-analogue framing slips: it states *"`bout_n_did_not_return_day` has prior direction +1 (more failures-to-return under elevated sympathetic tone) while `bout_n_fast_recovery_day` has prior direction −1; the inheritance template is `β_template = +0.056/mg` (sign-flipped to match the +1 prior direction; same magnitude)"*. The sub-MD §6 table gives `bout_n_fast_recovery_day` buildup-post-CPAP β = **−0.056 [−0.145, +0.034] p=0.223**, verdict **weakly_consistent**. The CI **crosses zero**; the p-value is well above 0.05; the sign-concordance with the −1 prior is the only reason it's not NULL. Using this CI-crosses-zero β as an inheritance template — and *flipping its sign by fiat to match a different feature's prior direction* — is the L4.7 a-priori-class move dressed as sensitivity arm. Two specific concerns:

1. **The sign-flip itself imports a +1 directional prior on `bout_n_did_not_return_day` that the recalibration never tested for this feature** (the feature wasn't in the recalibration scope). Calling this "inheritance-by-analogue" doesn't rescue it — the analogue β has prior-direction −1 in the recalibration, so its template's directional meaning is *"more plasma → fewer fast-recovery bouts"*. The fiat sign-flip is the assertion *"by analogy, more plasma → more did-not-return bouts"*, which is a-priori on `bout_n_did_not_return_day`. The recalibration audit's L4.7 discipline explicitly distinguishes *underpowered-NULL* ("we cannot demonstrate per-bout dose-modulation at this n") from *definitive dose-naivety*; the HA-C4c §4.9 framing leans on the analogue's *weakly-consistent* verdict-direction to import a magnitude AND a sign, both of which the recalibration treats with discount. §8 caveat 3 names this as "inheritance-by-analogue (NOT inheritance-by-recalibration on the operand itself)" — that wording is honest at §8 but the §4.9 derivation slips past it.

2. **The "sensitivity-of-verdict-to-CI-bounds" sub-arm at §4.9 paragraph 4** runs Approach A at the analogue β's CI lower bound (−0.145 → +0.145 after sign-flip) AND upper bound (+0.034 → −0.034 after sign-flip). Note that the upper-bound run *flips the directional adjustment* (the dose-adjustment subtracts a negative number on heavy-dose days, i.e., adds to the count). The result.md is supposed to surface this as a "β-precision-fragility finding" if the verdict flips across the CI range — but that's already informative *of nothing* because the analogue β CI crosses zero; the CI range is structurally a NULL-bracket. Re-running primary on a NULL-bracket template is descriptively defensible but the framing should match: this is **not** a dose-correction sensitivity arm in the recalibration's Approach A sense; it is a "what if Approach A were extended by analogy to a non-CONFIRMED β" descriptive companion. The §8 caveat 3 last sentence has the right reading (*"a pre-spec sensitivity rather than a load-bearing dose-correction"*), but §4.9 should state it inline at the derivation, not only at §8.

Magnitude: **substantive**. The verdict outcome on the primary cell is unaffected (the Approach A arm is sensitivity-only by §5.0 and cannot promote to SUPPORTED), so this does not block lock at the *verdict* level. But the **framing** of the §4.9 derivation is structurally vulnerable to the same a-priori-class drift the recalibration audit closed at five surfaces. A pre-lock revision absorbing the L4.7 framing — naming the inheritance-by-analogue as a *descriptive companion using the analogue β as a coefficient-of-convenience, not as a CONFIRMED dose-correction* — would honour the same discipline the recalibration audit established.

**[L4.4 — Crash-drop sensitivity row per CONVENTIONS §3.4]** — passes (§4.10 + Authorship "Locked decisions" item 5; threshold |Δ Cliff's δ| > 0.10 surfaces a §3.4 finding, > 0.20 with sign-flip routes primary to REJECTED). No fire.

**[L4.6 — Counts named with scheme + unit + source file]** — passes (§4.11 + Authorship "Locked decisions"; the per-arm `n_bouts` + per-cell `n_did_not_return_bouts × n_days × n_did_not_return_bouts_per_day` reporting discipline is explicit; CONVENTIONS §3.6 named-counts phrasing is shown). No fire.

## 4. What would strengthen this finding

Concrete, ordered by leverage:

1. **(L1.2 + side observations) Resolve the column-confusion at the primary stratum definition before lock.** The pre-reg currently aliases `recovery_phase` values to `citalopram_phase` and mis-numbers phases as 5 + 6 instead of 4b + 5. Either (a) rewrite §4.2 to specify the stratum on the correct axis: *"primary stratum = `recovery_phase(d) ∈ {pacing_habit_established (4b), citalopram_modulated (5)}`"*; or (b) specify the intersection explicitly: *"primary stratum = days where `recovery_phase(d) == citalopram_modulated` OR (`recovery_phase(d) == pacing_habit_established` AND `citalopram_phase(d) == unmedicated`)"*. The "unmedicated-only sensitivity arm" at §4.2 second paragraph similarly needs the intersection framing. Propagate the fix to §1, §6, §10.2, §10.4, and the §3.8 gate table. **Expected effect**: the pre-reg becomes unambiguously implementable; the `test.py` writer doesn't have to interpret which axis is meant; the stratum-membership the §4.7 n estimates assume is the stratum the verdict bars get applied to.

2. **(L4.7 §4.9 framing tighten) Rewrite §4.9 inheritance-by-analogue derivation to lead with the descriptive-companion framing.** Currently §4.9 leads with the mechanics of the sign-flip and the dose-adjustment formula, with the "weakly_consistent CI crosses zero" caveat appearing only at paragraph 3. Lead instead with: *"Because `bout_n_did_not_return_day` was not in the recalibration's 7-feature scope, no per-feature β is available. The closest-analogue β (`bout_n_fast_recovery_day` buildup-post-CPAP) carries a weakly_consistent verdict with a CI that crosses zero (β=−0.056 [−0.145, +0.034] p=0.223). Per the recalibration §6 architectural-implications + the underpowered-NULL framing the recalibration audit (`reviews/bout_level_dose_response_calibration-r3-2026-06-22.md` §3) preserved at five surfaces, no inheritance is load-bearing at this corpus's bout-level n. This sensitivity arm uses the analogue β as a coefficient-of-convenience for a descriptive companion; it is NOT a dose-correction with statistical warrant on `bout_n_did_not_return_day` specifically. The fiat sign-flip imports a +1 directional prior consistent with the substantive Wiggers C4 reading but unproven for this feature."* Then proceed to the mechanics. **Expected effect**: closes the L4.7 framing slip; aligns the §4.9 derivation with the §8 caveat 3 framing; honours the recalibration-audit's five-surface framing discipline at the HA-C4c surface. Does not change any verdict.

3. **(L3.3 Holm family-size disclosure) Add a §5.3 (or §4.6) one-sentence note** stating: *"If the motion-clean-only sensitivity arm is INCONCLUSIVE (anticipated per §8 caveat 4 + the 99.3% finding), the Holm family-size shrinks to 3 cells (primary + unmedicated-only + transient-excluded); the cutoffs become α/3, α/2, α/1."* Inherits the disclosure pattern from HA-C4 v2 §5.5 r2 fewer-comparisons annotation (`reviews/HA-C4-v2-2026-06-18.md` side observation 2 absorb). **Expected effect**: closes the L3.3 reporting-discipline minor; result.md reader can audit the Holm step-down under the actually-realised family-size without re-deriving it.

4. **(Mechanical absorb opportunity) §4.7 "Expected n per arm"** uses STOCKTAKE §6's "~70 day-clusters" gain estimate as the basis for "~317 heavy-T / ~569 non-heavy-T". The figure inherits the dose-naivety + cross-phase-pooling-permission chain from STOCKTAKE §6 cleanly, but the STOCKTAKE §6 estimate is itself a rough gain figure — pre-reg should either replicate the estimate inline (citalopram_modulated days × heavy-T fraction) OR cite the precise STOCKTAKE §6 row, so the §4.7 walk-forward gate evaluation at dry-run time is not relying on a chain of inheritances. Mechanical r2 absorb opportunity if substantive.

5. **(Optional clarification) §6 last bullet** reads *"Unmedicated-stratum restriction NOT REQUIRED for HA-C4c primary per parent MD §6 — that restriction is the framework-validity gate's scope (HA11-bout-redo)"*. The reader benefit would be a one-clause companion: *"...and the cross-phase pooling permission for substantive (not framework-validity) tests on bout-level operands at this corpus's n derives from recalibration §6 + STOCKTAKE §6 architectural-implications paragraph, conditional on the underpowered-NULL framing."* Closes the chain explicitly at §6.

The four items above are all **mechanical-or-framing r2 absorb scope** per `hypothesis_lock_process.md §3.6` compression criteria. None change the §5 verdict bars or the single-cell headline lock. Item 1 is structurally blocking; items 2-5 are precision improvements within the structural frame.
