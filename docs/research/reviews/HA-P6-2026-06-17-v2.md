## 2. What fired and why

### Layer 1 — Universal reporting (inherits from SCRIBE 2016, STROBE 2007)

**[L1.3 — analysis method named with parameters, minor — override magnitude pre-registered as a rule, not a number]**
The v2 §4.8.1 override table for the two FAIL-expected channels specifies the override as `E[L] = round(E[L]*)` where `E[L]*` is the pooled-LC interpretation-(ii) estimate computed at the dry-run. The override RULE is pre-registered at v2 lock; the override MAGNITUDE is data-determined at the next dry-run. The [`methodology/permutation_null_block_length.md`](../methodology/permutation_null_block_length.md) "Decision (proposed; pending user review)" override clause requires "(ii) override is pre-registered in the hypothesis file before any test run" and "(iii) justified in the hypothesis file with a 1-paragraph ACF readout." A strict reading wants both the override magnitude AND the ACF readout in `hypothesis.md`; v2's split (rule + rationale in `hypothesis.md`, magnitude + readout in `result.md` at the test session) is defensible — the rule is deterministic and pre-committed, the magnitude is mechanically derivable from the estimator's output — but stretches the MD's plain reading. **Minor** because the deterministic rule + the §4.8.1 cross-reference paragraph + the §8 v2 caveat together carry the intent of the override clause; closure is one sentence acknowledging the split-compliance reading.

**[L1.3 — analysis method named with parameters, minor — override magnitude has no upper bound]**
The §4.8.1 override table uses `round(E[L]*)` with no documented cap on the upper end. The v1 dry-run under interpretation (i) produced `E[L]* = 30.72` for `stress_low_motion_min_count_S60_Mlow`; under interpretation (ii) the magnitude may be different but is still unbounded by the spec. With n=29 episodes and an override E[L] in the tens, the effective bootstrap sample size for the per-day median CI collapses (geometric block lengths with E[L]=30 on a 5-day window means most resamples are dominated by a single block). The v2 §8 caveat acknowledges "honest CI inflation > false-conservative E[L]=7" — which is true at moderate inflation — but the spec doesn't bound the override at a maximum where the CIs become uninformative. **Minor** — a one-line cap (e.g. `min(round(E[L]*), 21)` to keep the resampled-block fraction of the corpus < 50%) would close this; absent the cap, the result.md should surface the effective resampling-resolution per FAIL-channel.

**[L1.3 — analysis method named with parameters, minor — §9 head asserts paired-bootstrap machinery not explicitly described in §4.8.1]**
§9 head adds: *"per channel, the per-day block-bootstrap 95% CI on the median DIFFERENCE (crash trajectory minus matched-control trajectory, computed via the §4.8.1 paired stationary-bootstrap machinery at the per-channel E[L] from §4.8.1, B=10000) excludes 0 on ≥ 2 of 5 primary-window days"*. §4.8.1 describes per-day median + IQR + per-day CIs on the median per channel × phase × baseline-arm cell, but does not explicitly describe a PAIRED bootstrap for the crash-minus-matched-control difference (paired in the sense of resampling crash episodes and their matched controls together). Two plausible operationalisations exist (paired by episode index within the same resample vs independent per-day median CIs subtracted), and the spec doesn't name which. **Minor** — closure is one sentence in §4.8.1 specifying that the §9 trigger uses paired-by-episode resampling (the matched-control trajectory for episode `i` resamples in the same block as the crash trajectory for episode `i`).

### Layer 2 — Observational n=1 (inherits from Daza 2018, Personal Science)

**[L2.2 — stationarity assumption checked or acknowledged, substantive — pooled-LC E[L]* under interpretation (ii) inherits intervention-induced level-shift contamination]**
v2 §4.8.1 commits the pooled-LC E[L]* computation to "no same-phase restriction for the pooled-LC headline E[L]*" with rationale "(the headline is pooled)." The pooled-LC daily time series spans 2022-04-04 → 2026-06-05 (~4 years), crossing the CPAP-start boundary (2024-04-09), Citalopram buildup (2024-04-30 → 2024-06-19), 30mg plateau (2024-06-20 → 2026-03-19), and afbouw (2026-03-20 → 2026-06-05). [intervention_effects_descriptive.md](../methodology/intervention_effects_descriptive.md) documents multiple confirmed level shifts on the autonomic-load channels across these transitions (β=+0.57/mg for `all_day_stress_avg`; β=+0.43/mg for `stress_mean_sleep`; β=-1.13/mg for `bb_lowest`). The data-driven block-length estimator computes the empirical ACF of the input series; on a non-stationary series with intervention-induced level shifts, the ACF will show spurious long-range correlation that reflects the level shifts rather than the within-phase autocorrelation structure. This is the methodologically distinct (but mechanically equivalent) flavour of artefact that interpretation (i)'s concat boundaries produced — interpretation (ii) replaces "fake lag-1 discontinuities at episode-window edges" with "real long-range correlation driven by intervention transitions." The v2 spec commits to a per-phase E[L]* as a diagnostic at §4.8.1 ("Per-phase E[L]\* is also computed as a diagnostic with same-phase restriction added") but explicitly excludes per-phase E[L]* from the §7 override policy (the override "operates on the pooled-LC E[L]\* per channel only"). **Substantive** — the same channels that triggered v1's HALT (`all_day_stress_avg`, `stress_low_motion_min_count_S60_Mlow`) are precisely the channels with the strongest documented dose-response, so their pooled-LC ACF will inherit the strongest level-shift contamination. v2 will likely still produce FAIL on these two channels under interpretation (ii), and the spec's pre-spec'd override absorbs that — but the FAIL on these specific channels will be at least partly an artefact of pooled non-stationarity, not a clean reading of within-phase autocorrelation. The four "PASS-fallback expected" channels (`bb_lowest`, `bb_overnight_gain`, `resting_hr`, `gevoelscore`) inherit the same risk in the opposite direction: their pooled E[L]* may end up artefactually inflated (PASS-fallback "all lags significant" again) for the same reason. Closure is either (a) compute the headline E[L]* on a single low-confounding stratum (e.g. unmedicated only, 2022-04-04 → 2024-04-08) and use the result as the cross-cell binding number per channel, with the pooled-LC E[L]* relegated to diagnostic-only, or (b) explicitly acknowledge the contamination in §8 and report per-phase E[L]* alongside the pooled headline as a sensitivity arm.

### Layer 3 — Time-series specific (inherits from Natesan Batley 2023, WWC 2022, CENT 2015)

**[L3.4 — permutation null block length documented, substantive — PASS-fallback discipline is thinner than PASS-real or FAIL]**
v2 §7 three-verdict policy treats PASS-fallback ("estimator could not compute; e.g. ACF inconclusive, closed-form degenerate") as proceed-with-default-E[L]=7 plus a methodological flag in result.md. The v1 dry-run produced PASS-fallback for four of seven channels under interpretation (i); the notes were:

| channel | v1 (i)-interpretation note |
|---|---|
| `bb_lowest` | "No clear ACF cutoff (all lags within max_lag are significant); returning default" |
| `bb_overnight_gain` | "Closed-form formula degenerate; returning default" |
| `resting_hr` | "No clear ACF cutoff (all lags within max_lag are significant); returning default" |
| `gevoelscore` | "Closed-form formula degenerate; returning default" |

"No clear ACF cutoff (all lags significant)" is a positive signal of LONG dependence, not a neutral fallback — proceeding with E[L]=7 in that situation is structurally false-conservative (CIs too narrow because the resampling pretends the dependence ends at 7 days when the empirical ACF says it doesn't). v2's hypothesis is that interpretation (ii) will resolve this (the daily series ACF "should still be cleanly within range"), but the spec doesn't pre-spec what happens if interpretation (ii) still degenerates: §7 + §4.8.1 simply repeat the "proceed with E[L]=7 + flag" disposition. The result is that under interpretation (ii)-still-degenerate, four channels' per-day CIs would silently use a default that the estimator's diagnostic explicitly contradicted, and the only audit trail is the `note` field surfacing in result.md. Per the [permutation_null_block_length.md "Decision"](../methodology/permutation_null_block_length.md) clause "If E[L]\* differs from 7 by more than a factor of 2, flag for review before locking the verdict" — the "ACF doesn't cleanly cut off" diagnostic is a stronger-than-factor-of-2 signal in the long direction, but the v2 logic does not promote that to a FAIL-equivalent override. **Substantive** — the v1 HALT was partly because the v1 sanity policy treated fallback-returned-default as equivalent to verified-default; the v2 spec refines the FAIL side (real-value-outside-range) but inherits a structurally similar disposition on the PASS-fallback side. Closure is either (a) extend the per-channel override pre-spec to cover PASS-fallback channels (e.g. on "all lags significant" the override defaults to a conservative E[L] = 14 with explicit rationale), or (b) treat PASS-fallback with the "all lags significant" note as a structural FAIL that halts to v3, while keeping PASS-fallback with "closed-form degenerate" or "n<30" notes as proceed-with-default.

**[L3.4 — permutation null block length documented, minor — FAIL gate uses [3.5, 10.5] vs the MD's example [3, 14]]**
The methodology MD's override clause condition (i) gives the example "ACF crosses zero at a lag substantially different from 7 (e.g. < 3 or > 14 days at lag |corr| < 0.1)." v2 operationalises "substantially different from 7" as `|E[L]*-7|/7 > 0.5`, which yields `E[L]* < 3.5 OR E[L]* > 10.5`. The v2 gate is tighter than the MD's example on both ends — it catches `E[L]*=12` as FAIL where the MD's example would not. The MD's range is given as "e.g." (example, not binding), so v2's choice is within the spirit, and the factor-of-2 framing aligns with the MD's earlier "If E[L]\* differs from 7 by more than a factor of 2, flag for review." But the two thresholds in the MD ("factor of 2" → `[3.5, 10.5]` vs "< 3 or > 14" → `[3, 14]`) are themselves slightly inconsistent and v2 picks the tighter reading without flagging the choice. **Minor** — closure is one sentence in §4.8.1 acknowledging which of the MD's two ranges v2 binds to and why.

**[L3.4 — permutation null block length documented, minor — interpretation (ii) eligible-day filter deviates from the MD's "Stratum 4 days"]**
The methodology MD's override clause condition (i) specifies the ACF is "computed over Stratum 4 days, see lc_era_temporal_segmentation.md." Stratum 4 per [`lc_era_temporal_segmentation.md` §1-§2](../methodology/lc_era_temporal_segmentation.md) is "LC with gevoelscore + crash labels" — i.e. the full LC era including crash days. v2's interpretation (ii) filter is "LC era only, non-crash days only, no same-phase restriction." Excluding crash days is sensible (you don't want crash-day spikes to dominate the ACF estimate of the inter-crash trajectory) but technically deviates from the MD's plain reading. **Minor** — the deviation is in the conservative direction (filtering out the most anomalous days), and the v2 §4.8.1 paragraph names interpretation (ii) explicitly enough that the deviation is auditable; closure is one sentence in §4.8.1 acknowledging the non-crash filter as an addition to the MD's "Stratum 4 days" baseline.
