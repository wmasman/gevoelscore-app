## 1. What the MD specifies

The MD is an **operand definition** — a Layer-1 descriptive-framing methodology MD — for Q24 sub-parts 1 (day-after activity), 4 (sleep + autonomic), and a newly added subjective sub-part. It locks: LC-era stratum (`lc_phase == 'lc'`); the inherited HA-C4c heavy-day trigger (`exertion_class_lagged_lcera ∈ {heavy, very_heavy}`); episode-end (gap=0) as primary unit with per-heavy-day as sensitivity; a matched-ordinary comparator (no-heavy in `[D, D+w]` + no-crash in `[D, D+w]` + per-outcome valid-data filter); a window ladder of +3d / +5d primary + +10d extended (+14d dropped at n=5); both strict-clean and inclusive overlap policies reported side-by-side; a crash-adjacency compensatory-success vs compensatory-failure pool split (§3.5, user-endorsed pre-lock); five primary autonomic outcome channels plus six sensitivity sleep operands cross-referencing `sleep_metrics.md`; nine trajectory summary statistics per (outcome, window, overlap, stratum) cell including a peak-based decay operationalisation `|Δ(w)|/|Δ(k*)| < 0.5` (§8.1, user-endorsed pre-lock); a pre-committed four-branch two-clock decision tree run **per autonomic channel** (§8.2, user-endorsed pre-lock — 5 channels × 4 branches at each primary window); an intensity-stratified sensitivity arm (combined / very_heavy_only / heavy_only); seven pre-committed caveats propagated from Stage -1 audit §6.7; and a Zero-vs-NaN discipline inherited from `bout_level_recovery_dynamics.md` §11. ## 2. What fired and why

### Spine — §2.2 four-input bar (inherits from CONVENTIONS §2.2)

#### I1 — Best-practices standards

- **I1.1 — State-of-art for pre-vs-post window comparison around a documented trigger not named [substantive].** The MD locks a matched-ordinary comparator design around trigger events (heavy episode-ends) with a symmetric no-heavy filter. This is the shape of an **interrupted time-series (ITS)** design applied event-wise: the *pre*-side collapses into the matched-ordinary pool and the *post*-side is the per-episode trajectory. **Bernal, Cummins, Gasparrini 2017 (*Int J Epidemiol* — Cochrane EPOC ITS guidance)** is the state-of-art anchor for this class of analysis (level β2 + trend β3 in a segmented regression). The MD does not name ITS as the state-of-art nor state why the matched-ordinary contrast is the deliberate descriptive simplification chosen over segmented regression. Silence, not rejection-with-reason — this is the I1.2 failure mode. **Sister methodology MD `intervention_effects_descriptive.md`** already handles ITS-adjacent comparisons and the §3.7 detrend hook; a one-sentence forward-cross-ref to that MD naming ITS as the state-of-art being deliberately descriptively simplified would close the fire.

- **I1.2 — Trajectory-summary statistic menu not framed as a menu-choice with reason [minor].** §7 locks nine trajectory summary stats (per-day means + CIs, mean-difference vector, AUC, slope, peak-location, RTBT, below-baseline count, variability, first-crossing). These are all reasonable per-day / integrated / dispersion characterisations, but nine is a lot and there is no forward statement that this is a *pre-committed pool the analyst reads together* vs *nine independent tests the analyst picks the best of*. The multiplicity implication is silent at §7 (see B6 fire below). A one-line pre-commit — "these nine stats are read jointly per (outcome, window, overlap, stratum) cell as an ensemble characterisation; the primary Stage-D read is per-day mean ± CI + the peak-based decay verdict per §8.1; the other seven are companion characterisations, not independent falsifiers" — would clarify the intended usage.

- **I1.3 — Peak-based decay screen `|Δ(w)|/|Δ(k*)| < 0.5` at n=52-125 is a plausible choice but not anchored to a published anchor [minor].** §8.1's rationale for the 0.5 threshold ("at least half the peak divergence has recovered by window end") is well-argued from first principles and the user-endorsement note is transparent, but no published decay-quantification method is named — nor rejected. In sports-science recovery literature the analogous concepts are recovery-percentage-to-baseline (typically 50 / 63 / 90) and half-life fitting; **Buchheit 2013** (already cited in the target's §8 preamble for the two-clock construct) is the closest project-adjacent anchor, and **Stanley/Peake/Buchheit 2013** on HRV recovery half-life is the canonical operationalisation of "decay to half-peak" in the exercise-physiology tradition. Naming "half-life-of-perturbation" as the framing paradigm, even briefly, would connect the corpus-specific `|Δ(w)|/|Δ(k*)| < 0.5` operationalisation to the established literature framing.

This is the §2.2 "each citation gets a sentence on what it contributes" bar cleared with room to spare.

- **I2.2 — Chu 2018 anchor for peak-based decay is material [pass, worth noting].** §8.1's justification of peak-based over window-start-based normalisation because Chu 2018 documents 24-72h onset lag is a *substantively* material citation — the paper's evidence directly supports the operationalisation choice. Not ornamental. Good example of §2.2 discipline.

- **I2.3 — Buchheit 2013 cited for "two-clock" existence but not for the decay-percentage anchor [minor].** Buchheit 2013 is invoked at the §8 preamble ("channel-specific recovery clocks") for the two-clock motivation. The paper is also directly relevant to the decay-percentage operationalisation itself (perceptual vs physical acclimatisation on different time-courses with quantified return-to-baseline windows). A second explicit cite at §8.1 for "the 0.5 threshold is the sports-science-standard decay-halfway point per Buchheit's tradition" would close I1.3 and further material the Buchheit anchor.

#### I4 — Research limitations + objectives

- **I4.1 — n=1 + single-subject acknowledged [pass].** §4.3 explicitly cites CONVENTIONS §3.1 personal-baseline discipline and rejects propensity matching on n=1 grounds; §5.1 flags extended-window CI-only reads; §9.2 pins the intensity-stratified sample-size floor with explicit descriptive-with-CI-only tagging at n=19.

- **I4.5 — Retrospective coding acknowledged as a caveat [minor].** The MD does not directly acknowledge that the heavy-day class labels (`exertion_class_lagged_lcera`) are computed retrospectively from a `[d-90, d-30]` reference window, which means the participant's *tactical* pacing responses during the trigger day were not made against the same class label the analytical read uses. This is a soft caveat — not load-bearing since the analytical framing is *post-hoc descriptive*, but worth one sentence acknowledging that the class label is analytical, not experienced-in-real-time.

### Layer 2 — Observational n=1 (inherits from Daza 2018)

### Layer 3 — Time-series specific (inherits from Natesan Batley 2023, WWC 2022, CENT 2015)

- **L3.1 — Autocorrelation [substantive].** The nine trajectory summary stats (§7) do not explicitly name autocorrelation handling. The bootstrap CIs in §7.1 / §7.2 / §7.3 are referred to as "block bootstrap per §7.9", but §7.9 is actually the first-crossing-day metric — not a block-bootstrap spec. The MD lacks an explicit reference to `permutation_null_block_length.md` for the block length (the parent MD's E[L]=7 anchor). At n=125 episode-ends across ~4 years of LC-era, day-level autocorrelation between episode-ends is likely small (episodes are typically weeks apart), BUT the *within-window* trajectory-difference vector `Δ(k)` is autocorrelated across k by construction (consecutive days share physiological state). The bootstrap CI on `Δ(k)` needs a block scheme that respects within-trajectory autocorrelation. **This is a §7 specification gap** that should be closed pre-lock. The parent MD's day-level E[L]=7 stationary-bootstrap anchor per `permutation_null_block_length.md` may or may not be the right unit for the trajectory-level analysis; this needs an explicit call.

- **L3.2 — Trajectory-detrend sensitivity per CONVENTIONS §3.7 [substantive — HIGHEST PRIORITY LAYER 3 FIRE].** As flagged under I4.2, this MD's primary trajectory reads are *raw pre-vs-post-style comparisons on the LC frame* in exactly the §3.7 audit-hook sense. The matched-ordinary comparator draws from the same LC-era stratum so the concurrent-control partially handles cross-year drift, but the heavy-vs-ordinary contrast magnitude may itself drift as the participant's overall envelope contracts. CONVENTIONS §3.7 explicitly names raw comparisons on the LC frame without a detrended sensitivity column as a fire. The sister `intervention_effects_descriptive.md` MD's `linear_detrend_on_pre` helper produced dramatic real revisions to two findings in Session C (`stress_mean_sleep` × 2022-09-22 raw p<.001 → detrend p=0.62; `bb_overnight_gain` × 2026-03-20 raw p<.001 → detrend p=0.96). **Recommendation**: add a §7.10 or §5.4 subsection specifying a detrended-Δ(k) sensitivity column — fit a linear trend on the pre-window matched-ordinary comparator pool and subtract it from both heavy and ordinary arms. This is inheritable verbatim from `intervention_effects_descriptive.md`.

- **L3.3 — Multiplicity [substantive].** §8.2 reporting matrix explicitly names "5 channels × 2 primary windows = 10 rows at primary; 5 additional rows at extended +10d = 15 total branch verdicts" — plus 1 subjective decay screen per window per pool. §6.2.1 sensitivity table adds 6 more sleep-sensitivity operands per window. Plus 9 trajectory summary stats per operand. The multiplicity surface is large (single-digit multiples per channel × ~15 primary operands × 2-3 windows × 2 overlap policies × 2 pools = order-of-hundreds of cells). The MD invokes multiplicity discipline once ("multiplicity-correction across the family is a Stage-D pre-reg decision" §6.1) but does not pre-commit the pattern the parent MD `bout_level_recovery_dynamics.md` §5.2 uses (single-cell headline + Holm step-down companions), which the target explicitly names as "the project-canonical default". §8.2's per-channel discipline explicitly says "no interpretive overhead" for the 5×4 = 20-cell decay matrix, but the multiplicity surface across the *combined* §6.2 sleep-autonomic × §6.3 subjective × §7 summary-stat × §8 decision-tree apparatus is not surfaced anywhere. **Recommendation**: add a §7.10 or §12 subsection stating "the multiplicity policy across the (outcome × window × overlap × stratum) grid is deferred to Stage D but the project-canonical default (single-cell headline lock per outcome-family + Holm step-down across companions, per `bout_level_recovery_dynamics.md` §5.2) is the starting pattern; departures require explicit justification in the Stage D report." One-line pre-commit closes the fire.

- **L3.4 — Level vs trend [pass, partial].** §7.4 slope is an explicit trend descriptor and §7.1 per-day mean is the level descriptor — the MD does distinguish level from trend within the trajectory. The larger CONVENTIONS §3.7 concern (level shift vs slope inflection at the cross-year scale) is the L3.2 fire above; within the trajectory the level-vs-trend split is handled.

- **L3.5 — State-of-art for the specific time-series question [minor].** As noted in I1.1, ITS with segmented regression is the discipline-standard for event-anchored longitudinal comparisons and is not named. Nor rejected. The Layer-1-descriptive framing partially absolves this (I1.4 note in the skill file: "the four-input bar is lower for a descriptive MD but not zero"). Naming ITS as the framework being deliberately descriptively simplified — with a forward-cross-ref to `intervention_effects_descriptive.md` — would close.

## 4. What would strengthen this MD

Concrete revisions, each named + rationale + expected effect:
