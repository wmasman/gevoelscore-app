# Review: Testable hypotheses from the Wiggers smartwatch-pacing handleiding (wiggers_testable_hypotheses)

**Target**: [../wiggers_testable_hypotheses.md](../wiggers_testable_hypotheses.md)
**Target commit**: `5577492` (2026-06-12) with uncommitted working-tree changes dated 2026-06-13 (Priority shortlist section, B-block reclassification, H4 reclassification, batch-3 verification log).

## 1. What the data shows

The doc is a **hypothesis register / pre-registration draft** distilled from the Wiggers patient-authored smartwatch-pacing handleiding (07-2025), operationalised against this project's `per_day_master.csv`. The empirical claims are Wiggers' (or her cited sources, e.g. Flack on the +15-20 bpm threshold), each grounded in verbatim PDF-line citations. The doc's own contribution is the operational column-selection layer + a tiering of which hypotheses are pre-reg-ready (23 testable now, 11 partial via the `stress_mean_sleep` HRV-proxy, 2 hardware-blocked) and the Priority Shortlist (Tier 1: B1, C3, A1, C4; Tier 2: A4, H5; Tier 3: G3, H1, H4).

Two things are mixed in the doc and worth separating: (a) the source-verified Wiggers claims themselves, and (b) the operational column choices the project made to test them. The verification log (batches 1-3) keeps (a) cleanly grounded; (b) is where the framing concerns surfaced below sit.

## 2. What fired and why

### Layer 1 — Universal reporting (inherits from SCRIBE 2016, STROBE 2007)

- **[L1.3 — CONVENTIONS §2.2 — minor]** — Some test methods are coarse for a pre-reg draft. A2: "correlation; allow both-direction deviation". B3: "rolling-window correlation". F1: "peri-event window". These don't name the correlation family (Spearman vs Pearson), the alpha, or the primary vs secondary status. Acceptable for a *draft* (the doc itself says formal pre-regs get their own locked spec) — but the verification log already names the specific test for A1 (Jonckheere-Terpstra), C3 (binned mean / natural spline) and C4 (stratified comparison). Inconsistency: high-priority hypotheses are precise, lower-priority ones are coarse, which is reasonable, but the precision boundary isn't called out.

### Layer 2 — Observational n=1 (inherits from Daza 2018, Personal Science)

- **[L2.1 — CONVENTIONS §5 — substantive]** — Counterfactual framing not explicit. The doc operationally implements within-subject counterfactual reasoning ("deviation = value minus your own rolling personal baseline", line 13) but never states the framing in Daza's terms ("on day d at exposure X, comparison is to subject-baseline at exposure ~X, not to a population"). Why this matters: Daza 2018 (the only published framework directly aimed at observational self-tracked n=1 time series, [../literature/methodology/daza_2018_self_tracked_n_of_1_counterfactual.pdf](../literature/methodology/daza_2018_self_tracked_n_of_1_counterfactual.pdf)) makes the counterfactual explicit so downstream readers can evaluate whether the comparison is well-defined. Implicit framing works for a register where the analyst is also the subject; it travels less well when the doc is the public-facing reference (as it is — `wiggers_response_article_*.md` cites it back to the advisor).

- **[L2.5 — CONVENTIONS §5; memory `project_garmin_research_bias_boundary` — substantive]** — Held-out structure framing inherits a now-superseded framing from [`../methodology/train_validate_split_fate.md`](../methodology/train_validate_split_fate.md) via two references (lines 250, 351-357). The doc says: "Optional M3 descriptive overlay (train-era vs validate-era discrimination) may be reported but cannot claim per-portion verdicts; under the overlay, train-vs-validate divergence is a number, not a narrative." That sentence is *correct* under the new memory. But the doc does NOT surface the tactical-vs-analytical Garmin distinction that motivates *why* "a number, not a narrative" is the right framing. A reader new to the project (e.g. Wiggers herself in a response cycle) would not learn from this doc that pre-2026 validate eras are held-out from analytical eyes in the strict research sense, while still being lived-through and tactically Garmin-paced. The doc just collapses everything into "full Stratum 4 single pool" framing. Why this matters: the new memory was created precisely to prevent regression to "user has been continuously analysing the data" framing, and the parent doc this register cites (`train_validate_split_fate.md`) still uses that language (e.g. lines 92-93: "user has been looking at post-2023-12-31 data continuously since it accrued") which the user has flagged as needing correction. The register inherits the framing by reference.

- **[L2.2 — CONVENTIONS §5 — minor]** — Stationarity is addressed for H1/H5 specifically ("Stationarity: first-difference series before CCF", lines 304, 308) but not named explicitly for the rolling-baseline tests in B/C/D blocks. The 28-day rolling baseline implicitly handles slow non-stationarity, but the doc doesn't say so. Minor; the operational implementation likely covers it.

### Layer 3 — Time-series specific (inherits from Natesan Batley 2023, WWC 2022, CENT 2015)

No fires. The doc explicitly addresses autocorrelation ("Days are autocorrelated — don't treat them as independent samples. Use block bootstrap or model the autocorrelation explicitly", line 47), cites a dedicated methodology MD for the block length ([`../methodology/permutation_null_block_length.md`](../methodology/permutation_null_block_length.md)), names multiple-comparisons correction (Holm step-down on N_eff ≈ 4 per `wiggers_test_design_on_chained_regime.md` § Cross-cutting statistical hygiene), and separates trend claims (B3, E2) from level claims (D3) cleanly. This is the layer the Natesan Batley 2023 systematic review found 83.8% of n-of-1 medical studies failed on; this doc passes it cleanly with documented machinery.

### Layer 4 — Project-specific audit hooks (inherits from CONVENTIONS §3, §4)

- **[L4.2 — CONVENTIONS §3.2 — substantive]** — The Column-choice-for-`per_day_master.csv` table at lines 17-37 lists `_lagged` (non-lcera) variants for PEM hypotheses: `exertion_rank_composite_lagged`, `exertion_class_lagged`, `step_rank_lagged`, `eff_exertion_rank_lagged`, `max_hr_rank_lagged`, `vigorous_min_rank_lagged`, `push_burden_7d_lagged`. This contradicts the doc's own pre-reg-draft default stated at lines 210-212: *"exertion proxy defaults to `exertion_class_lagged_lcera` (PEM) or `exertion_rank_composite_lagged_lcera` (continuous) per the use-lagged-exertion-for-pem memory."* The pre-reg-draft variable mapping (lines 218-308) then correctly uses `_lagged_lcera` everywhere (A1, A4, B4, C4, D5, H4, H5). Net effect: a reader who consults the upper Column-choice table for a fresh hypothesis (which is what that table is *for*) will pick the wrong variant for the PEM frame. The fix is mechanical (suffix the upper table's columns with `_lcera`, or add an "or `_lcera` for PEM" qualifier per column) but the inconsistency is internally load-bearing — `_lagged` baselines on the LC frame mix in pre-LC and corona-infection days, which is exactly the contamination CONVENTIONS §3.2 was added to prevent.

- **[L4.3 — CONVENTIONS §3.3 — substantive]** — The A4 column infrastructure row at line 37 lists BOTH `hr_min_above_daytime_baseline_plus_20_waking` AND `hr_area_above_daytime_baseline_waking`. Per CONVENTIONS §3.3 these are the ρ=1.000 near-identical pair (annotated inline in [DATA_DICTIONARY.md](../DATA_DICTIONARY.md)); only one should be picked for any downstream model. The A4 pre-reg draft at line 221 picks correctly (`hr_longest_elevated_run_min_waking_lcera` + `hr_area_above_daytime_baseline_waking_lcera`, which are distinct constructs), so the bug is confined to the upper column-infrastructure row, not the pre-reg. Same fix shape as L4.2 — surface the pair guardrail at the column-choice layer, not just at the pre-reg-draft layer.

- **[L4.4 — CONVENTIONS §3.4 — substantive]** — Crash-drop sensitivity rows are required for every Layer 4+ correlation / CCF / regression that touches PEM-pacing variables. The doc cites this only for G3 (Tier 3, line 166): *"run crash-distortion sensitivity row per [[feedback_crash_distortion_sensitivity]]"*. But Tier 1 (A1 dose-response, C3 non-linear stress→fatigue) and Tier 2 (H5 CCF lag-profile) are exactly the Layer 4+ tests where the §3.4 finding (dropping `is_crash==True` rows inflates the exertion × resting_hr Spearman from ~+0.0 to ~+0.4) bites hardest. Why this matters: A1's primary test is a Jonckheere-Terpstra on `resting_hr` deviation stratified by `exertion_rank_composite_lagged_lcera` quartile. That test is structurally susceptible to the crash-distortion sensitivity finding — on crash days the exertion-HR co-movement is suppressed, so leaving crash days in dampens the dose-response signal. The pre-reg-draft constraints (lines 246-253, 344-367) list 6 explicit B-block constraints but don't include crash-drop sensitivity. Per CONVENTIONS this is mandatory at Layer 4+, not optional per-tier.

- **[L4.5 — CONVENTIONS §3.5; memory `project_crash_triggers_user_experience` — minor]** — Some hypotheses use average-based metrics for claims about acute episodes. `stress_mean_sleep` is justified by the HRV-proxy framing (Firstbeat collapses to HRV-derived during sleep, lines 224-236) so the sleep-mean is defensible. But C1's primary (line 259) uses `stress_mean_sleep` for "night orange high stress" — Wiggers' "orange" is by Garmin's own binning a per-minute category, so a duration metric (`stress_high_duration_min` during waking, which the doc lists as secondary) more directly tests the Wiggers claim. The proxy framing requires sleep MEAN; the *waking* analogue could be a spike-detecting variant. Worth flagging in the formal C1 pre-reg.

- **[L4.6 — CONVENTIONS §3.6 — minor]** — Counts mostly OK because the doc IS its own source (the 23/11/2 summary table). One soft fire: "~40 hypotheses" (line 46) and "1755 rows × 161 cols" (line 199) lack a date or commit anchor; the master is rebuilt frequently. Easy fix.

- **Side observation (not a CONVENTIONS fire)** — Line 229 reads "Cohen's d = +0.90 (CI95 [+1.51, +8.22])". The CI95 does not bracket the point estimate +0.90. Either the d value is wrong, the CI is for a different statistic (e.g. the underlying mean stress-unit difference), or the labels are swapped. Worth verifying against [`../methodology/hrv_proxy_via_stress.md`](../methodology/hrv_proxy_via_stress.md) §7 before this number propagates into B-block pre-reg power planning, where line 249 cites it as the effect-size anchor.

## 3. What does not fire (selective)

- **L3.1 (autocorrelation) passes with documented machinery** — block-permutation null with the methodology MD cited by name. The Natesan Batley 2023 systematic-review failure-mode bar is cleared.
- **L3.3 (multiple testing) passes with a Holm step-down correction on N_eff ≈ 4** documented in a referenced methodology MD. The tiering itself (Tier 1 = primary pre-regs; rest = exploratory) is the right shape per SCRIBE Item 18 reasoning.
- **L4.8 (prior-driven vs post-hoc) passes** — the doc explicitly separates Wiggers-sourced hypotheses (need source verification + descriptive evidence before pre-reg) from personally-derived priors (parallel register `personal_hypotheses.md`, "different validation discipline — descriptive characterisation, no held-out required, forward-collected data as natural validation", line 142). This is the right reading of the new feedback memory `feedback_data_peeking_needs_prior_check` and the right architectural split.
- **L4.7 (caveat-class framing kept, a-priori framing cut)** passes — the doc uses honest caveats throughout: "no literal HRV claim", "Wiggers UI anchors do not translate", "this test is on the `stress_mean_sleep` proxy, not on HRV proper". Caveats are clearly distinguishable from analytical decisions.
- **Source verification log** is exemplary work — each Tier 1/2 hypothesis is grounded in verbatim PDF-line quotes with explicit DROPPED / KEPT / EXPANDED annotations, and the H1 framing carefully softens the doc's claim relative to Wiggers' explicit predictive claim ("Wiggers-faithful proxy channel" vs "Wiggers-extension channels"). This makes the doc auditable independent of any project-internal context.

## 4. What would strengthen this finding

1. **Resolve the `_lagged` vs `_lagged_lcera` table inconsistency** (addresses L4.2). Add `_lcera` suffix to the Column-choice table's PEM rows (lines 21-26), or add a "PEM frame → `_lcera`; cross-era trajectory → no suffix" qualifier under the table heading. Effect: removes the trap for a reader new to the variant family who consults only the upper table. The fix is self-contained; the pre-reg-draft section already uses `_lagged_lcera` correctly.

2. **Surface the tactical-vs-analytical Garmin-use distinction** in the held-out-structure paragraph (lines 250, 351-357) per memory `project_garmin_research_bias_boundary`. Concrete suggestion: replace the current bullet with something like *"Optional M3 descriptive overlay (train-era vs validate-era discrimination) may be reported but cannot claim per-portion verdicts. Train-vs-validate divergence is a number, not a narrative — the user used Garmin tactically throughout the pre-2026 validate era (real-time pacing, body signals, ergotherapy principles) but did not analyse aggregated cross-day patterns until the 2026 GDPR dump, so pre-dump validate eras are held-out in the strict research sense even though they were lived through and tactically Garmin-paced; cf. [`../methodology/train_validate_split_fate.md`](../methodology/train_validate_split_fate.md) §5."* Effect: the doc becomes self-defending against the regression the user explicitly flagged in `train_validate_split_fate.md` §4.4. Also flag the parent doc's lines 92-93 for the same correction.

3. **Move crash-drop sensitivity from G3-only to a cross-cutting pre-reg constraint** (addresses L4.4). Add it to the B-block constraints list at lines 246-253 / 344-367 as a 7th item — applies identically to all Layer 4+ tests across A/B/C/D/H. Effect: A1's Jonckheere-Terpstra dose-response and H5's CCF lag-profile both get the §3.4 sensitivity-pair row by construction, not by per-pre-reg memory.

4. **Pick one column per ρ=1.000 pair at the column-choice-table layer** (addresses L4.3). Replace the A4 row's "`hr_min_above_daytime_baseline_plus_20_waking` (minutes), `hr_area_above_daytime_baseline_waking` (bpm⋅min)" with the chosen primary (the pre-reg-draft already picks `hr_area_above_daytime_baseline_waking_lcera`) and a note that the count variant is dropped per CONVENTIONS §3.3.

5. **Verify the d=+0.90 / CI95 [+1.51, +8.22] reading** before B-block pre-regs ship — the CI doesn't bracket the point estimate. Likely the CI is for the underlying mean stress-unit difference; if so, label it as such ("mean stress-unit difference 4.87 [+1.51, +8.22]; Cohen's d = +0.90"). Effect: B1-B5 power planning anchors on a correctly-labeled effect size.

6. **Make Daza 2018 counterfactual framing explicit** (addresses L2.1). One sentence under "How to read this" stating that *"all tests below are within-subject counterfactual comparisons against the participant's own rolling baseline, not against a population; the framework is Daza 2018 (Methods of Information in Medicine)"* would make the doc defensible to an outside reader (e.g. Wiggers in a response cycle) without referencing project-internal context.

## 5. Verdict

**REVISION RECOMMENDED** — Layer 4 substantive fires on the upper Column-choice table (`_lagged` instead of `_lagged_lcera` for PEM rows, both ρ=1.000 pair members listed for A4) create an internal contradiction with the doc's own pre-reg-draft layer; combined with the Layer 2 inheritance of the now-superseded held-out-structure framing from `train_validate_split_fate.md`, the doc's framing layer needs a self-contained tightening pass before the Tier 1 pre-regs (B1, C3, A1, C4) ship to `analyses/hypotheses/`.
