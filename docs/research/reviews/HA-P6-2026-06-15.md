## 1. What the data shows

The doc is a pre-registration, not a result. The empirical claims it commits to are descriptive:

- **Primary (§1.1)**: for each of 7 named per-day channels, the LC-era per-day median + IQR trajectory across `[t+1, t+5]` after each crash_v2 episode-end will be characterised — depth, duration-to-baseline, and completeness — against TWO matched-baseline arms (Arm A: matched-deep-trough non-crash days as a strict RTM control; Arm B: lagged personal baseline per CONVENTIONS §3.2). The doc is explicit that this is Layer 1 descriptive characterisation per CONVENTIONS §2.1 and carries **no SUPPORTED / NOT-SUPPORTED bar** (§5 is "findings shape," not a falsification criterion; §9 enumerates downstream propagations per observation shape).
- **Secondary (§1.2)**: Spearman correlations with block-bootstrap 95% CIs between (recovery-rate, same-episode crash-duration) and (recovery-completeness, next-crash-interval). Explicitly correlational-descriptive, no SUPPORTED bar.
- **Tertiary (§1.3)**: identical shape characterisation on `[t+6, t+10]` as a late-recovery sensitivity arm.

The interpretive framing is held to mechanism-naming (Wiggers H5 channel-lag order; PEM recovery-debt; Citalopram dose-response calibration) without lifting those mechanisms to analytical basis (per §4.2 caveat-vs-a-priori).

n = 29 LC-era crash_v2 episodes (named with scheme + predicate + source file in §3, per §3.6).

## 2. What fired and why

### Layer 1 — Universal reporting (inherits from SCRIBE 2016, STROBE 2007)

**[L1.5 — limitations stated separately, substantive — power-calc dispatch absent from §8]**
The [hypothesis_lock_process MD §3.2 step 4](../methodology/hypothesis_lock_process.md#32-drafting-step-step-1-of-the-arc) is explicit: every pre-reg's §8 *must* include one of two power-calc dispatches — either the one-line "power calc inapplicable per Daza 2018 within-subject design — see Daza 2018 PDF for the within-subject counterfactual framing" OR an explicit calculation with assumptions named — and the absence of any §8 power-calc line is "itself a Layer 1 fire (silent power-calc invokes asymptotic-power assumptions that don't hold at n=1)." HA-P6's §8 covers RTM, n=29 sparsity, t0 definition, self-report instrument bias, dose-response calibration, channel coverage gaps, §3.7 binding, §3.4 explicit dispatch, Arm A operational-not-gold-standard, pre-crash-window scope, and mechanistic-claim scope — but contains no power-calc line. Per the lock-process MD §3.8, this is lock-blocking gate 1 (unmet). **Substantive** — closure is mechanical (one line; the descriptive-mode framing of HA-P6 strengthens the dispatch case rather than exempting it).

**[L1.3 — analysis method named with parameters, substantive — §4.8.3 qualitative shape classifier mostly unoperationalised]**
§4.8.3 commits to classifying each (channel × phase) median trajectory into one of six categories: `monotonic-recovery`, `stair-step-recovery`, `overshoot-then-settle`, `slow-grind-incomplete`, `no-meaningful-change`, `noisy-inconclusive`. Only `no-meaningful-change` has a pre-spec (the (a)/(b)/(c) ALL-of conjunct). The other five labels have no algorithmic definition. The §9 downstream propagations (e.g. the second-branch "Recovery shape matches matched-baseline... on the majority of (channel × phase) cells") cite "shape match" as a trigger, but without operational criteria for "match" vs distinct shape, future-result.md cannot reproducibly fire one branch over another. **Substantive** — the discipline of pre-registration is reproducible classification of the observed shape; §4.8.3's enumeration is half-pre-spec'd.

**[L1.3 — analysis method named with parameters, minor — §9 "statistically-distinguishable" trigger undefined]**
§9 first major branch: *"≥ 3 of 7 channels in the pooled LC × Arm-A × no-detrend cell show statistically-distinguishable median trajectory from matched control"* triggers the "distinct post-crash signature" propagation. The phrase "statistically-distinguishable" is not operationalised against the §4.8.1 block-bootstrap CI machinery. Plausible operationalisations (CI-non-overlap vs matched-control CI; CI excludes 0 on the median-difference; block-bootstrap p-value on the median-trajectory difference at any t+k below some α) all exist; the doc does not name which. **Minor** because §4.8.1 supplies the CI machinery; closure is a one-sentence binding to one of those operationalisations.

### Layer 3 — Time-series specific (inherits from Natesan Batley 2023, WWC 2022, CENT 2015)

**[L3.3 — multiple testing across lags/channels, minor — multi-cell secondary correlations without explicit Bonferroni or single-cell promotion lock]**
§4.8.4 specifies that the secondary correlations (recovery-rate ↔ crash-duration; recovery-completeness ↔ next-crash-interval) are reported "per channel × phase × matched-baseline-arm" — for 2 correlations × 7 channels × 4 phases × 2 baseline arms = up to 112 cells. §1.2 says "no SUPPORTED bar" and frames the secondary as descriptive correlational. However, §9's seventh and eighth bullets propagate downstream actions based on "OR (CI excludes 0) on recovery-rate ↔ crash-duration" and "OR on recovery-completeness ↔ next-crash-interval" — an implicit inferential read of the CI as a non-null trigger. With ≤ 112 cells and CI-excludes-0 as the trigger, ~5-6 cells would be expected to fire under a true null at α = 0.05; the §9 propagations therefore have a non-trivial false-positive risk despite the §1.2 "no SUPPORTED bar" framing. The single-cell-headline-lock pattern from the [hypothesis_lock_process MD §3.5/§4.2](../methodology/hypothesis_lock_process.md#42-layer-3-substantive--multi-comparison-discipline) (used by HA-C4b + HA-P7 r2) would close this: pre-specify ONE cell (e.g. pooled-LC × Arm-A × no-detrend × consolidation-phase) as the propagation-driving cell; other cells diagnostic only. **Minor** because §1.2's "no SUPPORTED bar" framing partially absorbs the concern, but the §9 propagations need a single-cell lock to be honest about which cell fires the downstream action.

## 4. What would strengthen this finding

Concrete and named:

1. **Add the §8 power-calc dispatch line.** Per [hypothesis_lock_process MD §3.2 step 4](../methodology/hypothesis_lock_process.md#32-drafting-step-step-1-of-the-arc) and §3.8 gate 1. Recommended wording (descriptive-mode-adapted): *"Power calc inapplicable per Daza 2018 within-subject design (see [Daza 2018 PDF](../../literature/methodology/daza_2018_self_tracked_n_of_1_counterfactual.pdf)); additionally, HA-P6 is Layer 1 descriptive characterisation per CONVENTIONS §2.1 with no SUPPORTED bar to power against. The block-bootstrap CIs per §4.8.1 are the inference machinery; their honest width at n=29 is the discipline."* Expected effect: closes lock-blocking gate 1; reframes the n=29 caveat from absent-power-acknowledgment to honest descriptive-CI framing.

2. **Operationalise the §4.8.3 qualitative shape classifications.** Each of `monotonic-recovery`, `stair-step-recovery`, `overshoot-then-settle`, `slow-grind-incomplete`, `noisy-inconclusive` needs a pre-spec analogous to the existing `no-meaningful-change` pre-spec. Suggested approach: define each as a conjunct over (a) per-day median signs / first-differences, (b) per-day block-bootstrap CI properties, (c) detrend-residual properties. Expected effect: makes the result.md classifier reproducible from doc-only knowledge; closes the L1.3 substantive fire on §4.8.3.

3. **Bind "statistically-distinguishable" in §9 to a §4.8.1 CI criterion.** One sentence at the head of §9 specifying e.g. *"throughout §9, 'statistically-distinguishable from matched control' = the per-day block-bootstrap 95% CI on the median difference (crash − matched) excludes 0 at ≥ N of 5 days in the primary window."* Expected effect: closes the L1.3 minor fire on the §9 first-branch trigger.

4. **Single-cell lock for the §9 secondary-correlation propagations.** Pre-specify ONE cell from §4.8.4's per-channel × per-phase × per-arm grid (e.g. pooled-LC × Arm-A) as the secondary-correlation-propagation-driving cell; all others reported but cannot fire §9 bullet 7/8 independently. This matches the [hypothesis_lock_process MD §4.2 closure (a)](../methodology/hypothesis_lock_process.md#42-layer-3-substantive--multi-comparison-discipline) used by HA-C4b + HA-P7 r2. Expected effect: closes the L3.3 minor fire and aligns HA-P6's secondary discipline with the sister pre-regs.

5. **Per-phase minimum-n gate on §9 third-branch propagation.** Add a one-line eligibility criterion: "the §9 third-branch (consolidation-only detrend failure) propagation fires only for phases with ≥ 5 episodes contributing to the per-phase trajectory." This protects against n=1-3 phases producing apparent "survival" of detrending that is actually just noise. Expected effect: closes the side observation on buildup + afbouw n's interacting with the §9 third-branch.

6. **(Lock-process gate 3, not yet due but worth noting.)** When this pre-reg locks, the [personal_hypotheses.md P6 register entry](../personal_hypotheses.md) needs a `→ superseded by: HA-P6 r<N> locked <date>` forward pointer per [hypothesis_lock_process MD §3.8](../methodology/hypothesis_lock_process.md#38-lock-step-stage-5-of-the-arc). The P6 register entry contains the lived-experience anchor + the prior-source citations that motivated the formal pre-reg; the formal pre-reg substantively narrows the channel set + operationalisation. Inline-pointer pattern (a) is preferred. Expected effect: gate 3 satisfied at lock; sibling P-family pre-regs avoid the staleness pattern HA-P7's r2 left in personal_hypotheses.md P7.
