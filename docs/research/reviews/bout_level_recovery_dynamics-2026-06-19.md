## 1. What the MDs propose

**Parent** locks an *operand definition* for per-bout recovery dynamics on the per-minute Garmin stress trace: a bout is the interval from a `stress ≥ 60` onset (with a `stress < 50` for 10-of-15 pre-window hysteresis floor) through a peak (max in a 120-min window, ≥ 5 min after onset, with a 60-min minimum separation between bouts) to an end defined as either return to within +5 of the local pre-bout baseline for 10 consecutive minutes, or a 180-min forward cap (`did_not_return_flag`). Per-bout features are `peak_height`, `pre_bout_baseline`, `peak_minus_baseline`, `recovery_half_life` (capped at 180 with `did_not_half_recover_flag`), `tail_length`, `decay_slope`, `AUC_above_baseline`, plus seven flag fields. Six per-day aggregations are derived (`bout_count_day`, two distribution descriptors, `bout_AUC_above_baseline_sum_day`, `bout_n_did_not_return_day`, and the load-bearing `bout_n_fast_recovery_day = count{recovery_half_life ≤ 15 AND tail_length ≤ 45}`). Cross-phase scope is in: Approach A (bout-level dose-adjusted predictor mirroring `citalopram_phase_stratification §5.B`) is headline; B (mixed-effects with dose covariate) and C (per-phase stratify + meta-analyse) are sensitivity. A framework-validity gate (§6) requires any downstream HA pre-reg using this operand to first show `bout_n_fast_recovery_day` reproduces HA11 v1's `+22.8 pp` train discrimination, restricted to the unmedicated × train × calm-day pool, at three bars (direction agrees + magnitude `≥ +12.8 pp` + p < 0.05 under block-permutation null at day-level E[L]=7); failure HALTs the downstream substantive cascade. Lock scope is narrow: C4 + HA11 only; A4 / C1 / C4b / H4 are named as enabled-on-request candidates with register forward-pointers landed at `9ddfafb`.

**Sub-MD** locks the per-bout β recalibration: rather than assume linear extension of the daily-aggregate v3 β coefficients (+0.43, +0.57, −1.13 / mg for `stress_mean_sleep`, `all_day_stress_avg`, `bb_lowest`), the same three-pronged spec (afbouw 2026 primary, buildup post-CPAP 2024 secondary, spring 2025 control) is re-fit at bout-level for each of the five primary per-bout features and the two per-day bout aggregations. Bout-level fits use a linear mixed-effects model with day-level random intercept + cluster-robust SE at the day level; per-day aggregations use Newey-West HAC at day-level mirroring the parent dose-response MD. Headline inheritance β for `bout_level_recovery_dynamics.md §5.3 Approach A` is the `buildup_post_cpap_2024 × primary` row (the §6 inheritance table is the empty schema at lock; populates at result-time as r2). Per-feature verdict bands inherit `citalopram_phase_stratification §4`: CONFIRMED features get Approach A dose-adjustment; REJECTED features remain dose-naive. A framed "level vs dynamics" substantive sub-finding is reserved for the result.md when the calibration runs.

Both MDs are co-locked per the cascade discipline: sub-MD's β estimates feed parent §5.3 Approach A; parent's bout-detection rule is the operand the sub-MD's features are computed on. Neither MD pre-authors any downstream HA pre-reg content. The descriptive-vs-interpretive boundary is held cleanly: per-bout features are operational descriptions of the per-minute trace, not mechanistic claims about autonomic state. Both MDs are NOT LOCKED at r1; status is "awaiting fresh-session audit per CONVENTIONS §2.2" — i.e. this report.
