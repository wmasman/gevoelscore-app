## 1. What the data shows

The target is a **v1 pre-registration** (status: drafted, not locked) for HA-C3, a Tier 1 Wiggers source-verified-verbatim test: that the within-subject same-day mapping of `all_day_stress_avg` onto `gevoelscore` on the unmedicated phase (2022-04-04 → 2024-04-08) is monotone-decreasing AND convex (accelerating decrement as stress rises). The single headline cell is **unmedicated × 5-bin (0-20, 20-30, 30-40, 40-60, 60+) × `gevoelscore` bin-mean × {Jonckheere-Terpstra monotonicity (a) + second-difference contrast (b) + natural-cubic-spline non-linearity (c)} × block-permutation null at E[L]=7 × 3-condition gated verdict (SUPPORTED iff all 3 MET; PARTIAL iff 2-of-3 MET and no wrong-direction firing; REJECTED otherwise or on any wrong-direction firing).** The dose-modulated `all_day_stress_avg` channel adopts **§5.A per-phase stratification (unmedicated headline) as primary** per [`citalopram_phase_stratification.md`](../methodology/citalopram_phase_stratification.md) with **§5.B dose-adjusted-predictor cross-phase as a sensitivity arm** plus within-consolidation §5.A replication, train+validate M3 overlay, crash-drop sensitivity, and t+1 lagged variant all reported as descriptive sensitivity only (cannot promote to SUPPORTED).

### Layer 3 — Time-series specific (inherits from Natesan Batley 2023, WWC 2022, CENT 2015)

**[L3.3 — multi-comparison discipline at the within-condition level, [CONVENTIONS §2.1](../CONVENTIONS.md#21-descriptive-before-inference) + [`hypothesis_lock_process §4.2`](../methodology/hypothesis_lock_process.md#42-layer-3-substantive--multi-comparison-discipline)] — minor.** §5.0 single-cell headline lock is satisfied at the **across-arm** level (one headline cell, all other arms descriptive only), which is the load-bearing discipline. However, **within the headline cell the verdict bar is a 3-condition gate** (Jonckheere + second-difference + spline) with three independent p-value tests at α = 0.05 each. The gate is conjunctive (SUPPORTED requires all 3 MET), which **lowers** the false-positive rate at SUPPORTED — but the **PARTIAL band (2-of-3 MET)** has no multiplicity correction across the three p-values. Under H0 (no convex relationship + no monotonicity), the chance of any 2 of 3 p < 0.05 simultaneously is ≈ 3 · 0.05² · 0.95 ≈ 0.7% if the tests were independent, but the three tests (rank-trend / discrete-second-derivative / continuous-second-derivative) share substantial structural dependence (they all read the same bin-means + spline-on-the-same-data). The effective false-PARTIAL rate is higher than 0.7% but neither explicitly bounded nor reported. Magnitude: minor — PARTIAL is descriptively informative and explicitly disclaimed from SUPPORTED-bar weight in §9.2, so this does not leak into the headline verdict. Closure path: §5.1 could add one sentence acknowledging that the PARTIAL band's nominal α is not Bonferroni-corrected (or explicitly choose not to correct because the three tests are construct-redundant), and §9.2 could note that PARTIAL is a descriptive band rather than an inferential-band claim. Inherits from CONVENTIONS §2.1 pre-specification of analysis method with parameters.

**[L3.4 — permutation null target for the spline condition (c), [`permutation_null_block_length.md`](../methodology/permutation_null_block_length.md)] — substantive.** §4.5.1 (b) explicitly cites the block-permutation null at E[L]=7 for the second-difference contrast. §4.5.1 (a) inherits the same null via §4.7 (Jonckheere-Terpstra under block-permutation). But §4.5.1 (c) — the spline non-linearity test — specifies an **F-test on the difference in residual sum of squares** between linear-only and full-spline models. The F-test assumes i.i.d. residuals, which the day-level autocorrelated `gevoelscore` series violates. The §4.7 block-permutation machinery is set up to handle (a) and (b) but is silent on whether (c)'s F-test p-value is computed via the F-distribution (parametric, i.i.d.-assuming) or via block-permutation. If parametric F is used, condition (c)'s p-value is anti-conservative and the 3-condition gate's "all 3 MET" requirement still inherits a too-easily-met condition. Magnitude: substantive — half the gate's PARTIAL/SUPPORTED logic depends on (c) being well-calibrated, and the anti-conservative parametric F is exactly the L3.1 failure mode Natesan Batley 2023 found in 83.8% of n-of-1 medical studies. Closure path: §4.5.1 (c) should explicitly state the F-test p-value is computed via block-permutation at E[L]=7 (compute the F-statistic on the observed bin-label sequence, then on each of B=10,000 null draws under the same §4.7 stationary-bootstrap label resampling, and report the empirical-null one-sided p). Inherits from [`permutation_null_block_length.md`](../methodology/permutation_null_block_length.md) — the canonical block-aware-inference policy already mandates this for ALL inferential machinery, not just (a) and (b).

### Side observations

- **Side** — §4.5.1 condition (c) Holm-correction language is absent. The pairwise Mann-Whitney secondary in §4.5.2 cites Holm step-down across 4 adjacent-bin comparisons (B1↔B2, B2↔B3, B3↔B4, B4↔B5) which is correct. But the three primary conditions (a)/(b)/(c) are **not** Holm-corrected. Per the L3.3 minor above, this may be intentional (the conjunctive 3-of-3 gate is the multiplicity control) but the pre-reg should say so explicitly. One-sentence addition to §5.1 closes both the L3.3 minor and this side observation.

- **Side** — §4.5.1 (c) says "visual inspection of the spline confirms convexity" — the **drafter-flagged open question 1** asks if the ≥3-of-5 bin-midpoints negative-second-derivative operationalisation is the right test. Two observations: (i) "negative second derivative" is *correct* for convex-down with negative slope (the monotone-decreasing-and-convex shape the C3 claim implies); the algebra checks. (ii) The 5 bin midpoints are at stress values (10, 25, 35, 50, 80) — but the spline knots are at the bin **boundaries** (20, 30, 40, 60), so the bin midpoints are intra-segment, not knot-adjacent. The second derivative of a natural cubic spline is **linear** within each segment between knots, so the sign at the midpoint is the same as the sign at the segment center; checking 5 midpoints is reasonable but B1's midpoint at 10 (in the segment from −∞ to 20, where the natural-cubic boundary condition forces zero second derivative at the leftmost point) may behave oddly — the natural-cubic-spline second-derivative at x ≤ 20 is **constrained to zero at x = 20 minus**, so the segment-interior second derivative is near-zero by construction. Recommend midpoints at (25, 35, 50, 80) and pass-condition ≥ 2-of-4, OR keep the (10, 25, 35, 50, 80) anchors but spell out that B1's expected behavior is "near-zero second derivative, neither sign carries weight". This is a one-sentence pre-lock fix that prevents a foreseeable result.md interpretation ambiguity.

- **Side** — §4.5.1 (b) E[L]\* derivation target is *residuals from the binned-mean fit* (a 5-parameter fit on n ≈ 555-580); §4.7 says E[L]\* is computed from *residuals from the linear `gevoelscore ~ all_day_stress_avg` fit* (a 2-parameter fit). These are different residual series and will produce different E[L]\* estimates. Recommend one explicit derivation target before lock; the linear-residuals choice in §4.7 is the cleaner default since it avoids circularity with the binned predictor (the E[L]\* check is supposed to be independent of the test machinery).

- **Side** — §4.4 secondary "within-consolidation replication" requires ≥ 30 observations in each of the 5 bins of the consolidation phase. The drafter's **open question 3** asks whether there should be a pre-commit on the n-per-bin threshold for reporting vs suppressing. The §4.4 text already gives the threshold (≥ 30 per bin → report; below → INCONCLUSIVE descriptive). What's missing is the **across-bin aggregation rule**: if 4 of 5 bins have ≥ 30 but B5 has 22 (the same pattern §7.5 gate 1 expects on unmedicated), is consolidation reportable on the 4-bin reduction or suppressed entirely? Recommend the same §7.3 halt-option-A/B choice (4-bin reduction is preferred) be applied recursively to the consolidation secondary; one sentence in §4.4 closes it.

- **Side** — §4.5.1 (b) co-permutation target is "the `(date, all_day_stress_avg)` label sequence" — the drafter's **open question 4** asks whether the E[L]\* should also be computed on the bin-label sequence as a second factor-of-2 check. Yes: the bin label is the actual permutation target, and its autocorrelation is what the resampling preserves/breaks. The linear-residual E[L]\* (current §4.7 default) measures autocorrelation in the continuous predictor; the bin-label E[L]\* measures it in the categorical version actually used in the test. Report both, flag if either crosses factor-of-2. Inherits from the methodology MD's expected-behavior for the data-driven companion.

- **Side** — §7.3 (the drafter's **open question 2**) defers the halt-option-A-vs-B choice to v2 time. This is acceptable for v1 but the §7.5 dry-run report needs to explicitly state which option the user must pick at v2 time; otherwise the v2 redraft is one round-trip slower. Recommend §7.3 add a one-sentence "v2 redraft will present halt-option-A (preferred) with explicit override to halt-option-B if user prefers" — pre-commits the v2 framing without pre-committing the decision.

- **Side** — §4.5.1 (b) sentence "the contrast vector `c = (+1, +2, 0, -2, -1)` (sums to 0; orthogonal to the linear contrast)" — the sum is +1+2+0−2−1 = **0** ✓. Orthogonality to the linear contrast `(-2, -1, 0, +1, +2)`: dot product = (+1)(−2) + (+2)(−1) + (0)(0) + (−2)(+1) + (−1)(+2) = −2 − 2 + 0 − 2 − 2 = **−8** ✗. The companion contrast is NOT orthogonal to the linear contrast. This does not affect the verdict (the companion is "for transparency, not verdict weight" per §4.5.1 (b) note and §4.5.2 third bullet), but the arithmetic claim is wrong. The standard orthogonal quadratic contrast for 5 evenly-spaced points is `(+2, -1, -2, -1, +2)`. Recommend dropping the orthogonality claim and either keeping the current contrast as a "concentration-on-extremes" measure (which is what `(+1, +2, 0, -2, -1)` actually measures — it weights B1+B2 positive vs B4+B5 negative with B3 ignored, picking up "low-stress higher than high-stress" rather than "convexity") OR replacing with the textbook orthogonal quadratic `(+2, -1, -2, -1, +2)`. Numerical inconsistency in cited mathematics; not a fire on the test machinery (the second-difference test is the primary, companion is reporting-only) but pre-lock cleanup is warranted.

## 4. What would strengthen this finding

- **Add to §4.5.1 (c): "F-test p-value computed via block-permutation at E[L]=7 per §4.7, NOT via the parametric F-distribution"** — closes the L3.4 substantive fire. Effect: prevents the spline-non-linearity p-value from being anti-conservative; brings all three conditions onto the same null-machinery foundation; addresses the Natesan Batley 2023 finding (83.8% of n-of-1 medical studies ignore autocorrelation) at the single point in the pre-reg where it currently leaks. Cite [`permutation_null_block_length.md`](../methodology/permutation_null_block_length.md) inline.

- **Add to §5.1 verdict bar: one sentence on the 3-condition gate's multiplicity discipline** — closes the L3.3 minor. Recommended wording: "The conjunctive 3-of-3 SUPPORTED gate provides effective multiplicity control at the headline; the PARTIAL band (2-of-3 MET) is a descriptive characterisation, not a Bonferroni-corrected inferential band, and the three tests (Jonckheere / second-difference / spline non-linearity) share substantial structural dependence on the same bin-means." Effect: pre-empts a downstream reader's question and clarifies that PARTIAL is informative descriptively but does not carry α-correction weight.

- **Fix §4.5.1 (b) orthogonality arithmetic** — closes the Side numerical-inconsistency. Either (i) drop "(orthogonal to the linear contrast)" and re-describe `c = (+1, +2, 0, -2, -1)` as a low-vs-high concentration measure, OR (ii) replace with the textbook orthogonal quadratic `c = (+2, -1, -2, -1, +2)` for 5 evenly-spaced points and re-cite as the standard quadratic contrast. Effect: removes a verifiably-wrong arithmetic claim from the pre-reg; the companion remains reporting-only so no verdict change.

- **Add to §4.5.1 (c) spline-midpoint pre-spec: drop B1's midpoint (10) from the ≥3-of-5 check OR explicitly disclaim its expected near-zero second derivative** — closes the open-question-1 operationalisation concern. The natural-cubic-spline boundary condition forces the second derivative to zero at the leftmost interior knot (x = 20), so the segment 0-20 has a structurally-near-zero second derivative that neither supports nor refutes convexity. Either change to "≥ 2 of 4 midpoints in segments {[20,30), [30,40), [40,60), [60,100]}" or keep all 5 with explicit disclosure. Effect: prevents a result.md interpretation ambiguity that is foreseeable now.

- **Specify the §4.7 E[L]\* derivation target consistently** — closes the Side §4.5.1 (b) vs §4.7 mismatch and the open-question-4 second-derivation suggestion. Recommend: report TWO E[L]\* values: (i) on the linear-residual series (current §4.7 default; measures continuous-predictor autocorrelation) and (ii) on the bin-label categorical series (measures the actual permutation target's autocorrelation). Both flag if `|E[L]* - 7| / 7 > 0.5`. The two reads complement each other and address the open-question-4 second-factor-of-2 check directly.

- **Pre-commit §7.3 halt-option-A as default with halt-option-B as user override** — closes open-question-2. The dry-run should report n per bin + state "If gate 1 fires, v2 redraft will adopt halt-option-A (B4 widened to [40, 100]; convexity test reduces to 4 bins with 2 second-differences) unless user explicitly invokes halt-option-B before v2." Effect: removes one round-trip from the v2 cycle if the gate fires; the pre-commitment is methodologically clean because B5 underpower is the *expected* fragility per §7.2's distribution forecast.

- **Add to §4.4 within-consolidation secondary: the across-bin aggregation rule** — closes the open-question-3 sub-question and the Side §4.4 observation. Recommend: "if 4 of 5 consolidation bins have ≥ 30 but B5 has < 30, report the consolidation replication on the 4-bin reduction (per the §7.3 halt-option-A pattern); below 4 bins with ≥ 30, suppress the consolidation secondary." Effect: pre-commits to the discipline at lock time rather than at result.md write time.
