## 1. What the MD specifies

A confirmatory methodology MD locking the analytical specification for a single narrow follow-up question descended from [`intervention_effects_descriptive.md`](../methodology/intervention_effects_descriptive.md) §8: does the participant's nighttime autonomic load (`stress_mean_sleep`) respond to the citalopram afbouw in a graded, dose-dependent way? The chosen design is a linear regression of `stress_mean_sleep` on a PK-smoothed plasma dose proxy (one-compartment exponential decay convolution with t_half = 35h) plus a linear `days_from_afbouw_start` covariate; primary inference uses Newey-West HAC standard errors with Andrews 1991 lag selection (`maxlags ≈ 4` at n≈70); four sensitivity columns (block bootstrap on 7-day-block residuals, prescribed-step dose representation, `_lagged_lcera` outcome variant, `maxlags = 7` HAC) hedge the primary against modelling-choice fragility; a 4-condition all-must-hold null pre-spec (§4.4) binds the decision rule against post-hoc reframing.

**Framing**: confirmatory per CONVENTIONS §4.3, justified by three independent priors (lived experience braindump, SSRI nocturnal-autonomic literature queued at QUEUED-WORK Tier 3, monotonic-receptor-occupancy mechanism). The MD explicitly does NOT pre-commit to script implementation details beyond §6.1-§6.3; the script run is a follow-up session.

**Downstream artefacts that would inherit this choice**: every PEM-pacing hypothesis using `stress_mean_sleep_lagged_lcera` as a baseline component across the 2026-03-20 → 2026-06-05 frame (P4a, P4b, P5b in the Personal register inherit the reference-frame discontinuity question per §5.1); the open question in [`garmin_pacing_practice.md`](../methodology/garmin_pacing_practice.md) §7.4 is partially resolved for one channel (§5.1); the parent MD §8.4 follow-up bullet upgrades from "step-change at one boundary" to "graded dose-response across the entire afbouw window" if the script confirms.

## 2. What fired and why

### Spine — §2.2 four-input bar (inherits from CONVENTIONS §2.2)

#### I1 — Best-practices standards

- **[I1.1, L3.5 — CONVENTIONS §2.2 input 1 — substantive]** — Pharmacokinetic / pharmacodynamic (PK/PD) modelling is the state-of-art framework for *time-varying drug-exposure regression in single subjects*, but the MD does not explicitly name PK/PD as the framework being adopted. §2.3 derives a one-compartment exponential-decay convolution from first principles (correct), but the reader is left to recognise this as PK/PD without anchor. The fire is small in substance (the math is right) and large in audit-trail: a downstream reader checking "is the exposure model defensible at the state-of-art" hits a gap. **Fix is one sentence**: name "one-compartment first-order PK absorption-elimination model" with a citation (Bies, Heersink, Pugh 2007 textbook; or the citalopram FDA label which specifies the same kinetics). Per the state-of-art pointer table for "Drug-effect transition windows (pharmacokinetics)".
- **[I1.4 — CONVENTIONS §2.2 input 1 — minor]** — §4.6 rejects ITS for this MD's question on the grounds that "ITS estimates level-change β2 + slope-change β3 at a *single* intervention boundary; this MD's question is the *graded shape across 3-4 dose steps*". The rejection-reason is question-type-based (single boundary vs. graded shape), not corpus-constraint-based. This is defensible but could be sharper: with 3 dose-step boundaries one COULD fit a multi-boundary ITS with 3 sets of β2/β3 — the MD's choice to regress directly on dose rather than on step-indicators is the substantive call. One sentence clarifying "we collapse the 3 step-boundaries into a continuous dose function because the mechanism is dose-graded, not step-discontinuous; a multi-boundary ITS would estimate 6 step-level parameters where mechanism predicts a single per-mg slope" would close I1.4 cleanly.

#### I2 — Established literature

- **[I2.2, I2.4 — CONVENTIONS §2.2 input 2 — substantive]** — Three concrete citations are missing where the §2.2 bar is explicit about material literature support:
  - **Bernal, Cummins, Gasparrini 2017 BMJ** (ITS guidance): the rejection in §4.6 is methodologically correct but uncited. Parent MD §1 cites this paper; the dose-response MD should too, in the same §4.6 location, with one sentence on what Bernal et al. materially contribute (the level-change β2 + trend-change β3 decomposition that the dose-regression framework subsumes when the exposure is continuous).
  - **Künsch 1989 (moving-block bootstrap) and/or Politis & Romano 1994 (stationary bootstrap)**: §4.3 Sensitivity Column A specifies 7-day-block residual bootstrap without naming the canonical method or citation. Per the state-of-art pointer table for "Block bootstrap / block length selection". Local PDFs are at [`literature/methodology/kunsch_1989_jackknife_bootstrap_stationary.pdf`](../literature/methodology/kunsch_1989_jackknife_bootstrap_stationary.pdf).
  - **Newey & West 1987 (Econometrica) and Andrews 1991 (Econometrica)**: §4.2 names "Newey-West HAC" and "Andrews 1991 rule" but neither is cited with the journal reference. The substantive content is correct; the citation form is incomplete.
- **[I2.3, A7.2 — CONVENTIONS §2.2 input 2 — substantive]** — The confirmatory framing in §1.4 rests on a literature prior (Marin 2010, Tantucci 2003, Licht 2010, Kemp 2010, Wichniak 2017), but the prior literature is **queued, not retrieved**. The MD says retrieval is not gating the script run — that's correct for the descriptive Layer 1 stance; it is **uncomfortable for a confirmatory MD locking inferential machinery**. The §4.3 rule allows confirmatory framing if the prior is independent, but a §2.2 reviewer can fairly push that "independent prior" means "named and citable", not "queued for future retrieval". Mitigation: the lived-experience prior + mechanism prior are independent of corpus and citable on their own; the literature prior is the *third* leg and the framing survives even if it's queued. But the fire is real and worth a one-line acknowledgment: "Confirmatory framing rests on lived-experience + mechanism priors; literature prior is queued (Tier 3) and would tighten but not change the framing."
- **[I2.4 — small-N effect-size literature — minor]** — §4.4 specifies effect-size gating in "0.05 × SD per mg" units, which is correct in spirit but doesn't anchor to the WWC 2022 SCED Handbook §V menu (Cohen's d / Hedges' g for small N; Tau-U; NAP; IRD). Per Layer 3 of the reviews framework, effect-size measures named in single-subject literature should be acknowledged even if the chosen metric is the raw regression coefficient. One sentence in §4.4 mapping the SD-normalised β to Cohen's d equivalent would close it.

#### I3 — Tradeoff vision

- **[I3.4 — CONVENTIONS §2.2 input 3 — minor]** — §4.1 specifies a one-sided test (H1: β_dose > 0) which is justified by the §1.4 confirmatory prior, but the one-sided vs. two-sided choice is not surfaced in §4.1 as a tradeoff with explicit weighting. The implicit logic is: one-sided gains power AND commits to direction (defensible because prior commits direction), but the two-sided alternative (loses ~half the power, preserves agnosticism about effect direction) should be named and rejected in one sentence. Without it, a reviewer can read the one-sided choice as motivated reasoning (analyst picked the side that gave significance). The §1.4 priors-justify-direction read is correct but needs to be applied explicitly in §4.1.
- **[I3.3 — CONVENTIONS §2.2 input 3 — minor]** — §4.1 four-input I3 for "linear in dose" says "with 4 dose levels the regression cannot reliably distinguish linear-in-dose from log-dose anyway". This is correct but partly understates the case: with 4 dose levels and ~70 observations, the regression CAN distinguish linear from log IF the log-dose shape is well-separated from linear; what it cannot do is *reject linear in favour of log* unless the log shape is dramatic. One clarifying sentence would tighten the read.

### Methodology-specific elements — Type A.7 (Intervention-effect MD)

- **A7.1 PASSES (cross-references I1.4 above)**: ITS named, deliberately rejected with reason. Citation gap noted in I2.2.
- **A7.2 SUBSTANTIVE (cross-references I2.3 above)**: PK literature (citalopram t_half source, SSRI nocturnal autonomic anchors) named but not cited.
- **[A7.3c — CONVENTIONS §2.2 input 4 + B3 confounder enumeration — substantive]** — **Seasonality is not enumerated as a confound**. The afbouw window 2026-03-20 → 2026-06-05 is mid-March → early-June: late winter through early summer. Multiple seasonal channels co-vary with calendar time across this 77-day stretch: daylight hours (~12h → ~16h, approximately linear), pollen onset (bursty, typically late-April for grasses), spring mood seasonality, ambient temperature. Because the dose function is monotonically declining across the same calendar window, seasonality and dose are partially collinear. The MD's linear `days_from_afbouw_start` covariate absorbs *monotonic linear* trends — it provides partial protection if seasonal influence on `stress_mean_sleep` is approximately linear across the window (likely true for daylight; less so for pollen). **Real risk**: a nonlinear seasonal effect leaks into residuals, residuals correlate with dose because both correlate with calendar time, β_dose biases. Fix: enumerate seasonality as a §1.3 confound, acknowledge the linear time covariate's partial coverage, and consider a nonlinear time term (cubic spline, or month-indicator) as a robustness sensitivity. This is the same general shape of confound the parent MD §1 acknowledges for the LC recovery trajectory; same treatment.
- **A7.3a, A7.3d, A7.3e — minor**: (a) Other concurrent interventions: the afbouw period overlaps with the "Breinvoeding-interventie" 2026-03-10 → 2026-08-31 (per parent MD §2 excluded interventions). One sentence in §1.3 acknowledging "Breinvoeding-interventie runs across the entire afbouw window and is not modelled separately" closes the audit. (d) Device-stability: FR245 throughout the afbouw period; one-line confirmation closes the hook. (e) Life events: §5.3 handles this as a diagnostic on counter-prior findings; adequate.

- **A7.7 PASSES**: Outcome (`gevoelscore` per parent §3b) is explicitly scoped out; the MD analyses the baseline channel only.

## 3. What does not fire (selective)

Non-trivial passes worth naming:

- **Confirmatory framing per §4.3 holds** despite the literature-prior being queued. The MD correctly identifies three independent priors (lived-experience, literature, mechanism) and the §4.3 rule explicitly requires only one. The mechanism-prior alone (serotonergic modulation of central autonomic regulation, monotonic receptor occupancy in 8-30mg range) is independent of corpus and sufficient. The lived-experience prior is independently cited via [`lived_experience_garmin_pacing_2026-06-14.md`](../lived_experience_garmin_pacing_2026-06-14.md). The literature-prior status doesn't collapse the framing.
- **§4.3 Sensitivity Column C (lagged-lcera outcome) engages the §3.2 caveat thoughtfully**. The "methodologically tricky point worth surfacing" paragraph in §4.3-C explicitly identifies that the lagged-lcera baseline window spans the afbouw and would *systematically attenuate* β_dose by construction. The interpretation rule — "if β_dose survives despite attenuation, finding strengthens; if it dies, read is ambiguous" — is the right asymmetric read for this constraint. This is the model treatment for a Layer 3 / Layer 4 audit-hook engagement: name the rule, name how the design tests against the rule, name how the test result is interpreted.
- **§4.4 4-condition null pre-spec mirrors parent MD §4 5-condition shape** and is the right asymmetric construction (multiple conditions must hold for null; any one failing → not null). The effect-size floor (|β_dose| < 0.05 SD per mg) is a meaningful guard against "significant but tiny" patterns and is pre-spec'd rather than post-hoc.
- **§2.4 buffered-dose rejection is corpus-aware**. The MD rejects the parent MD's transition-buffer pattern with the right reasoning: the transient between dose-step dates IS the signal in a dose-response design (excluding ±B days strips the regression of what it's designed to detect). The PK-smoothing handles the "not at steady-state on day-of-step" concern that the buffer pattern was designed for. The audit-trail of why buffer logic doesn't transfer is explicit; this closes a parent-pattern-transfer question that a reviewer would otherwise ask.
- **§4.5 8mg sub-phase asymmetry is acknowledged with proportionate weight**. The 9-day-at-new-dose constraint is named (correctly), the PK-smoothed exposure's natural down-weighting is named (correctly), the prescribed-step sensitivity's lack of attenuation is named (correctly). The §6.3 output requirement to surface "the 8mg sub-phase contributes minimally to β_dose either way" closes the disclosure.
- **§4.6 outright-disclosure-of-what-this-analysis-does-not-include** is the kind of structural transparency a §2.2-bar reviewer wants: four explicit non-inclusions (multiplicity correction, ITS, change-point detection, mediator analysis) each with reasoning. Closes most a-priori expectations the reader could bring.

## 5. Verdict
