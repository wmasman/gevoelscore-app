# Is the Stress U-Dip a POTS Marker, and Which POTS Can a Wrist Watch Actually See? — A PubMed Operationalisation and External-Validity Check

*Evidence retrieved from PubMed (DOIs linked throughout). Purpose: this brief interrogates the POTS side of the 2026-07-07 PEM-vs-POTS reframing. The project marks a "POTS-signature day" from the within-day Garmin **stress U-dip** count (`u_dip_count` z vs personal baseline) per [`methodology/pem_pots_mechanism_framing.md §3.1`](../../methodology/pem_pots_mechanism_framing.md) and the [separability analysis](../../analyses/descriptive/pem_pots_separability/findings.md). Three questions are asked against the external literature: (1) is that operationalisation one that others use, and is there a case for it; (2) which kinds of POTS can a wrist device of this class pick up; (3) what is the watch structurally blind to. The device is a Garmin Forerunner 245 (Elevate V3, wrist PPG); the stress score is the Firstbeat HRV-derived composite (GSS), and the corpus carries **no posture, no blood-pressure, and no standing-HR channel**.*

*Scope note: this is an n=1 instrument-test project. "POTS" below means the condition **as operationalised in the clinical literature** — a postural heart-rate response measured against a known change of posture — NOT a diagnosis of this participant. The load-bearing distinction kept throughout is **"what the watch measures" vs "what defines POTS clinically"**: the two are further apart than any single felt-state channel in this project. See [[findings-are-provisional]], [[site-is-medium-not-subject]]. Companions in this folder: [`wearable_validity_lc_mecfs_review.md`](wearable_validity_lc_mecfs_review.md) (R24, is the autonomic signal real in-population) and [`acute_viral_infection_autonomic_signature_review.md`](acute_viral_infection_autonomic_signature_review.md) (R23, acute-infection direction). This brief is the **orthostatic / POTS-specific** counterpart, distinct from both.*

## 1. Reader-facing synthesis (one paragraph)

Based on articles retrieved from PubMed, the honest reading splits three ways. **First, on whether the operationalisation is one others use: no.** POTS is defined everywhere in the clinical literature by a single observable — a **sustained orthostatic heart-rate rise of ≥30 bpm (≥40 bpm if under 20) within 10 minutes of standing or head-up tilt, without orthostatic hypotension** — i.e. a *postural delta* that requires knowing both supine and standing heart rate. The Forerunner 245 records no posture, so the project literally cannot compute the variable that defines the condition, and the marker it uses instead — a count of within-day *troughs* in the HRV-derived stress score — has **no precedent in the POTS literature** as a POTS or orthostatic marker. Worse for the label, its polarity runs the wrong way: a stress U-dip is a transient HRV *rise* (a vagal blip), whereas the established POTS autonomic signature is HRV *withdrawal* — reduced RMSSD/HF and raised LF/HF, at rest and especially on standing. **Second, on which POTS the watch can see:** of the three canonical subtypes (neuropathic, hyperadrenergic, hypovolemic) plus the deconditioning contributor, only the **hyperadrenergic-leaning tonic pattern** leaves a fingerprint a resting/overnight HRV-stress channel could plausibly detect — and even then non-specifically, indistinguishable from PEM load, anxiety, inappropriate sinus tachycardia, or deconditioning. The **hypovolemic** mechanism the U-dip is *named* for is a real, evidence-based subtype (≈13% blood-volume deficit, symptom relief from saline/salt), but the watch has no blood-volume channel — the "U-dip means blood volume" step is the participant-author's management heuristic, not an instrument reading. **Third, what it is blind to:** the defining orthostatic HR delta (no posture), blood pressure entirely, cerebral blood flow (which drops in ME/CFS-and-long-COVID orthostatic intolerance *even when HR and BP are normal*), orthostatic intolerance without tachycardia, and — a measurement irony — wrist PPG accuracy degrades exactly at the standing/moving transition where the orthostatic signal lives, and Firstbeat censors stress during motion. Net: the U-dip is a real, reproducible watch pattern honestly labelled a proxy, but as a *POTS* marker it is idiosyncratic, off-polarity, and blind to the condition's defining axis; its best defensible reading is "a within-day autonomic-variability event the participant has personally learned to manage," not "a POTS detector."

## 2. What "POTS" means operationally in the literature (a postural delta, not a resting state)

Every clinical operationalisation of POTS is a **change score across a posture transition**. This is the fixed point the project's marker has to be judged against.

- **The diagnostic criterion is an orthostatic HR increment.** POTS is defined by a heart-rate rise **≥30 bpm within 10 minutes of standing or head-up tilt in the absence of orthostatic hypotension**, standing HR often ≥120 bpm, with chronic (≥3–6 month) symptoms of orthostatic intolerance ([Low et al., 2009, *J Cardiovasc Electrophysiol*; DOI](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1540-8167.2008.01407.x); [Benarroch, 2012, *Mayo Clin Proc*; DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.mayocp.2012.08.013); [Rocha et al., 2021, *Arq Bras Cardiol*; DOI](https://doi.org/10.36660/abc.20200420)). In adolescents the threshold is **≥40 bpm**, established from active-standing normative data ([Zhao et al., 2015, *BMJ Open*; DOI](https://doi.org/10.1136/bmjopen-2014-007356)). Every one of these is a **supine→upright comparison**.
- **The diagnosis is made by tilt-table or active stand test** — including in the post-COVID POTS literature, where it is "easily diagnosed … measuring changes in orthostatic heart rate and blood pressure" ([Abbate et al., 2023, *J Cardiovasc Pharmacol*; DOI](https://doi.org/10.1097/FJC.0000000000001432)). The instrument that defines the condition is a *protocolised posture change*, not passive monitoring.
- **The HR criterion is itself imperfect even clinically.** Orthostatic HR magnitude **does not predict symptom burden** in chronic orthostatic intolerance, and a specific HR threshold is "neither predictive nor definitive" of who is symptomatic ([Boris et al., 2019, *Clin Auton Res*; DOI](https://doi.org/10.1007/s10286-019-00622-y)). This matters twice: it warns against reading any HR-derived marker as a felt-state proxy, and it is *consistent with* the project's own separability finding that POTS-signature days are not lower in gevoelscore.

The implication for the operationalisation is immediate: the marker the project can build (a resting/within-day HRV-stress feature with no posture) is, at best, a proxy for a *tonic autonomic state that sometimes co-travels with* POTS — not a proxy for the postural response that *is* POTS.

## 3. Is the U-dip operationalisation used by others? (No direct precedent; and the polarity runs backwards)

**Verdict: no external precedent found for a "within-day stress-trough count" as a POTS/orthostatic marker, and the U-dip's polarity is the opposite of the established POTS autonomic direction.**

**3a. How wearable POTS work is actually operationalised.** The consumer-device POTS literature — small and mostly 2024–2026 — does one of two things, neither of which is what the project does:

- **Posture-protocol capture.** A smartwatch single-lead ECG recorded *sitting then standing* with paced breathing tracked long-COVID POTS recovery via S-/R-wave amplitude ratio and respiratory sinus arrhythmia ([Kranck et al., 2025, *Eur Heart J Case Rep*; DOI](https://doi.org/10.1093/ehjcr/ytaf425)); a wearable heart-sound + ECG device separated pediatric POTS from controls by the *supine-to-upright* change in electromechanical activation time ([Zhang et al., 2025, *Pediatric Health Med Ther*; DOI](https://doi.org/10.2147/PHMT.S527129)); a smartwatch-HRV fall-risk tool inferred the *orthostatic* systolic-BP drop from a deliberate posture change ([Iakovakis et al., 2016, *Healthc Technol Lett*; DOI](https://doi.org/10.1049/htl.2016.0064)). All three keep the posture transition *in* the measurement.
- **Real-time HR pacing, patient-triggered.** Wearable POTS apps use *live heart rate* as a pacing ceiling for self-management, not a passive detector ([Finkelstein et al., 2025, *Stud Health Technol Inform*; DOI](https://doi.org/10.3233/SHTI250083); companion feasibility work [Gabriel et al., 2025, *AMIA Jt Summits Transl Sci Proc*, PMID 40502271, PMC12150731]). This is the same use-case as the participant's lived HR-pacing, and it is explicitly a *management* tool, not a *classification* of POTS.

Even the flagship trial treats wearables as unproven here: **RECOVER-AUTONOMIC** (the NIH long-COVID POTS platform trial) makes its primary endpoint an orthostatic-symptom questionnaire and relegates **wearable sensor data to an *exploratory* endpoint** ([Fudim et al., 2026, *Am Heart J*; DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ahj.2026.107384)). Conceptual "digital-twin"/mHealth POTS frameworks exist but are aspirational, not validated detectors ([Novak, 2025, *Front Neurol*; DOI](https://doi.org/10.3389/fneur.2025.1678955); [Warren et al., 2025, *Stud Health Technol Inform*; DOI](https://doi.org/10.3233/SHTI251192)). Across all of it, **no study uses a count of within-day stress/HRV troughs as a POTS or orthostatic marker.** The U-dip operationalisation is idiosyncratic to the Wiggers guide.

**3b. The polarity problem.** A stress U-dip is, by construction (GSS pre ≥40 → dip ≥25 points → higher plateau, per the HA11 spec), a **transient drop in the stress score**, and because GSS is an inverse-HRV composite, a stress drop is an **HRV *rise*** — a brief vagal/parasympathetic surge, co-occurring with a body-battery rise. But the established POTS autonomic signature is the *opposite*:

- At **rest, supine**, POTS shows **reduced RMSSD and HF power with raised LF/HF** — parasympathetic withdrawal, sympathetic predominance — and raised basal HR and LF/HF were themselves predictors of POTS ([Inbaraj et al., 2022, *J Basic Clin Physiol Pharmacol*, n=100 vs 160; DOI](https://doi.org/10.1515/jbcpp-2022-0069)).
- On **head-up tilt**, HF (vagal) power **attenuates *more* in POTS** than controls ([Orjatsalo et al., 2020, *Front Neurosci*; DOI](https://doi.org/10.3389/fnins.2020.00725)).
- Consistent with a vagal-deficit framing, **augmenting** vagal tone (transcutaneous vagus-nerve stimulation) *reduces* postural tachycardia and improves HRV ([Stavrakis et al., 2023, *JACC Clin Electrophysiol*, RCT; DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jacep.2023.10.015)).

So the canonical POTS direction is *HRV down / GSS up*, most sharply on standing. The U-dip's core event — *HRV up / GSS down* — is not that signature. This is exactly what HA11's own caveat already conceded: "the U-dip pattern is HRV-shape, not orthostatic-specific … the Wiggers interpretation is the most plausible reading but is not *demonstrated* to be orthostatic" ([HA11 result](../../analyses/hypotheses/HA11-stress-udip/result.md)). The literature strengthens that caveat into a substantive objection: the marker is not merely un-validated as orthostatic, it points the *other way* from the population POTS autonomic pattern.

## 4. Which POTS can the watch pick up, and which is it blind to? (subtype by subtype)

POTS is explicitly heterogeneous, with three overlapping mechanistic subtypes plus a deconditioning contributor; patients commonly show more than one ([Mar & Raj, 2019, *Annu Rev Med*; DOI](https://doi.org/10.1146/annurev-med-041818-011630); [Zhang et al., 2020, *Front Neurol*; DOI](https://doi.org/10.3389/fneur.2020.00525); [Benarroch, 2012; DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.mayocp.2012.08.013)). Mapping each onto what a wrist PPG + Firstbeat stress channel can see:

| POTS subtype / driver | Defining feature | What a FR245-class watch could see | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Hyperadrenergic** (excessive sympathetic drive; high standing norepinephrine) | Standing plasma NE ≥600 pg/mL, orthostatic hypertension, tremor/anxiety | A **tonic** low-HRV / high-GSS / raised-RHR fingerprint is plausible at daily/overnight resolution ([Inbaraj 2022; DOI](https://doi.org/10.1515/jbcpp-2022-0069)) | **Partially visible, non-specifically** — the resting sympathetic-predominance signal is the one channel that could carry it, but it is indistinguishable from PEM load, anxiety, IST, or deconditioning |
| **Neuropathic** (partial autonomic denervation; impaired lower-limb vasoconstriction / venous pooling) | Reduced distal sudomotor + vasoconstrictor response; defined by the *orthostatic* response | Nothing specific — the pathology is a postural vasoconstriction failure; needs posture + autonomic reflex testing | **Effectively blind** |
| **Hypovolemic** (reduced blood volume; low stroke volume → compensatory tachycardia) | ≈13% blood-volume deficit; cardiac atrophy / low SV; symptom relief from volume expansion ([Shibata/Fu/Levine 2012; DOI](https://doi.org/10.1113/jphysiol.2012.233858); [Figueroa 2014; DOI](https://doi.org/10.1152/japplphysiol.00367.2014)) | **No blood-volume channel exists.** The U-dip is *named* for this mechanism but the watch measures an HRV composite, not volume | **Mechanism-targeted but not measured** — the "U-dip → blood volume" inference is the participant's management heuristic, not an instrument reading |
| **Deconditioning** (near-universal contributor; low VO₂peak, cardiac atrophy) | Reduced stroke volume / exercise capacity; present in POTS *and* in orthostatic intolerance without tachycardia ([Parsaik 2013; DOI](https://doi.org/10.1007/s10286-013-0199-5)) | Only indirectly (elevated exercise/resting HR); confounded with fitness change over the 4-year corpus | **Blind as a mechanism** |

The single honest sentence: **the only POTS the watch can plausibly register is the hyperadrenergic-leaning tonic autonomic state, and only non-specifically.** The subtype the U-dip is *intended* to flag — hypovolemic — is real and management-relevant (volume/salt loading is first-line and symptom-reducing: [Ruzieh 2017, *J Interv Card Electrophysiol*, IV saline; DOI](https://doi.org/10.1007/s10840-017-0225-y); [Miller & Raj 2018, *Auton Neurosci*; DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.autneu.2018.04.008)), but it is precisely the subtype whose defining quantity the device cannot measure.

**A subtlety that cuts against the overnight channel specifically.** Where the project's cleanest HRV proxy lives (the sleep window, `stress_mean_sleep`), POTS may be *quietest*: in 24-hour ambulatory monitoring of POTS children, **asleep heart rate did not differ from controls**; the sympathetic excess showed up in asleep *blood pressure*, in awake/24-hour HR, and as a **morning surge** after waking ([Cai et al., 2020, *Auton Neurosci*; DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.autneu.2020.102715)). The orthostatic burden is a *daytime, upright, post-waking* phenomenon. An overnight-anchored autonomic channel is looking at the time of day POTS is least expressed — and the project's own R30 finding (resting HR is confounder-dominated, hence rejected as the POTS marker) is the in-corpus echo of the same problem.

## 5. What the watch is structurally blind to

Beyond the subtype map, five hard blind spots bound any POTS reading from this device:

1. **The orthostatic HR delta itself.** No posture channel on the FR245, so the supine→standing comparison that *defines* POTS cannot be formed. This is the irreducible gap.
2. **Blood pressure, entirely.** No cuff. The watch cannot separate POTS from orthostatic hypotension, cannot see the non-dipping nocturnal BP or orthostatic hypertension that characterise subsets ([Cai 2020; DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.autneu.2020.102715)), and cannot see the hyperadrenergic BP rise.
3. **Cerebral blood flow — and the normal-HR orthostatic intolerance it hides.** In ME/CFS and long COVID, **orthostatic cerebral hypoperfusion occurs frequently even when heart rate and blood pressure are normal**; HR and BP are only *proxies* for the abnormal CBF that is the shared pathophysiology of orthostatic intolerance ([Khan et al., 2025, *J Am Heart Assoc*; DOI](https://doi.org/10.1161/JAHA.124.036752)). A wrist device keyed to HR/HRV is blind to an entire, ME/CFS-overlapping class of orthostatic intolerance — the class most relevant to this participant.
4. **Orthostatic intolerance without tachycardia.** OI patients who lack the HR criterion are clinically similar to POTS but would never trip an HR/HRV-based flag ([Parsaik 2013; DOI](https://doi.org/10.1007/s10286-013-0199-5)).
5. **The measurement fails at the moment that matters, and the differential is unresolvable.** Wrist PPG agrees well with ECG *lying and sitting* but its discrepancy grows *standing and walking*, especially at short epochs ([Alfonso et al., 2022, *Sci Rep*; DOI](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-022-18356-9)) — i.e. accuracy is weakest exactly at the orthostatic transition — and Firstbeat *censors* the stress score during motion (gaps), removing the standing signal at source. Meanwhile the POTS differential explicitly includes **inappropriate sinus tachycardia, anxiety/somatic hypervigilance, and deconditioning** ([Benarroch 2012; DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.mayocp.2012.08.013)); a resting stress/HRV elevation cannot distinguish POTS from any of these.

## 6. The case *for* the operationalisation, kept honestly

The above is the case against reading the marker as a POTS detector. There is still a defensible, narrower case for the operationalisation *as the project actually frames it*, and it should be stated so the critique is not read as "drop it":

- **It is labelled a proxy, not a diagnosis, everywhere it appears** — and the methodology MD names the ideal-but-absent marker (positional/standing HR) as off-instrument ([`pem_pots_mechanism_framing.md §3.1/§6`](../../methodology/pem_pots_mechanism_framing.md)). This is the correct n=1 discipline; the proxy status travels.
- **It is grounded in a lived-experience prior, not data-peeking.** The U-dip is a real patient's personal electrolyte-timing trigger, so per [CONVENTIONS §4.3](../CONVENTIONS.md) its use is confirmatory of a pre-existing heuristic rather than a post-hoc invention.
- **The mechanism it gestures at is real.** Hypovolemia is one of the three established POTS subtypes, and volume/salt management is genuinely first-line and symptom-reducing (Ruzieh 2017; Miller & Raj 2018; Shibata/Fu/Levine 2012 above). The participant managing perceived blood-volume dips with electrolytes is doing something the evidence supports — the gap is only between *managing a felt state* and *claiming the watch measured it*.
- **Specificity-over-coverage is a reasonable choice for a *separability* claim** ([`pem_pots_mechanism_framing.md §3.1`](../../methodology/pem_pots_mechanism_framing.md)). For the narrow question "are two watch markers the same days or different days," a mechanism-faithful narrow marker is more honest than a broad autonomic composite that would blur the very axes being separated. The r≈0.09 separability result survives on those terms.

What the literature adds is a **ceiling on the language**: the marker can support "a distinct within-day autonomic-variability event that the participant associates with blood-volume management," but not "a POTS marker," "a POTS axis," or "the orthostatic signal." The MD's own softening ("POTS-proxy marker (one orthostatic pattern)") is the right direction; this review says the honest floor is one notch lower still — *one within-day HRV-variability pattern of unproven orthostatic identity and off-canonical polarity*.

## 7. Honest-strength verdict

| Sub-claim | External support | Strength |
|---|---|---|
| POTS is defined by an orthostatic HR delta (needs posture) | Universal in the literature | **Definitional** |
| The U-dip / within-day stress-trough count is used by others as a POTS marker | No precedent found | **None** |
| A transient HRV *rise* (stress U-dip) is the POTS autonomic signature | Contradicted — POTS is HRV *withdrawal*, esp. on standing | **Points the other way** |
| The watch can detect the **hyperadrenergic** tonic pattern | Plausible via resting low-HRV/high-GSS, but non-specific | **Weak / non-specific** |
| The watch can detect **neuropathic / hypovolemic / deconditioning** POTS as such | No channel for posture, blood volume, stroke volume | **Blind** |
| The hypovolemia mechanism the U-dip targets is real | Well-established subtype; salt/volume first-line | **Good (mechanism), but not measured by the device)** |
| The overnight channel is where POTS is most visible | Opposite — asleep HR ≈ normal; burden is daytime/post-waking | **Against** |

**Overall:** as a *separability* marker the U-dip is defensible and honestly caveated; as a *POTS* marker it is unprecedented, off-polarity, and blind to the condition's defining axis. The defensible published phrasing is "a within-day autonomic-variability event the participant manages as if orthostatic," not "a POTS signal."

## 8. Honest limits

- **PubMed-only, English-language, purposive (not exhaustive) search**, run 2026-07-07. The wearable-POTS field is small and moving fast; a directly-validating passive-detection study could appear.
- **No study validates the Garmin Firstbeat stress/GSS transform against an orthostatic ground truth** in any population; the polarity argument (§3b) rests on the *inverse-HRV* interpretation of GSS ([`hrv_proxy_via_stress.md`](../../methodology/hrv_proxy_via_stress.md)) plus the population HRV-in-POTS literature, which is one inferential step. If GSS behaved non-monotonically in this body, the argument would need re-checking.
- **Much of the pediatric POTS literature** (Zhao 2015; Cai 2020; Zhang 2020/2025; Orjatsalo 2020) uses child/adolescent cohorts; the autonomic *direction* generalises to adults but absolute thresholds and the asleep-HR finding are cohort-specific.
- **This review reads the project's operationalisation and the HA11/separability *characterisations*** to judge them; it does not re-run them and takes their reported numbers as given. It treats the external literature, not the corpus, as the standard.
- **"POTS" and "orthostatic intolerance" are not interchangeable.** Several blind-spot points (CBF, OI-without-tachycardia) are about the broader OI umbrella that ME/CFS-and-long-COVID patients often sit in; they bound what the watch misses, but they are not all strictly "POTS."
